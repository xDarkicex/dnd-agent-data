# Blood to Potions

**description**: 2nd-level transmutation Casting Time: 10 minutes Range: Touch Components: V, S, M (four glass vials) Duration: Instantaneous You drain blood from your target, magically transferring and transmuting it into a temporarily stable healing potion. When you cast this spell, choose one humanoid you can touch. The creature must be alive, or have been dead for no more than 1 hour. If the creature is alive, it can make a Constitution saving throw to resist the spell. On a failed saving throw (or if the creature does not resist), the creature loses 2d8 hit points and you create four healing potions that restore 2d8 + your spellcasting ability modifier hit points when consumed. The creature also gains a special Injury of dangerous severity, "Drained", which automatically recovers at the end of a long rest. While Injured in this way, the creature loses maximum hit points equal to the damage taken from this spell (a creature with 0 maximum hit points dies). This spell has no effect on a creature with this Injury. These potions lose their magic when you cast this spell again, or when you finish a long rest, whichever comes sooner. Once a creature has consumed one of these potions, it cannot benefit from another until it finishes a short rest. At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, the creature loses an additional 1d8 hit points and the potions restore an additional 1d8 hit points.
