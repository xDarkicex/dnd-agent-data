# Emergency Phylactery

**description**: Using the remaining life essence in a fallen ally, you can put a temporary stopper on their death, doing this causes the body to immediately die and their soul to move with their blood as you draw out and harden it in to the shape of a small glass bottle. This Blood glass appears to have wispy white glints flow within. Doing this, allows the caster to take the essence for later revival with an appropriate spell, or to allow the ally to attempt to possess another creature, with that of their fallen comrade. As an action, if you have a Phylactery with an ally’s soul, you can channel their life essence towards a creature within 100 ft of you that you can see, allowing them to attempt possession. creatures warded by a protection from evil and good or magic circle spell can't be possessed While the ally’s soul inhabits the phylactery, they are aware of their surroundings as if they were in the phylactery's space. They can't move or use reactions. The only action they can take is to project voice into the mind of the caster. The caster can also be heard by the ally inhabiting the phylactery. When the ally attempts to possess a creature, the target for possession must make a Charisma saving throw vs the casters spall save DC; as this is aided by the caster, they do so with disadvantage. On a failure, the allies soul moves into the target's body, and the target's soul becomes trapped in the phylactery. On a success, the target resists the efforts to possess it, and possession can’t be attempted again for 24 hours. Once the ally possesses a creature's body, they control it. Their game statistics are replaced by the statistics of the creature, though they retain their alignment and their Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma scores. They retain the benefit of their own class features. If the target has any class levels, you can't use any of its class features. Meanwhile, the possessed creature's soul can perceive from the container using its own senses, but it can't move or take actions at all. While possessing a body, the ally can use their action to return from the host body to the phylactery if it is within 100 feet of them, returning the host creature's soul to its body. If the host body dies while the ally is possessing it, the creatures soul dies, and the ally must make a Charisma saving throw against the casters spell save DC. On a success, the ally returns to the phylactery if it is within 100 feet of them. Otherwise, they die. If the container is destroyed then the ally dies. If another creature's soul is in the container when it is destroyed, that creature's soul returns to its body if the body is alive and within 100 feet. Otherwise, that creature dies. This spell can only be used on a creature that died no longer that one hour before casting. When the spell ends, the container is destroyed. This spell has no effect on creatures without blood.
