# Bloodthorn Bonds

**description**: 2nd-level necromancy Casting Time: 1 action Range: 60 feet Components: V, S, M (dried, thorny vines, grown from a creature's remains, which the spell consumes) Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute Gesturing, and pricking your finger on one of the thorns, you cause a creature in range to suffer intense pain as thorny vines sprout from their veins and arteries, binding them in place as they delve into the earth. One creature in range that you can see must make a Constitution saving throw or take 2d6 piercing damage and gain a special Injury of Dangerous II severity, "Bloodthorn Host", until the spell ends. While Injured in this way, a creature is restrained and takes 2d6 piercing damage at the start of each of its turns. As an action, it may attempt a Strength check against your spell save DC to break the bonds. Alternatively, any creature may attack the vines to break it free. Each vine has an AC of 15 and must be dealt at least 10 damage with a single action to be destroyed. The vines are vulnerable to fire damage, resistant to bludgeoning and piercing damage from non-magical weapons, and immune to psychic damage. Once three vines have been destroyed, the spell ends. At Higher Levels. Whenever you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the spell’s damage rolls deal an additional 1d6 damage for every two spell levels beyond 2nd. If the spell is cast using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, the vines must be dealt at least 20 damage in a single action to be broken.
