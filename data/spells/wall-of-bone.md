# Wall of Bone

**description**: 3rd-level conjuration Casting Time: 1 action Range: 120 feet Components: V, S, M (a handful of small bones) Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute Classes: Druid, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard You create a wall of razor-sharp, jagged teeth and bones. You can make the wall up to 50 feet long, 10 feet high, and 1 foot thick. You can shape the wall in any way you choose so long as it makes one continuous path along the ground. The wall lasts for the duration. When the wall appears, each creature within its area must make a Dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 3d8 piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Medium or larger creatures or objects can't pass through the wall. Arrows, bolts, and other ordinary projectiles launched at targets behind the wall are deflected and automatically miss. The wall is an object that can be damaged and thus breached. It has AC 10 and 5 hit points per 10-foot section, and it is vulnerable to bludgeoning damage. Reducing a 10-foot section of wall to 0 hit points destroys it and leaves behind a pile of bones and dust. At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the damage the wall deals when it appears increases by 1d8 for each slot level above 3rd.
