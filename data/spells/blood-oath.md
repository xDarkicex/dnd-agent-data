# Blood Oath

**description**: When you cast Blood Oath, you swear to achieve a difficult, task. Typical tasks are to protect someone/something, deliver justice/vengeance, or destroy an item, but others may be taken at the DM's discretion. Once you swear the Blood Oath, you become cursed and gain the following penalties: -1 to any skill check that is not in pursuit of the oath and take 3d10 damage every 12 hours if you make no attempt to satisfy the Blood Oath. You also gain the following bonuses while attempting to satisfy the Blood Oath: +1 to all skills (including passive skill checks) +10 to damage (not to hit) versus any creature that opposes your attempt to satisfy the Blood Oath, i.e., if you swear to kill Bargoth the Orc King, you get +10 damage against him and anyone working for him, but not against random enemies. If you are killed before satisfying your Blood Oath, and you are not brought back to life within 1 hour, you rise as an undead. A revenant, the physical manifestation of your soul born to undeath and sworn to fulfil your Blood Oath. As a revenant, you cannot be healed by spells other than blood mage spells and you cannot be returned to life as you were before undeath, except via the use of a wish spell The Longer you spend as a revenant, the more you begin to lose yourself. After 10 days, if your Blood Oath is not complete, you will lose your sanity, and self-control becoming enraged, and view all entities as enemy. The Revenant has the exact same stats as your normal self, but with a – 4 to strength (to a minimum of 1), and resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from non-magical weapons. This spell ends if someone other than the caster removes it using Remove Curse, Greater Restoration or Wish. This can only be done prior to becoming undead. If you complete the Blood Oath, the curse is lifted and the spell ends. If the blood mage completes its blood oath as a revenant, its soul is no longer bound by the curse, and passes on as death finally takes it.
