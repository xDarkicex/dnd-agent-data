# Mend the World Break the World

**description**: Mend the World / Break the World Requirements: Level 20 Casting Time: special Range: touch / special Duration: concentration / special Cost: special (minimum 2 WP) Just like a seamstress either mending a garment or ripping it apart, the philosopher can manipulate, repair or destroy the fragile connections between the worlds, especially those of fae and mortals, and the very fabric of the world. In the wrong hands, this power can cause untold destruction as it allows the direct manipulation of the forces of Possibility and Permanence. If one wanted to undo the millennia of work by the Faerie Queens, this is the way. Likewise, if one were to join forces with them, this is the power which could help them finish their work, especially if an entire group of philosophers were to act in unison.On a more practical scale, this power allows the philosopher to open or close (or even destroy) planar passageways and similar reality-defying constructs; recreate, manipulate or destroy the planar technology of the fae (or similar constructs) in general; change the ways the natural world behaves in small locales (the effects of gravity come to mind, for example); and whatever else the player and Game Master can agree on. This is an epic power that works best with poetic thinking, and a willingness to contribute to a satisfying, world-shaking endgame. Casting time and cost should be set by the GM according to the needs of the story, but as a rough guideline use the following: The GM sets the base time at 1 to 5 hours, depending on the difficulty, scale and intricacy of the task. The base cost is time × 2 Weave Points. The player then has the option to modify casting time, range and duration according to the table below: find the step in time, range or duration you want and note the extra cost. Repeat for each category you want to modify and add the costs.Basic range is touch, and basic duration is concentration For example , the philosopher wants to destroy a spatial connection between two fae-built cave systems located in different areas of the world. The GM sets the casting time at 3 hours, which makes the base cost 3 × 2 = 6 WP. Extra Weave Points Casting Time Range Duration 2 10 min. 30 ft. 1 min. 4 1 minute 300 ft. 1 h. 6 1 action sight permanent The player now wants to modify all three categories: casting time, range and duration. He is sorely pressed for time and wants to get it all done in 1 action. Looking at the table, he sees that this step costs 6 WP. As he still has lots of Weave Points left, he decides to step up the range to 30 feet just in case the effect in place was booby-trapped: another 2 WP are added to the cost. Finally, he decides the effort is only worth it if he destroys the passage for good; he makes the effect permanent for another 6 WP. Altogether he has now spent 6 (base) + 6 (time) + 2 (range) + 6 (duration) = 20 Weave Points. The philosopher takes a look at the arcane symbols on the cave walls, concentrates for a moment and annihilates the mystic portal with barely a thought.
