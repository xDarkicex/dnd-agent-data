# Reactionary Forced Coagulation

**description**: You target a creature within range that has just been wounded and attempt to gain control of the coagulation of their blood. If the creature is unwilling, they must make a successful Constitution saving throw vs your spell save DC to resist this spell. When this spell successfully affects a creature, you can cause one of the following effects to take place: You can staunch the bleeding of the creature whose blood you are currently controlling. Before they receive damage, you can roll a 3d6 and reduce the damage by that amount. This can only be used when the creature takes slashing, or piercing damage. You can control the flow of the target creatures’ blood to form a blood barrier around them that lasts until the start of that creatures next turn, until then, the creature gains a +5 bonus to AC, takes no damage from Blood Mage Spells, and only takes half Necrotic damage from attacks that deal it.

**properties**:
- Category: Spells
- Level: 3
- School: abjuration
- Components: V, S
- Casting Time: 1 Reaction which you take when a creature within range takes slashing or piercing damage
- data-RangeAoe: 30 feet.
