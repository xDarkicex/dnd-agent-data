# Haemorrhagic Cloud

**description**: You spill your own blood taking 1d8 Necrotic damage and use the magical essence within to create a 20-foot-radius sphere of red, wispy gas centred on a point within range. The cloud spreads around corners, and its area is heavily obscured. The cloud lingers in the air for the duration. Each creature that is completely within the cloud at the start of its turn, or enters the cloud on their turn, must make a constitution saving throw against your Spell save DC. On a failed save, the creature becomes incapacitated as it starts retching and reeling whilst lacerations form all over its skin causing the creature to bleed and take 2d8 slashing damage. A moderate wind (at least 10 miles per hour) disperses the cloud after 4 rounds. A strong wind (at least 20 miles per hour) disperses it after 1 round. Any creature with blood that is affected by this spell becomes blood soaked. When this spell ends, the cloud drops to the floor causing the area to become covered in blood. At Higher Levels: When you use a spell slot of 6th level or higher you can roll 1d8 whenever a creature fails a con save in the cloud and regain the result as hit points.
