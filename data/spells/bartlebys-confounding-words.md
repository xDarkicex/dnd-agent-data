# Bartleby’s Confounding Words

**description**: You put a magically imbued puzzle or riddle before a creature that can hear you within range. The creature must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failure, the creature has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks for the duration. At the end of each of its turns, the target can make another Intelligence saving throw. On a success, the spell ends on the target. A creature with an Intelligence score of 4 or less isn’t affected. At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can target on additional creature for each slot level above 2nd. The creatures must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.
