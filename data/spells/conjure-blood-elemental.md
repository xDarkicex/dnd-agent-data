# Conjure Blood Elemental

**description**: 5th-level conjuration Casting Time: 1 action, or 1 reaction after you gain an Injury of Dangerous II severity or higher Range: 60 feet Components: V, S, M (your own blood, drawn from a wound when you cast the spell) Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute As the blood flows from your body, you conjure a dark spirit and command it to fight. The blood pools in the air, expanding and taking on the form of a blood elemental. When you cast this spell, if you do not have an Injury, you can inflict an Injury of Setback I severity on yourself and lose hit points equal to half your Setback I Injury threshold. While Injured in this way, your maximum hit points are reduced by half your Setback I Injury threshold. If you do not, and do not have an Injury, the spell fails. You cannot cast this spell if you are a construct or an undead. When you finish casting the spell, you conjure a blood elemental (see page 111) in a space you can see within 60 feet of you. The blood elemental acts on its own initiative, but you control its actions. If you lose concentration on the spell, the blood elemental goes on a rampage, attacking the nearest living creature on each of its turns. In this case, the blood elemental does not disappear with the end of the spell, instead remaining until it is destroyed (though it may flee the battle).
