# Qualyth

**description**: Qualyths are lizard-like humanoids with a lithe and agile build imbued with an uncanny ability to quickly recover from injuries and adapt to any environment. Over the span of days or even hours, qualyths can take advantage of their unique epigenetic adaptability to evolve webbed appendages and gills for swimming, sticky pads for climbing, or the ability to change color to blend in with their environment. Qualyths usually live in close-knit communities. Leadership is usually meritocratic and every member of their society has a role. However, these roles are fluid and always adapting to circumstances; a clan's most adept hunter might lead the community during hunting season, while a more careful and considerate member governs during a harsh winter. As a reflection of their adaptability, qualyths have developed into two unique clades that excel in different environments: the larger type is adept in forested and tundra conditions, while a more diminutive variety thrives in deserts and aquatic regions. Qualyth Traits Creature Type: Humanoid Size: Medium (about 5–6 feet tall) or Small (about 3–4 feet tall), chosen when you select this species Speed: 30 feet. As a qualyth, you have these special traits: Darkvision. You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet. Damage Resistance. You have Resistance to Fire damage. Fast Healing. When you regain Hit Points, you can roll a d6 and add the result to the amount of Hit Points you regain. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest. Qualyth Adaptation. When you finish a Long Rest, you gain your choice of one of these benefits until you use this trait to choose a new benefit. Camouflage. You can change the color of your skin and you have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide. Spider Climb. You have a Climb Speed equal to your Speed and falling damage you take is halved. Waterborne. You have a Swim Speed equal to your Speed and you can breathe air and water.
