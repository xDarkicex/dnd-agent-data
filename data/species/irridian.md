# Irridian

**description**: Irridians are wiry humanoids with blue skin that have adapted to prolonged exposure to radiation. Resilient and pragmatic, irridians have a rich cultural tradition that celebrates adventure and community. While their nomadic lifestyle has instilled a sense of independence and self-sufficiency in most irridians, their societies also tend to place a high value on sharing – whether that entails experiences or wealth. Each tribe or clan has its own unique traditions, but they find common ground in their ability to thrive in places others would deem uninhabitable. Irridian Traits Creature Type: Humanoid Size: Medium (about 5–6 feet tall) Speed: 30 feet As an irridian, you have these special traits: Irradiated. You have Resistance to Poison and Radiant damage and Advantage on saves against the Poisoned and Radiation Sickness conditions. Channeled Radiation. You can use radiation to harm others or restore yourself in the following ways. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest. Radiant Purge. At the start of your turn, you can end the Paralyzed, Poisoned, or Stunned condition on yourself. If you do, you take 1d6 Radiant damage that can't be reduced in any way. Radioactive Pulse. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can replace one of your attacks with a radioactive pulse in a 15-foot Cone. Each creature in that area must succeed on a Constitution save (DC 8 + your Constitution modifier + your Proficiency Bonus) or take 1d8 Radiant damage and be Poisoned until the start of your next turn. The damage increases by 1d8 when you reach character levels 5 (2d8), 11 (3d8), and 17 (4d8).
