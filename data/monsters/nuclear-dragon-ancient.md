# Nuclear Dragon, Ancient

**description**: Nuclear dragons are horrific, luminescent beings, with scales that glow with an ominous, sickly light. Born from the chaotic energies of nuclear catastrophe, their moods are as unstable and unpredictable as the radioactive decay that fuels their fearsome abilities. Solitary Confinement The radiation that seeps from a nuclear dragon's body makes prolonged contact with other living beings nearly impossible, as it inflicts severe sickness and mutations on others – and eventually turns any place the dragon chooses to call home into a barren wasteland. Due to their dangerous nature, nuclear dragons are often shunned even by other dragons and are forced to lead a life of solitude – while harboring a deep resentment for the isolation imposed upon them by their own nature. A Nuclear Dragon's Lair Most nuclear dragons make their lairs in forsaken realms, such as the remains of obliterated cities, radioactive wastelands, or cursed battlegrounds. These locations are chosen for their inhospitality to other life forms and for being able to withstand or even thrive under the dragon's radioactive influence. Other nuclear dragons relish in the decay they spread and are drawn to vibrant ecosystems teeming with life. These dragons usually abandon their lairs in search of new regions to desecrate as soon as an area is thoroughly tainted and drained of life. Regional Effects The region surrounding a legendary nuclear dragon's lair is altered by radiation, creating one or more of the following effects within 6 miles of the lair: Dust Cover. The air is Lightly Obscured by dust. Radioactive Energy. When a creature finishes a Long Rest, it must succeed a DC 10 Constitution save or suffer Radiation Sickness . Sickened Wildlife. The first time each day a creature consumes food or water foraged in the area, it must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution save or be Poisoned for 24 hours. Acute Radiation Effects Several of the Nuclear Dragon's abilities can cause an Acute Radiation Effect . For ease-of-reference, the Acute Radiation Effects are repeated below (roll a d4 to determine the effect at random): Fever. Disadvantage on D20 Tests using Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution. Headache. Disadvantage on D20 Tests using Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. Nausea. The creature can only do one of the following on its turn: move, take an Action, or take a Bonus Action. Pain. The first time each turn the creature takes damage, it takes an extra 1d6 damage.
