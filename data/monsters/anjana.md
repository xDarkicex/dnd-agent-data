# Anjana

**description**: Anjana These fey can often be mistaken for a sprite. Standing about three feet in height, their forms are very elven in appearance. An anjana’s hair is always a shade of blond, and adorned with a crown woven of local flowers, usually dressed in a light-coloured tunic. She has two transparent wings and carries a small hazel stick and a small bottle containing a healing nectar. Their clothing always includes a belt of some kind to hold the two objects always in an anjana’s possession: her flask and her wand. An anjana’s flask can vary in appearance, from fine crystal to simple pottery, but always contains nectar gathered from local flowers and instilled with fey magic to provide healing when drunk. The wand acts as a focus for their magic and can deliver nasty jolts to hostile creatures. Fair Forest Folk . Anjana are often found in forests, and typically friendly to those that respect their homes. They often aid lost travelers to find their way. Those that despoil or threaten the forest home of an anjana will be suitably punished by any and all anjana that make their homes there. Anjanas often leave some of the silver from their crowns among the bushes where they roam, to make the happiness and fortune of the person who finds them. Their peculiarities vary according to the place, there are areas where it is said that they wear spikes of silver around their waists and on their heads, and bells that give off pearls when they ring. Protectors of Rivers . An anjana may make her lair near a river, claiming that waterway as her own and devoting herself to its protection. A river may have more than one anjana as its designated protector, and such anjana will certainly cooperate to mitigate any threat to the waterway. Legends say they dwell in small caves covered in gold near their chosen river, and from this gilded lair she helps those who get lost in her riverine forest and punishes those who damage it. Sweet Music . An anjana’s songs brighten the day if they are happy and darken it if they are sad. Her voice is soft and sweet and his mood is contagious to those who listen to it. They often watch over near herding societies, leaving their refuges when the cattle and sheep go up to the mountains, and returning in the autumn when they return to the valleys.
