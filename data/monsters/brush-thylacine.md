# Brush Thylacine

**description**: Thylacine Thylacines are well known dangers of the central Stolen Lands, rivaling wolves in some regions. These creatures are predatory marsupials that look vaguely like lean wolves at first glance—until they stretch open their jaws in almost unnatural displays of aggression. Brush Thylacine Brush thylacines are larger, rarer, and more muscular variants of their leaner kin, who prefer to dwell in the thick undergrowth of the central woodlands of the region. Their fur coloration tends to be darker than other members of their kind, as befits their tactic of hiding in the shadows cast by thick patches of underbrush. Traits Ambush Hunter. If the brush thylacine moves at least 10 feet before it makes an attack, the target of the attack cannot make an opportunity attack against the thylacine, and the thylacine can use its bonus action to take a Hide action. Actions Multiattack. The thylacine uses its hunting cry, then makes two bite attacks. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (2d4+4) piercing damage. Hunting Cry. The thylacine howls, causing distress to those around it. Any creature that is not a thylacine within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. Frightened creatures can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns, ending the effect on a success. A creature that succeeds on this saving throw is immune to this thylacine’s hunting cry for 24 hours.
