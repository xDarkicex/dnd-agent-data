# Malacoda

**description**: The malebranche (“evil claws”) are helper monsters who serve the fallen angels in this Hell circle. Dante only mentions them in the fifth bolgia, but we recommend using them in the entire region. These monsters are pitch black, approximately the height of humans and equipped with spiky tails, horns and bat-like wings, just as one would imagine a typical Hell-being. Their wings, however, are quite useless. Other than hovering a few feet above ground, they do not permit real flight. Because of their emblematic appearance, most mortals humans would identify them as demons or devils, but the group already knows that those are of another category and stand much higher in the hierarchy of Hell. As the malebranche are naked, it is easy to discern that there are male and female specimen who will openly engage in sexual activities near the wall of this Hell circle whenever their duties allow. The malebranche speak the Infernal language and do not usually venture out of Hell. They are amongst the foot soldiers who do not carry a hair of Lucifer and are therefore mortal. But because of their long life span (approximately 150 years), their enthusiastic procreation and their extremely short maturation (6 months to grow into adulthood), there will never be a shortage. The malebranche are as mean as they are aggressive and will make excellent foes with their long three-pronged pitchforks. Although they are loyal, these creatures are undisciplined and often start fighting amongst themselves. This is when the fallen angels will have to step in and sometimes even eliminate them. The malebranche are plagued by one other weakness and this is a fear of the true angels. This is why the Feather of Uriel will help to repel them if they ever threaten to become a danger. Actions Multiattack. Malacoda makes two fork attacks. Fork. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d10 + 3) piercing damage.
