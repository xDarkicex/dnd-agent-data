# Tartuccio

**description**: Tartuccio meets the PCs early on, but hides his true motives from them as best he can. His skill with illusions and trickery could make him an unusual leader of a criminal organization in a small town, or perhaps as a unique specialist working for an organization with greater reach. Traits Change Shape. By performing a 10-minute ritual, Tartuccio can polymorph into a Small humanoid with a unique appearance, or back into his true form. He can’t use this ability to disguise himself as a specific individual. His statistics are the same in each form, and he reverts to his true form if he is slain. He uses this ability to take on the appearance of a purple-scaled kobold named Tartuk. Special Equipment. Tartuccio has a wand of magic missiles . Spellcasting. Tartuccio is a 4th-level spellcaster. His spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 14, +6 to hit with spell attacks). Tartuccio has the following sorcerer spells prepared: Cantrips (at will): light, mage hand, minor illusion, true strike 1st level (4 slots): burning hands, mage armor, shield, silent image 2nd level (3 slots): invisibility, mirror image, scorching ray Actions Multiattack. Tartuccio makes two attacks with his sickle, or he uses his wand of magic missiles. Sickle. Melee Weapon Attack: +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 3 (1d6) slashing damage. Wand of Magic Missiles. Tartuccio expends a charge to activate his wand of magic missiles , launching three darts of force. Each dart hits a creature of Tartuccio’s choice that he can see within range, dealing 3 (1d4+1) force damage. The wand has 7 charges, and Tartuccio can expend extra charges, adding one dart per charge spent. The wand regains 1d6 + 1 expended charges daily at dawn. If the last charge is used, roll a d20. On a 1, the wand turns to ash and is destroyed. Reactions Invoke Old Sharptooth. When a Sootscale kobold harms Tartuccio while he is disguised as Tartuk, he demoralizes the kobold with a short warning such as “Old Sharptooth will punish you for your transgressions!” The kobold gains disadvantage on all checks until the end of its next turn.
