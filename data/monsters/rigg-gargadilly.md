# Rigg Gargadilly

**description**: Rigg Gargadilly is a high-strung murderer. While he’s encountered in a remote, forgotten ruin in Kingmaker, he’d work well as a supernatural foil against a group of urban-based PCs, for Rigg’s talents and predilections would make him a particularly frightening and effective killer. Transplanting him from a remote woodland ruin into the heart of the city gives him an entirely new theme and role in the game without having to change any of his statistics at all. Traits Innate Spellcasting . Rigg’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 12). Rigg can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: 1/day each: dancing lights, levitate, minor illusion, shatter Invisibility. When not moving, Rigg is invisible. After an attack, Rigg remains visible until the start of his next turn. Any equipment Rigg wears or carries is invisible with him. Magic Resistance. Rigg has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Sneak Attack (1/Turn). Rigg deals an extra 17 (5d6) damage when he hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of Rigg that isn’t incapacitated and Rigg doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Supernatural Speed. Rigg’s enemies have disadvantage on attacks against him when he moves. Actions Multiattack. Rigg makes three shortsword attacks. Shortsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d6+5) piercing damage.
