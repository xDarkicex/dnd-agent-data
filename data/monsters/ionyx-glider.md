# Ionyx Glider

**description**: Ionyxes are fearsome predators fine-tuned to the gravitational conditions of their environment. Thought to once have been domesticated and purposefully spread to a multitude of different worlds, ionyxes always appear as feline predators with prominent, tentacle-like whiskers – but their size, mass, and hunting techniques are as different as the distinct places they've come to inhabit. Ionyx Glider In areas with low gravity, ionyxes have evolved elongated limbs and claws that support high leaps and swift climbing, as well as leathery flaps of skin that allow them to glide through the air. Utilizing the light atmosphere, their whisker-like appendages have evolved into bioelectric weapons that let the predator stun prey with jolts of electricity. Ionyx Stalker In high gravity environments, ionyxes evolve to be smaller and more muscular, relying on a combination of stealth and strength. They can change their skin color to blend into their surroundings, and their whiskers allow them to manipulate sound – providing the predator with a tremorsense, the ability to emit staggering sonic waves, and mute sound by producing inverted sound waves of equal amplitude. Note: Evolved for different gravities The ionyxes here are presented as their statistics would be in an environment with normal gravity. If encountered in their natural environment – Low Gravity for the ionyx glider and High Gravity for the ionyx stalker – they would be affected as any other creature would ( Gravity for more information on how gravity affects creatures).
