# Belleros

**description**: These magnificent winged horses are renowned for their courage, grace, and nobility. Though highly prized as aerial steeds, pegasi are wild and shy creatures not easily befriended. A typical pegasus stands 6 feet high at the shoulder, weighs 1,500 pounds, and has a wingspan of 20 feet. Most pegasi are white, though occasionally one is hatched with conventional horse colors and markings. Pegasi lay eggs, and these eggs (or young pegasus hatchlings) are quite valuable on the open market, but as they are intelligent, good-aligned creatures, selling eggs and young is essentially slavery and is looked down on or outlawed in good societies. Training. The proper method to convince a pegasus to serve as a mount is to befriend it with diplomacy, favors, and good deeds. A pegasus has an intuition about alignment and is generally indifferent to a good-aligned creature, unfriendly to a neutral one, and hostile to an evil one. Before a pegasus will consent to serve as a mount, it must be befriended with Charisma (Persuasion) checks or other means. Riding a pegasus requires an exotic saddle especially designed for it or no saddle at all, as a regular saddle’s straps interfere with the creature’s wings. Pegasi mature at the same rate as horses, and once properly trained they are unafraid of combat and will serve a good or neutral master with absolute faithfulness for life. Belleros. Belleros pegasi share the proud bearing of their ordinary pegasus cousins but are even more devoted to the cause of heroism than their kin. Each belleros fancies itself a great hero, and many ardently seek out companions in adventure and try to encourage them towards acts of greater heroism. While unable to speak, the noble belleros is as intelligent as a human. As such, those who try to train a pegasus to serve as a mount find the pegasus to be recalcitrant and even violent if they are mistreated in any way, but they are eager companions for any with the spirit to keep up with them. Olympian Pegasi. In some pegasi, the blood of a mighty heroic stallion ancestor runs strong, touched by actual divinity and inspired to glory. These champions have the lifespan of a human and can speak a few words in Common or Celestial. They are fully aware of their superiority over common horses and pegasi, and need no training to fly with a rider, but only allow themselves to be ridden by great heroes.
