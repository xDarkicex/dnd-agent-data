# Virthad

**description**: Virthad is a particularly mean-spirited doppelganger. As a cleric of Lamashtu, their ability to change shape affords them a layer of subtlety that many villains lack. Though Virthad might adopt any guise—such as the goblin priest reflected in their stat block—who they impersonate in your game is defined by your imagination. Their skill at deception, however, should help them pose as a member of any faith common to the region in which they reside. Traits Ambusher. Virthad has advantage on attack rolls against any creature they have surprised. Spellcasting. Virthad is a 7th-level spellcaster. Their spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 17, +9 to hit with spell attacks), and they need only verbal components to cast their spells. They have the following cleric spells prepared: Cantrips (at will): guidance, mending, resistance, sacred flame 1st level (4 slots): command, cure wounds, sanctuary 2nd level (3 slots): enhance ability, hold person, silence 3rd level (3 slots): dispel magic, protection from energy, spirit guardians 4th level (1 slot): death ward Surprise Attack. If Virthad surprises a creature and hits it with an attack during the first round of combat, the target takes an extra 10 (3d6) damage from the attack. Actions Multiattack. Virthad makes three dogslicer attacks. Dogslicer. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8+4) slashing damage. Deploy Flytraps (1/day). Virthad dumps seed pods from a sack onto two squares within 5 feet while invoking prayers to Lamashtu. The pods grow into two snapping flytraps that immediately attack any non-worshipper of Lamashtu they can detect. The snapping flytraps act on Virthad’s subsequent turns. Read Thoughts. Virthad magically reads the surface thoughts of one creature within 60 feet. The effect can penetrate barriers, but 3 feet of wood or dirt, 2 feet of stone, 2 inches of metal, or a thin sheet of lead blocks it. While the target is in range, Virthad can continue reading its thoughts, as long as Virthad’s concentration isn’t broken (as if concentrating on a spell). While reading the target’s mind, Virthad has advantage on Wisdom (Insight) and Charisma (Deception, Intimidation, and Persuasion) checks against the target. Shapechanger. Virthad polymorphs into a Small or Medium humanoid they have seen, or back into their true form. Their statistics, other than their size, are the same in each form. Any equipment they are wearing or carrying isn’t transformed. Virthad reverts to their true form if they die.
