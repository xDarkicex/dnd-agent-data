# Bloodling

**description**: The bloodling is an elliptical ooze-like plant that grows on corpses that still have blood in them. It attaches itself to the corpse, digging its roots deep inside, and feeding on blood. As its roots are weak and fragile, it can’t penetrate living flesh. When a bloodling reaches maturity, it releases its spores that are invisible to the naked eye, and dies. It takes two weeks for a bloodling to reach maturity. If there is a bloodling on a fresh corpse, there are usually other bloodlings attached to the same corpse as well. When threatened, the bloodling jumps at the hostile creature and releases a toxin. The spores of a bloodling are not harmful to living creatures, and in dormant form, they can survive for up to a month in a living creature’s body. If a creature that has blood and bloodling spores in its body dies, the spores start to grow on said body, becoming a grown bloodling at the end of one day.
