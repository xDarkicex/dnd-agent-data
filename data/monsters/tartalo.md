# Tartalo

**description**: These brutish humanoids stand nearly three times the height of a human, with a single eye glaring out from beneath a beetling brow. They typically dress roughly in stitched sheepskins, but a gleaming gold ring usually adorns one of its fingers. These one-eyed giants dwell in temperate and subtropical hilly and mountainous regions. Tartalos are generally solitary, living alone in large, spacious caves. Occasionally, mated pairs or small family groups with children are encountered. Tartalos raise herd animals such as sheep or goats, grazing them on the slopes near their home, and bringing their herd into their cave home with them at night. Cannibalistic. While tartalos mainly subsist on the flesh of their herd animals, they crave the taste of humanoid flesh. If a lone hunter, lost hikers, or band of wayward travelers pass near their lair, a tartalo is sure to attack if it notices them, eager for a meal. Cursed Rings. Tartalos are known for their crafting skills, specifically rings. Tartalo have a natural magical ability, and are able to imbue the ring they forge with a captured spirit. This spirit inhabits the ring, keeps the tartalo company, and provides useful magical aid when needed. This rings rarely leave a tartalo’s hand, except when passed on to offspring. A ring is loyal to the tartalo that crafted it, or to whomever a tartalo gives it to freely. If stolen, a tartalo’s ring will attempt to get back to its owner however possible. If the thief, or anyone given the ring, puts it on, the ring cannot be removed except by the casting of a remove curse or by the ring’s own will. Typically, the ring will scream for help, loudly and incessantly, in order to attract its owner. If its owner is killed, the ring will do whatever it can to get the killer in trouble (shouting insults at inopportune moments, yelling to attract the attention of monsters, etc). The one way to stop such behavior is to agree to take the ring to another tartalo and give it to the giant, though often a ring will simply tell that tartalo about the killing of its owner, urging it to exact justice.
