# Fallen Huntmaster

**description**: Fallen Huntmasters typically travel with a pack of several Fallen beasts. These beasts can vary in threat from simple hunting dogs like the Fallen Mastiff to those as threatening as a Fallen Lion . Traits Master of the Hunt. The huntmaster has advantage on Wisdom (Survival) and Wisdom (Perception) checks made to track and spot other creatures. The huntmaster and its allies can move at full pace while tracking and remaining alert to danger. The huntmaster and its beast allies cannot be surprised. Beastmaster. The huntmaster has advantage on attack rolls against creatures within 5 feet of an allied beast. Join the Fallen. Whenever the huntmaster reduces a creature to 0 hit points, inflicts an Injury, or scores a critical hit, all creatures within 120 feet that can hear or see the target of its attack must succeed on a DC 8 Wisdom saving throw or gain a Mindscar. A creature who gains three Mindscars begins to lose its mind. During its next long rest, it will begin to scar its flesh with whatever tools are available to it. Once its rest is completed, it becomes one of the Fallen. Mindscars can be removed by any process that can remove a curse, although mental trauma will still remain. If the mental trauma is overcome, the Mindscars are removed as well. Relish the Pain. Whenever the huntmaster suffers an Injury, they gain a +1 bonus to damage rolls until the Injury is removed (up to their proficiency bonus), and gain temporary hit points equal to ½ the threshold of the Injury gained. The huntmaster never has damage loss regardless of its hit point tier for Injury thresholds, and always suffers an Injury when it takes enough damage to become Injured (the Injury it gains is chosen by the damage dealer). Actions Multiattack. The huntmaster makes two Shortsword attacks, or two Longbow attacks. Longbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, one target, range 150/600. Hit: 8 (1d8+4) piercing damage. Shortsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, one target, reach 5 feet. Hit: 7 (1d6+4) piercing damage or slashing damage. Bonus Actions Aim (recharge 5-6). The huntmaster takes aim at a creature. The next time the huntmaster hits the chosen creature with a ranged attack before the end of its turn, it deals an additional 7 (2d6) damage. Reactions Sic ‘Em. When a creature within 5 feet of an allied beast of the huntmaster hits the huntmaster with an attack, the huntmaster can use its reaction to allow that beast to make an opportunity attack without spending its own reaction.
