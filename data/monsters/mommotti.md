# Mommotti

**description**: Quiet, menacing figures, the mommotti are wicked fiends that stalk the night in search of victims. A mommotti’s flesh is dark as the surrounding night, with red eyes glowing faintly from its face. A bulging sack slung over one broad shoulder seems to shift and move, as if it holds something alive. Cruel beings, they prefer to prey on the weak and fearful, those smaller than themselves and those who cannot fight back. The fate of a mommotti’s victims is always terrible. Some are simply eaten. Others are traded or sold to other horrible creatures. Mommotti often work for hags or other fiends, kidnapping folk at their behest. Whatever their fate, those stolen by a mommotti are never seen again. Exult in Fear. Mommotti enjoy scaring their prey. They take great pleasure in the fear of others. When a mommotti fails to capture its intended victim, it is often because the fiend takes too much time indulging itself in terrifying the victim, allowing time for the victim to escape or for help to arrive. Ironic Terror. The mommotti is a bogeyman figure, often mentioned by parents as a way to scare children into behaving. (“Go to sleep! If you stay up past your bedtime, the mommotti is sure to come and steal you away!”) A mommotti that hears such things is sure to take the scolded child, as they revel in the grief experienced by such parents when their children go missing, as if they had summoned the thing itself, and blame themselves for the loss.
