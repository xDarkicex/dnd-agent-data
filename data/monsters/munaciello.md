# Munaciello

**description**: A munaciello is a non-evil ghost variant that haunts homes where families live, making pranks but also leaving small signs of its favor. It appears as a spectral apparition of a lean, emaciated child with sunken eyes, wearing a tattered friar’s robe, flashes for a moment before your eyes. You manage to catch a faint smile on its face before it vanishes again. Plague Spirit. It is said to be the ghost of a child dead by disease; in old times, parents dressed weak and ill children as monks, consecrating them to Heaven in exchange for recovery. That’s why a munaciello wears monastic robes, even in death (“munaciello” means “little monk”). Mostly, this spirit is retained in the world of the living by a regret of its unfulfilled childhood or a desire of revenge for being bullied by adults or other children when it was alive. Solitary Spirit . There’s always only one munaciello in a house. It can be an unlucky presence, causing small items to be lost, clothes to unstitch, food and milk to go bad. If its death came at the beginning of adolescence, leaving it the regret of not having known love, it can vent its frustration by touching young women in improper ways; younger munacielli may only desire to play and will manifest as spectral laughter in dark rooms and sudden voices calling the family’s children in the middle of the night. A family sometimes is forced to have the spirit exorcized, but this could attract bad luck too.
