# Slurk

**description**: The slurk is a sticky, tusked frog-beast found in underground lairs and caves. It has two massive tusks that it uses to gore prey and tangle with rival slurks. While these the protrusion of a slurk’s walrus-like tusks is certainly the slurk’s most distinctive physical trait, the copious amount of sludge and slop exuding continually from its body remains a close second, making the creature look like it just clambered out of a slime pit. Traits Back Slime. The slurk’s back slime grants a creature riding it advantage on Animal Handling checks made to stay mounted, but disadvantage on Animal Handling checks to dismount. Belly Grease. Creatures have disadvantage on Strength (Athletics) checks made to grapple the slurk, and the slurk has advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks made to escape a grapple. It never slips on grease or similar slippery terrain. As an action, a slurk can wallow on a solid surface to create a 5-foot space of slime that acts as difficult terrain for 1 minute, after which the grease dries to a nasty crust. Once per minute, a mire slurk can spend a full round wallowing and smearing its slime in its own square and all adjacent squares, creating an area of difficult terrain that lasts for 10 minutes before drying out. Hunker. The slurk has advantage on saving throws to resist being pushed or knocked prone. Actions Tusks. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 12 (3d6+2) piercing damage. Slime Squirt. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 30/60 ft., one creature. Hit: 4 (1d4+2) bludgeoning damage and the creature is restrained (escape DC 13).
