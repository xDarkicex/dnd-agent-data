# Kraken of Tolia

**description**: The Kraken of Tolia… The Bane of Ships, the Enemy of Renowned Hunters, the Tool of Legends. Some doomsayers say that this creature of many names is the scourge of the gods, an extension of the punishment they see fit for Tolia’s merciless, evil residents. Some others say it protects the greatest, the most marvelous, the most divine, and the most valuable treasures of all the world. And some who claim to have seen the monster and lived to tell the tale say that it is divine, a god in its own right that must be worshiped. The truth is, they are all right, just as they are all wrong. The Kraken of Tolia is a kraken that has evolved as a result of environmental pollution caused by the overpopulation in Kanrath and Cyherested, the humongous amounts of waste dumped into the sea from Kanrath on a daily basis, the arcane side effects of the reckless magical experiments of sorcerers in Cyherested, the magical power that the blood of the creatures Kraken hunt and eat carries, the sacrificial blood of the creatures sacrificed by the tribes, and the unique nature of angst. All of these changed the kraken over the years, turning it into a monster the likes of which none have ever seen. It is true, in this sense, that the Kraken of Tolia is a punishment of sorts; but Tolians have brought it upon themselves, and the divine has had no hand in its creation. It does protect a great treasure that it has accumulated from sunken ships and dead hunters, but the treasure is neither divine nor secret. It has power that would give the gods a run for their money, but it is not a god. The Kraken of Tolia is a unique creature. It has vampiric powers, and it can cast sorcerer spells. It is technically a half-vampire half-sorcerer kraken. Whether the kraken came to Tolia intentionally or whether it happened upon it accidentally is unknown, but it certainly has no intention of leaving.
