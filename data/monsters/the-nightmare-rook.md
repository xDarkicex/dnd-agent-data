# The Nightmare Rook

**description**: The Nightmare Rook looms both figuratively and literally over the realm of Thousandbreaths. This strange, supernatural bird can serve in your game as an unusual primary foe and source of mysterious terrors assaulting a kingdom. The more surreal and strange your encounters get, the better, so that when the PCs finally confront the source of these nightmares, the creature lives up to its name. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The Nightmare Rook’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 18, +10 to hit with spell attacks). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: 3/day each: phantasmal killer 1/day each: weird, wish Keen Sight. The Nightmare Rook has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight. Magic Resistance. The Nightmare Rook has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Magic Weapons. The Nightmare Rook’s weapon attacks are magical. Regeneration. The Nightmare Rook regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn. If the Nightmare Rook takes damage from silvered weapons, this trait doesn’t function at the start of the Nightmare Rook’s next turn. The Nightmare Rook dies only if it starts its turn with 0 hit points and doesn’t regenerate. Actions Multiattack. The Nightmare Rook can use its Frightful Presence. Then it makes two attacks: one with its beak and one with its talons. Beak. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 27 (4d8+9) piercing damage. Talons. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (4d6+9) slashing damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 22). Until this grapple ends, the target is restrained, and the Nightmare Rook can’t use its talons on another target. Frightful Presence. Each creature of the Nightmare Rook’s choice that is within 30 feet and aware of it must succeed on a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature’s saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to the Nightmare Rook’s Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.
