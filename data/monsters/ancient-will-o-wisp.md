# Ancient Will-o'-wisp

**description**: A will-o’-wisp that lingers in a region particularly suited to keeping it well-fed, such as an area under the influence of a tormented soul or other undead haunt, can over the course of decades become a much more powerful variant of its kind. Often, the vague image of a ghostly skull can be glimpsed floating within the depths of an ancient will-o’-wisp’s glow. Traits Consume Life. As a bonus action, the ancient will-o’-wisp can target one creature it can see within 5 feet of it that has 0 hit points and is still alive. The target must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw against this magic or die. If the target dies, the will-o’-wisp regains 21 (6d6) hit points. Ephemeral. The ancient will-o’-wisp can’t wear or carry anything. Incorporeal Movement. The ancient will-o’-wisp can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. It takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside an object. Variable Illumination. The ancient will-o’-wisp sheds bright light in a 5- to 20-foot radius and dim light for an additional number of feet equal to the chosen radius. The ancient will-o’-wisp can alter the radius as a bonus action. Actions Shock. Melee Spell Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 18 (4d8) lightning damage. Invisibility. The ancient will-o’-wisp and its light magically become invisible until it attacks or uses its Consume Life, or until its concentration ends (as if concentrating on a spell).
