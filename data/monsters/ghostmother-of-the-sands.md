# Ghostmother of the Sands

**description**: Named for its ability to seemingly vanish into the desert sands where it resides, the ghostmother is also known for its unsettling ability to seemingly produce offspring, ghostlings, from the slightest shard broken from its body. Ghostmothers are massive, armor-plated worms with cross jaws made of four interlocking armored plates. Their relatively short length and spiraled exoskeleton allows them to burrow at high speeds through sand by simply spinning through it, and even leap high into the air. Ghostlings. The animated shards that break off of the ghostmother when damaged are thought to be infantile ghostmothers, and do in fact resemble a tiny version of these monstrous worms. Ghostlings differ from their mothers in that they are much longer than they are wide, and their mouths are entirely circular, rather than having a cross jaw of armor plates. Ghostlings are also not well armored and are much easier to destroy. When a ghostmother appears, she is often accompanied by several ghostlings. Tactics. Ghostmothers rely on the sand of their environments to hide from their enemies and to take them by surprise whenever possible, using their Deadly IIII Crush to hit as many foes as possible. When they must make themselves vulnerable to damage, they try to spread the damage out as much as possible to make the best use of their Ghostmother's Children as possible. Traits Armored Worm. The Ghostmother's Injury thresholds are reduced by 5 for every Injury it has to its Defenses Aspect. Ghostmother's Children. When the Ghostmother takes damage for the first time on a turn, a Ghostling breaks away from its body and appears in any unoccupied space within 5 feet of the Ghostmother. Sand Ghost. While burrowing through sand, the Ghostmother adds double its proficiency bonus to Stealth checks (included in stat block). Standing Leap. The Ghostmother's long jump is up to 30 ft. and its high jump is up to 15 ft., or twice as far with a running start, as long as some of its turn is spent underground before it jumps. Actions Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: (6d12 + 4) piercing damage. Deadly Crush. If the Ghostmother jumps at least 15 ft. as part of its movement, it can then use this action to land in a space that contains one or more other creatures. Each of those creatures must succeed on a DC 16 Strength or Dexterity saving throw (target's choice) or be knocked prone and take 18 (4d6 + 4) bludgeoning damage plus 18 (4d6 + 4) slashing damage. On a successful save, the creature takes half damage, isn't knocked prone, and is pushed 5 ft. out of the Ghostmother's space into an unoccupied space of the creature's choice. If no unoccupied space is within range, the creature instead falls prone in the Ghostmother's space. Ghostling
