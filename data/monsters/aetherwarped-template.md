# Aetherwarped (template)

**description**: Creatures that spend too much time near aetheric radiation typically experience blackouts, fatigue, weariness, and then a catatonic state as their soul is slowly separated from their body. However, aetheric radiation has a subtler effect if experienced in low dosages over a prolonged period of time. Aetherwarped creatures tend to congregate in locations suffused with aetherite radiation. Curiously, they are also drawn to sources of music and can spend hours listening to the same voxophone recording. Radioactive Undead. The bodies of aetherwarped creatures twist and mutate due tolong term aetherite radiation poisoning, gaining hideous deformities and bizarre supernatural powers. The nature of aetherwarped creatures causes their lifespans to dramatically shorten, though some who perish from their sickened state often rise again as aetherwarped undead. Burnout. Once created, an aetherwarped creature lives for 2d10 days, after which it dies and cannot be returned to life except by true resurrection. If a dead aetherwarped creature rises as an undead, it also has the aetherwarped template, but does not suffer burnout, instead existing until destroyed. AETHERWARPED TEMPLATE Any corporeal living creature or undead creature can become an aetherwarped creature. When a creature becomes aetherwarped, it gains the aether tag and retains all its statistics except as noted below. Challenge Rating. The AC increase, movement, and new attacks of the aetherwarped creature can provide a +1 increase to CR. However, the Intelligence loss often negates a creature's other advantages, such as spellcasting, in which case the CR may remain the same, or even lower by 1, depending on what losses the creature suffers from the Intelligence decrease. Armor Class. The telekinetic abilities of an aetherwarped creature give it a +2 bonus to Armor Class. Speed. An aetherwarped creature gains a fly speed of 30 feet if it does not already possess a fly speed, though it cannot rise more than a foot above any solid or liquid surface. Intelligence. An aetherwarped creature's Intelligence is reduced to 2, unless it was already 2 or lower. Condition Immunities. An aetherwarped creature is immune to aetherite radiation. Senses. The aetherwarped creature gains darkvision with a range of 60 feet, and thoughtsense out to a range of 60 feet. Thoughtsense. An aetherwarped creature can detect a creature's thoughts within a 60 foot radius, allowing it to detect and locate thinking creatures as if it were using blindsight. Thoughtsense is not blocked by solid objects. Creatures can be rendered undetectable to thoughtsense through nondetection, mind blank, and similar effects. Languages. An aetherwarped creature retains the ability to understand any languages it knew prior to its affliction, but they cannot speak. Their ability to comprehend what is said to them is limited by their newly modified Intelligence. New Ability: Shun Gravity. The aetheric energies coursing through the aetherwarped creature release it from the clutch of gravity. It gains a fly speed of 30 feet, though it cannot rise more than 1 foot above any solid or liquid surface. As a bonus action, it can also change its personal gravity, allowing it to walk on walls or even ceilings as if they were level floors. New Action: Telekinetic Thrust.
