# Adamastor

**description**: Adamastor Adamastors are undead titans, towering humanoids of immense size. Slain in ancient wars with the gods, their deathless spirit endures in the depths of the sea. They generally inhabit rocky straits and continental capes, avenging their fury upon sailors of the sea and smashing their vessels when they dare to explore beyond the bounds of familiar waters. An adamastor’s underlying features are heroic but faded and worn, with heavy jowls, a long scraggly beard, hollow eyes, and an earthy pale complexion. His wild hair is matted with clay, and his mouth is full of teeth that have long since turned to black and yellow from decay. Adamastors are 60 feet tall and weigh 15 tons. Navigator’s Bane. Adamastors are often seen as a personification of the sea’s perilous nature. They are sometimes seen in a tragic light, the dying embers of a divine ambition, now lonely and melancholic under their divine curse. Some even revere them as embodying the challenges and bravery inherent to nautical exploration, a symbol of maritime history and exploration and the open horizon of possibility, while others see them as a symbol of arrogance, a conquest, and imperialism. However they are viewed by sailors, adamastors can sometimes be founts of knowledge about the oceans if caught in a sanguine mood, but approaching their haunts is dangerous indeed and rarely worth risking ship and crew. Cast into the Depths . The elder titans strove with the nascent gods and godlings in the earliest ages but their rebellion against divine authority was quelled. Some became servants of the gods and others were cursed with eternal torment or eternal imprisonment. Others were slain in the divine war and cast into the sea, but their spark of immortality left them unable to ever truly die and they became titans of the deep. The abysmal depths quenched the seething wrath of some, as they calcified into mountainous living reefs, and some even repented of their legacy of cruelty and death, but for most their loathing of the gods and their children endured throughout the ages has driven them to rise from their pelagic torpor to ravage the shipping lanes until their thirst for destruction is slaked and then fall back into the lightless depths for another generation until the furnace of their hate once more awakens them to wrath and ruin.
