# Fen Pudding

**description**: Ooze The ravenous oozes of the Stolen Lands often lurk in various types of wilderness, waiting to menace explorers they catch by surprise. Fen Pudding These more powerful variants of black puddings prefer dwelling in overgrown swamps, particularly in shallow caves abutting such terrain. They aren’t particularly great swimmers, however, and avoid sections of marshland with deeper water. Traits Amorphous. The ooze can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide without squeezing. Corrosive Form. A creature that touches the fen pudding or hits it with a melee attack while within 5 feet of it takes 4 (1d8) acid damage. Any nonmagical weapon made of metal or wood that hits the fen pudding corrodes. After dealing damage, the weapon takes a permanent and cumulative –1 penalty to damage rolls. If its penalty drops to –5, the weapon is destroyed. Nonmagical ammunition made of metal or wood that hits the pudding is destroyed after dealing damage. The pudding can eat through 2-inch-thick, nonmagical wood or metal in 1 round. Spider Climb. The fen pudding can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check. Actions Pseudopod. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8+4) bludgeoning damage plus 18 (4d8) acid damage. In addition, nonmagical armor worn by the target is partly dissolved and takes a permanent and cumulative –1 penalty to the AC it offers. The armor is destroyed if the penalty reduces its AC to 10. Reactions Split. When a fen pudding that is Medium or larger is subjected to lightning or slashing damage, it splits into two new fen puddings if it has at least 10 hit points. Each new pudding has hit points equal to half the original fen pudding’s, rounded down. New fen puddings are one size smaller than the original fen pudding.
