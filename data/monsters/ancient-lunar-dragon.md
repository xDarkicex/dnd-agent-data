# Ancient Lunar Dragon

**description**: A shimmering halo surrounds the spine-crowned head of the lunar dragon, and its vast wings ripple like milky gossamer lit by the moon. Lunar dragons spend long hours watching the activities of sentient races occurring on planets that interest them, and of all outer dragons living in the realms of space they are the most likely to interact with the curious creatures they encounter. Lair Actions On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), a lunar dragon takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects; the dragon can’t use the same effect two rounds in a row: Silvery light shines down on a point on the ground the dragon can see within 120 feet of it, creating a 20-foot high, 5-foot-radius column of moonlight. Each creature in the moonlight’s area must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw, being affected as the confusion spell on a failed save. A barrage of moonstones falls from above, striking up to three creatures that the dragon can see within 120 feet of it. The dragon makes one ranged attack roll (+6 to hit) against each creature. On a hit, the target takes 7 (2d6) bludgeoning damage and 7 (2d6) cold damage. A crater manifests at a point on the ground that the dragon can see within 120 feet of it, creating a 15-foot-radius circle of difficult terrain. Creatures that are within the area when the crater manifests must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw of fall prone. Regional Effects The region containing an ancient lunar dragon’s lair is warped by the dragon’s nature, which creates one or more of the following effects: There is perpetual night within 1 mile of the lair. This supernatural night sky is always clear and the moon, except in the case of a new moon, is always visible and unusually bright. Shapeshifters, such as werewolves, experience mild headaches within 6 miles of the lair. These headaches increase in severity as the shapeshifter draws nearer to the dragon itself. The moon appears to rapidly cycle through its phases while within 6 miles of the lair.
