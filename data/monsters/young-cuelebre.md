# Young Cuelebre

**description**: Cuélebre Cuélebres are serpentine dragons that live in caves, guarding their treasures and causing destruction to nearby settlements, farms, and livestock. They have bat-like wings, four clawed legs, and a powerful tail. Cuélebres favor mountains, and forests, usually making their lairs near lakes or rivers. They are typically solitary creatures, rarely interacting with others of its kind. Elder Retreat. Cuélebres grow larger as they age, with their scales grow thicker over the centuries, making them nearly invulnerable to attack with ordinary weapons. Eventually they stop growing, at which point they retreat to a place they call Mar Cujada, a near-mythical place unreachable by normal folk, which is full of precious stones, coins, and other treasures. No treasure hunter has ever found this place, and some wonder whether it is entirely fictional and whether ancient cuélebres facing death simply destroy their treasures and plunge their bodies into the deep in a final act of selfish avarice, so that no one else ever enjoys their plunder. Mighty Hunger. Cuélebres possess a nigh-insatiable hunger, driving them to devour livestock and villagers alike. In some stories, the local people offer it bread called boroña to appease it, though sometimes this is not enough and it must be offered first-born sons or virgins. In stories where the cuélebre is especially nasty, the villagers leave a red-hot stone in place of the bread, slaying the dragon with this ruse, though in some tales the dragon is able to save its life by flying to the nearest bay and diving into the water, cooling itself before it burned up from the inside, later returning to wreak its vengeance.
