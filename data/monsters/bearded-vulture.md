# Bearded Vulture

**description**: This dark-feathered vulture has a bald neck and head, and its long, hooked beak is caked with carrion. Unlike its smaller cousin this specimen has a magnificent beard below its beak. Though related to raptors and other birds of prey, bearded vultures mainly feed on carrion they spot or scent from on high. Vultures have weak claws compared to predatory birds, but their sharp and wickedly hooked beaks tear living flesh as readily as the sharpest blade. This particular specimen can crack bones easily as bone is the bearded vultures preferred food. Despite dining on carrion, bearded vultures prefer their corpses newly dead rather than rotten. Some even hunt smaller prey when food is scarce.
