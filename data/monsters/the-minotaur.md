# The Minotaur

**description**: This horned monster charging the group at the entrance to the Hell circle is the offspring of Pasiphae, queen of Crete, and a bull. He is a ferocious beast with the head of a bull and the body of a very tall and muscular man with short brown fur and a bull’s tail. In life, he fed on humans and lived in a maze-like labyrinth in which he was eventually killed by the hero Theseus. The Minotaur is incredibly strong. He can kill with his bare hands, either strangling or beating people to death. He can also attack with his horns, or use them to skewer a person he has thrown into the air. The Minotaur represents all three sections of the Hell circle: The brutality of Violence against others in his lifetime, and against himself, as he can be seen gnawing on his own arm when the group encounters him. And as the offspring of a human woman and an animal he also stands for Violence against nature, as Dante puts it. Although one would expect all lower Hell circles to be guarded by more powerful demons and not just a monster, this one has gained status through his fame in life. Lucifer’s hair is wound around his left horn. The Minotaur will be able to converse in Greek or in the Infernal language, but like some of the other monsters, he does not really talk a lot. Despite his beastly appearance, the Minotaur has quite delicate feelings. His life held in a labyrinth as a vicious monster damaged him severely. It made him more aggressive, but if someone approaches him with love and understanding, he will react like everyone longing for acceptance. Initially, he will act towards the group in a menacing way.
