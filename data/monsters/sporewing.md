# Sporewing

**description**: Sporewings are cunningly evolved variants of muck-dwelling ray-like creatures, adapted to life outside of the water. Their hunting and mating habits are similar to their aquatic cousins, but they have developed buoyant air-sacs that enable them to hover and fly, using their muddy coloration while clinging to the ground or to mounds of vegetation to hide their presence before launching themselves into the air to unleash their entangling spores onto their victims and gnawing them to death once they have been overcome. A sporewing is over 10 feet across, with a long dangling tail, and weighs around 600 pounds. Sporewing Dens. Sporewings often prowl alone to capitalize on choice hunting grounds, but some form small families digging out dens in thick tangles of trees and marshy hummocks. If the family group grows too large, individual sporewings set off to form their own groups rather than expanding the current den.
