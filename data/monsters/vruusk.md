# Vruusk

**description**: Driven long ago from the light of the surface world, the vruusk are a degenerate and troglodytic species of semi-­bipedal reptiles. They are covered in thick, cartilaginous plates and calloused leathery skin of mottled greys and browns that blend with the colors of their stony environments. They have hulking, broad-­shouldered frames reminiscent of great apes, with thick forelimbs that end in vicious, iron-­hard talons capable of burrowing through soil and solid stone. Due to their long, toothy maws, vruusk are superstitiously believed to be devolved from some distant draconic ancestry, but there is no evidence to suggest that these vile creatures bear any such relation. Vruusk are patient but vicious predators, stalking prey for days in the lightless tunnels they inhabit or laying in wait, all but invisible against the cold stone. When they strike, it is with lightning-­fast ferocity and utterly without mercy. Indeed, these vruusk are known to avoid killing their prey outright, preferring instead to leave them maimed and incapacitated, but alive nonetheless, as they are dragged back to their dens to be consumed piece by piece over the course of days or weeks. Traits Keen Smell. The vruusk has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell. Stone Camouflage. The vruusk has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in rocky terrain. Surprise Attack. If the vruusk surprises a creature and hits it with a bite attack during the first round of combat, the target takes an extra 11 (3d6) damage from the attack. Tunneler. The vruusk can burrow through solid rock at half its burrowing speed and leaves a 5-­foot-­wide, 8-­foot-­high tunnel in its wake. Actions Multiattack. The vruusk makes three attacks: two with its claws and one with its bite. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (1d8 + 5) slashing damage. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d8 + 5) piercing damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 16). Until this grapple ends, the target is restrained, and the vruusk can’t bite another target.
