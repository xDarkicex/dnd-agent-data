# College of Eradication Bard

**description**: These performers have chosen the battlefield as their stage, focused on evocation magic and the art of combat. Their magic discipline is native to Sparta, but other armies have tried to adopt this arcane style, to varying degrees of success. But a competent bard of this college will always manage to raise the spirits of their comrades-in-arms. Humanoids Humanoids are the most plentiful creatures amongst the creations of the gods. They create vast kingdoms, they make unbelievable technological advances and even achieve breakthroughs in the art of magic. Traits Magic Weapons. The bard's weapon attacks are magical. Spellcasting. The bard's Spellcasting Ability is Charisma (spell save DC 17, +9 to hit with spell attacks). They can cast the following spells: At Will: fire bolt, ray of frost, shocking grasp 3/day each: healing word, misty step, shatter (as a 4th level spell), shield 2/day each: counterspell, fireball, hypnotic pattern, lightning bolt, mirror image 1/day each: bigby's hand, cone of cold, chain lightning, dimension door Actions Multiattack. The bard makes two maul attacks. Maul. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d10 + 3) bludgeoning damage. Roar of Warmongering (2/day). The bard chooses up to 5 creatures within 60 feet of them. These creatures can choose one the following benefits: The creatures regain 14 (4d6) hit points. They can spend their reaction to immediately teleport anywhere within 30 feet of them that they can see. They can spend their reaction to make a single attack or cast a cantrip. Bonus Actions War-born Inspiration (3/Day). The bard can target one creature within 60 feet of them. The creature will have advantage on the next attack roll, saving throw or ability check they make. If they choose to make an attack they deal an additional 7 (2d6) damage. Taunt (3/Day). The bard can target one creature within 30 feet of them. If the target can hear the bard, the target must succeed on a DC 17 Charisma saving throw or have disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws until the start of the bard’s next turn.
