# Amadeus Filius Christianus

**description**: Amadeus is the eldest son of Christianus, whom the group has met in the previous scene. This dwarf in his mid-thirties is even taller than his father, almost 5.3 feet (1.6 meters). He has his father’s dark hair and the striking blue eyes of his mother. Amadeus looks extremely attractive in his shiny uniform and has already achieved a high rank in the legion of Polupolis. He commands the first century of the first cohort and will therefore lead the first cohort if there is a battle. A soothsayer once predicted a brilliant career to the young man and he is determined to make it come true. Yes, he is quite vain. Traits Protection. When a creature Amadeus can see attacks a target other than him that is within 5 feet of him, he can use his Reaction to impose disadvantage on one of its attack rolls once per turn. Second Wind. On his turn, he can use a bonus action to regain 12 (1d10 + 7) hit points once per day. Improved Critical. Amadeus‘ weapon attacks score critical hits on a roll of 19 or 20. Actions Multiattack. Amadeus makes two attacks with his pilum. Pilum +2. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing damage.
