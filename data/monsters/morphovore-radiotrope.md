# Morphovore Radiotrope

**description**: Morphovores are sentient plant carnivores that are able to constantly undergo genetic mutations to adapt to nearly any environment. When a morphovore arrives in an unfamiliar environment, it can drastically change its size, mass, and hunting techniques to complement its new environment within its own short-lived lifespan. Solitary Colonizers Morphovores are solitary hunters but may be found in loose clusters – usually in rich feeding grounds where they exploit a common resource rather than acting as a coherent group. They reproduce asexually by releasing spores when their flower-like appendage blooms. These resilient spores can travel long distances and remain dormant for extended periods until mophovores arrive in a suitable environment to germinate. This allows morphovores to quickly colonize new areas whenever a major change to an environment opens new ecological opportunities. Morphovore Devourer In areas with low gravity, morphovores develop large, bulbous bodies with a semi-translucent surface that reveals their internal structures – and the unfortunate creatures being digested within. This type of morphovore can effortlessly climb any surface. Moreover, its flower-like appendage can evolve into a wide, sticky maw that it can use to produce sticky excretion and engulf creatures, slowly breaking down its food with acidic secretions. Morphovore Radiotropes In areas with high levels of radiation, morphovores adapt by developing thick, leaden bark and a deep, vibrant glow. Their flower-like appendage evolves into a radioluminescent organ that can emit light and radiation, while their roots allow them to turn radioactive elements from the soil into nourishment – thus thriving where other species falter. Morphovore Stranglethorns In environments with intense gravity or dense vegetation, where physical strength and stealth are more advantageous than speed and agility, morphovores develop a small, compact body. These morphovores evolve the ability to spew deadly acid from their flower-like appendages while using thick and far-reaching vines to ensnare prey.
