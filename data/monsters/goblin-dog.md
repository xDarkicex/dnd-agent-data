# Goblin Dog

**description**: Goblins’ eponymous pets aren’t true canines at all, but rather large, blunt-nosed rodents with thin bodies and long legs. Those who point out the legendary fear of dogs most goblins have might wonder why these creatures carry such a name, but as any goblin can tell you, goblin dogs represent what a real dog should be. All the rest are little more than monsters that are worthy of being killed on sight–or more often, a panicked retreat. As cowardly as they are ugly, a goblin dog prefers to lurk in bushes or deep shadows, pouncing on lone or wounded prey, although hunger or encouragement at the hand of goblin trainers can make them much more bold. Their patchy bald hides are constantly infested with rashes and dander. Traits Goblin Pox. A creature exposed to goblin pox must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution saving throw or become afflicted with goblin pox. On a successful saving throw, it is immune to goblin pox for 24 hours. A creature with goblin pox is poisoned and must repeat the saving throw at the end of its next turn. On a success, the effect ends. On a failed save, the creature can use either an action or a bonus action on its turns, but not both. The creature can then repeat the saving throw after each long rest, ending the effects on itself on a success. Goblins and goblin dogs are immune to goblin pox. Irritating Dander. A creature that touches the goblin dog or hits the goblin dog with an unarmed strike is exposed to goblin pox. Keen Smell. The goblin dog has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell. Actions Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (2d4+2) piercing damage. Scratch. The goblin dog vigorously scratches itself, exposing all creatures within 5 feet of it to goblin pox. Reactions Juke. When the goblin dog is given an order by a creature mounted to it, the goblin dog moves up to 5 feet. The goblin dog doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks with this movement.
