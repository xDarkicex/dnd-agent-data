# Jas

**description**: These small creatures live in communion with the land and with clans and villages that follow ancient rites of mystery cults rather than following more modern religions. It is said that they knitted the first linens of humanity thousands of years ago. The jãs are fairy weavers - so skilled that in a matter of hours, they can weave meters and meters of linen (as fine as a strand of hair) without a single imperfection. These fey beings materialize in the embers of fireplaces after the sun has set. In exchange for their knitting work, they accept bread, wine, or cakes. Despite the benevolence they display, they have a self-interested nature and can be vindictive (it's said that they'll burn the provided linen if the offering displeases them). They can talk with birds and foresee the future in the woven threads. Occasionally, they might abduct human infants and replace them with their own fey or part-fey children (known as Janines), so that these are nursed and grow up outside the fairy realms, later returning their adopted human children with a fey blessing.
