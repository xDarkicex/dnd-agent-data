# Wererat Pack Lord

**description**: Pack lords are the alphas of the pack, and they are usually the largest and most experienced ones. To become a pack lord requires slaying the current one in single combat. Traits Keen Smell. The wererat has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell. Pack Tactics. The wererat has advantage on an attack roll against a creature if at least one of the wererat's allies is within 5 feet of the creature and the ally isn't incapacitated. Packmaster. An ally within 5 feet of the wererat has advantage on attack rolls against a creature that is within 5 feet of the wererat and another ally, provided the wererat and the other ally aren't incapacitated. Sneak Attack (1/Turn). The wererat deals an extra 14 (4d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the wererat that isn't incapacitated and the wererat doesn't have disadvantage on the attack roll. Actions Multiattack. The wererat makes three attacks, only one of which can be a Bite. Bite (Rat or Hybrid Form Only). Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d4 + 4) piercing damage. lf the target is a humanoid, it must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or be cursed with wererat lycanthropy. Shortsword (Humanoid or Hybrid Form Only). Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing damage. Hand Crossbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing damage. Summon Rats (Recharges after a Short or Long Rest). The wererat summons 3d4 giant rats. The giant rats appear in unoccupied spaces within 30 feet of the wererat and acts as its allies. The giant rats act right after the wererat on the same initiative count and fight until they're destroyed. They disappear when the wererat dies. Call Assault (1/Day). Up to three wererat allies that are within 30 feet of this wererat can use their reaction to move up to half their speed and make one melee weapon attack on the attack roll. This wererat can then take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action as a bonus action. Change Shape. The wererat magically transforms into a rat-humanoid hybrid or into a giant rat or back into its true form (humanoid), while retaining its game statistics (other than its size). This transformation ends if the wererat is reduced to 0 hit points or uses a bonus action to end it.
