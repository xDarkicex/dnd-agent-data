# Bonestealer - The Mask-Bearing Horror of Journey's End City

**description**: Traits Vile Depredation. When the bonestealer scores a critical hit against another creature, the bonestealer immediately regains 11 (2d10) hit points. Vile Mutation. At the start of its turn, if the bone­stealer’s current hit points are equal to or lower than 84, it gains the Foul Separation Shadowlands power (see Adventures in Rokugan , pages 410–411). If the bonestealer’s current hit points are equal or lower than 42, it gains the Dark Puppet Shadowlands power. The bonestealer loses these powers at the end of the encounter. Actions Multiattack. The bonestealer makes three slam attacks. Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (3d6 + 5) bludgeoning damage. Creatures damaged by this attack are pushed 5 ft away from the bonestealer; if this knocks them into a wall or other solid object, they take an additional 1d6 bludgeoning damage. Scores a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20. Swipe. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 5 ft., all targets in range. Hit: 14 (2d8 + 5) slashing damage. Creatures hit by this attack suffer the bleeding condition until the start of the bonestealer’s next turn. Legendary Actions The bonestealer can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature’s turn. The bonestealer regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Living Wounds. Use this legendary action only if the bonestealer took damage on the previous turn. The flesh damaged by the attack falls to the ground writhing, and it takes upon a terrifying life of its own. Create a lesser horror creature up to 5 ft away from the bonestealer (Adventures in Rokugan page 408). Ferocious Swipe. The bonestealer makes one swipe attack. Bonestealing . Targets a single creature within 10 feet of it that made an attack against it on their turn. That creature must make a DC 20 Dexterity saving throw. On a failure, the bonestealer grabs one of the creature’s limbs and saps their strength. The bonestealer gains 1d12 temporary hit points, and until the end of the encounter that creature becomes vulnerable to bludgeoning damage and gains the maimed condition. Lair Actions When fighting inside its lair, the bonestealer can harness the cursed energy of its domain to take lair actions. On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the bonestealer takes a lair action to cause one of the following effects: Bone stitching. The bonestealer regains 11 (2d10) hit points. Afterwards if it has less than half its maximum health it spawns a lesser horror ( Adventures in Rokugan , page 408) up to 5 ft away from it. Echoes of the Mask. The mask pulses with unholy energy, each creature within 15 ft. of the bonestealer makes a wisdom saving throw DC 15; if they fail, they have disadvantage on their next attack or saving throw this combat encounter.
