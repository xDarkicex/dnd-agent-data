# Legendary Priest

**description**: Traits Legendary Resistance (2/Day). If the priest fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead. Spell Penetration. When the priest casts a spell and is interrupted by an enemy's counterspell, treat the spell's level as 3 levels higher. Superior Focus. The priest has advantage on saving throws it makes to maintain its concentration. Actions Quarterstaff. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d6 + 1) bludgeoning damage, or 5 (1d8 + 1) bludgeoning damage if wielded with two hands. Spellcasting. The priest casts one of the following spells using Wisdom as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 15, +7 to hit with spell attacks): At will: guidance, light, sacred flame (2d8), thaumaturgy, word of radiance (2d6) 2/day each: guiding bolt, hold person, mass healing word 1/day each: banishment, cure wounds (as a 5th-level spell), dispel magic, flame strike, guardian of faith* *The priest casts this spell on itself before combat. Bonus Actions Divine Eminence (2/Day). The high priest causes its melee weapon attacks to magically deal an extra 10 (3d6) radiant (good or neutral) or necrotic (evil or neutral) damage to a target on a hit. This benefit lasts until the end of the turn. Legendary Actions The priest can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The priest regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Move. The priest moves up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks. Cantrip. The priest casts sacred flame or word of radiance . Quarterstaff. The priest attacks with its Quarterstaff. Cast a Spell (Costs 2 Actions). The priest casts cure wounds, dispel magic, guiding bolt, hold person , or mass healing word, if available.
