# Advanced Yoten Devotee

**description**: Yoten Devotee These Yoten are fluid in form, often changing shape. They tend to wander the worlds they end up on, with some working with one another, while others strike off on their own. Strength Over Time. Yoten devotees become more powerful the longer they survive. These experienced Yoten use the advanced Yoten devotee statistics block instead. These powerful Yoten also have access to Lair Actions in addition to their normal capabilities. Lair Actions When fighting inside their lair, the devotee can invoke the ambient magic to take lair actions. On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the devotee can take one lair action to cause one of the following effects: Vanish. The devotee becomes invisible until the end of their next turn, and all hostiles within their lair are deafened for the same duration. This invisibility is not broken by attacks or spells. Rumble. An earthquake shakes the ground in and around the lair. Any creatures standing on the ground are knocked prone and take 9 (2d8) bludgeoning damage. Reverberations through the ground causes the entire lair to be difficult terrain until another lair action is used. Surge of Corruption. Thunderous sound erupts throughout the lair, which can be heard from up to three miles away. Creatures in the region that are concentrating on a spell have their concentration broken and take 27 (6d8) thunder damage. Other creatures take 13 (3d8) thunder damage. A foul wind lingers and spoils all food and water unless quickly dispersed. Quiet. All sound suddenly ceases throughout the lair, as if affected by a silence spell. Creatures cannot communicate with each other verbally, and spells with vocal components cannot be cast. The silence lasts until another lair action is used. The devotee can’t repeat an effect until they have all been used, and they can’t use the same effect two rounds in a row.
