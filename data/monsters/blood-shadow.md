# Blood Shadow

**description**: Yoten The true origins of the Yoten are likely never to be known but not all are loyal to Muspell, like Boða. Many just want to roam and devour as they desire. Monstrous Offspring. The Yoten are capable of breeding with numerous other creatures, which makes it difficult—but not impossible—to ascertain which creatures are true Yoten. True Yoten include corpse swallowers, forest striders, ratatoskr, and yoten devotees. The offspring of Yoten are blood shadows and tussers. Strength Dominates. Yoten grow in power throughout their lifetime. Younger and weaker Yoten follow their more experienced brethren until ready to assert their own strength. Giant-like Vulnerability. All Yoten are vulnerable to weapons that do additional damage or effects against the Giant creature type, which suggests some deeper connection to the giant-folk. Child of Muspell. All Yoten are born from the entity Muspell. If targeted by a spell like banishment, the Yoten’s plane of existence is considered to be the plane where Muspell currently is. Death Cairns. Most Yoten turn to stone when they are slain. These cairns can be found throughout the Lost Lands and occasionally even in Grimnir. Blood Shadow Blood shadows are creatures of suffering, born from the torment inflicted upon captive wolves by their Yoten captors. No longer a wolf—or resembling any living creature—they maintain the pack-like instincts of their progenitors; except they identify the Yoten as their pack-mates. Unerring Allies. Blood shadows do anything other Yoten command them to do, even sacrificing their own lives.
