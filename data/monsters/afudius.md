# Afudius

**description**: The myths and legends of the Mediterranean are replete with strange and mystic serpents of every description. Afudius. This long, muscular snake rears revealing its devilish horns and fangs when threatened. Sometimes also called a haemorrhois, it is small, just over a foot long, with males having multiple bristling horns and females a single off-center horn. An afudius is pitch black or fiery red in color, with bright eyes in a broad head. Its scales are rough and make a rustling sound as the snake moves. They are slow and sluggish animals and make winding nests in rocky areas. Envenomation by an afudius is particularly gruesome. As the venom spreads, blood begins to leave by every opening possible, draining out through the eyes, ears, nostrils, mouth, even the very pores. Scars reopen. Teeth and nails fall out. The entire body becomes one bleeding wound, and death comes through catastrophic blood loss.
