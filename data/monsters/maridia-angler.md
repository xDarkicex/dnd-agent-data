# Maridia Angler

**description**: Maridia are an ancient race of aquatic beings that reside in underwater cities and vast cave complexes near the ocean. Encased in shimmering scales that span shades of indigo, green, and purple, most maridia are also distinguished by a bioluminescent dorsal fin that protrudes from their head. Both play a crucial role in their communication, which involves gestures and subtle shifts in their skin coloration. Devoted Hunters Maridia subsist mainly on fish, hunting in groups and employing techniques that utilize their ability to manipulate light. Maridia often harbor deep-seated traditions and beliefs, particularly revering the lunar cycles and their influence on tidal movements. Maridia also form deep bonds with sea creatures that they hold in high esteem as their companions. Communal Reproduction At the core of maridia society are the vast egg farms where all maridia are hatched. Distinct from many species, maridia do not have individual parental roles and instead adopt a communal approach to reproduction. Every maridia contributes to the fertilization and care of the eggs, ensuring a diverse genetic pool and reinforcing social cohesion. Maridia Angler Although many subspecies of maridia exist, Maridia Anglers are by far the predominant variant among them. This type of maridia occupies most societal roles, including that of laborers, caretakers, scouts, and hunters. Their ability to use bioluminescent signals to communicate and mesmerize prey makes them highly efficient at coordinated group hunting. Maridia Blightgills Maridia Blightgills contrast sharply with their kin. Devoid of the bioluminescent ray common to maridia, they have enlarged gills and display darker, subdued scales that help them blend with their surroundings. These maridia have a venomous bite and emit a foul stench, allowing them to ward off predators and mark their hunting grounds. Their resilient nature makes maridia blightgills the warriors and protectors of choice in maridia society Maridia Shamans Maridia Shamans serve as spiritual leaders of their communities and wield the power to heal injuries, manipulate water currents, and tame sea creatures. These maridia shamans are not only tasked with preserving the ancient traditions of their kin, but also serve as caretakers of their species' reproduction, ensuring that the temperature and water currents of their hatching grounds are always cared for. Communicating with Maridia Maridia have no spoken language and use a sophisticated system of gestures, bioluminescent flashes, and subtle shifts in their skin coloration to communicate, rather than relying on vocal communication. A character that spends 1 hour watching Maridia interact with each other can make a DC 15 Wisdom (Insight) check. On a success, it grasps the basics of their communication and learns simple signals such as “danger” or “food”; of course, carrying a conversation with maridia is an entirely different matter.
