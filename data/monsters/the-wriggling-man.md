# The Wriggling Man

**description**: The Wriggling Man is one of Nyrissa’s closest allies—a powerful wizard whose will to live outlasted his original body and continues on in a colony of writhing worms. He works best as a sinister mastermind in other campaigns, and can fill roles classically portrayed by lich warlords or sinister archmages. Due to his terrifying and unusual appearance, introducing him as a villain in your campaign is bound to keep your PCs on their toes! Traits Regeneration. The Wriggling Man regains 18 hit points at the start of his turn. If he takes acid or fire damage, this trait doesn’t function at the start of The Wriggling Man’s next turn. If the Wriggling Man starts his turn with 0 hit points and doesn’t regenerate, he makes a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, dying on a failure. On a success, his form collapses into a mass of dead worms, as it does when he dies, but one worm survives. The Wriggling Man reforms from the remaining worm in 1d10 days, fully healed. Spellcasting. The Wriggling Man is a 17th-level spellcaster. His spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 20, +12 to hit with spell attacks). The Wriggling Man has the following wizard spells prepared: Cantrips (at will): acid splash, light, mage hand, mending, prestidigitation 1st level (4 slots): grease, magic missile 2nd level (3 slots): invisibility 3rd level (3 slots): dispel magic, fireball, fly, protection from energy 4th level (3 slots): dimension door, faithful hound, fire shield (deals cold damage), greater invisibility 5th level (2 slots): geas, telepathic bond 6th level (1 slots): chain lightning, contingency, mass suggestion 7th level (1 slots): forcecage, reverse gravity 8th level (1 slots): feeblemind, mind blank, power word stun 9th level (1 slots): time stop Actions Multiattack. The Wriggling Man makes two slam attacks. Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 22 (5d6+5) bludgeoning damage.
