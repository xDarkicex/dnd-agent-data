# Anguana

**description**: Anguana These elusive fey have the upper body of a beautiful humanoid and the lower body of an animal- most often, a snake, fish, or goat. Almost all anguane are female: males are very rare and seldom spotted. They choose beautiful freshwater sites as their homes. Each anguana can also assume the full form of the animal she looks like. An anguana can be found living in a waterfall, stream, pond, or spring. A lake or river can be home to many anguane, living remotely or collectively as they choose. Private. The reaction of an anguana to outsiders depends on its history and individual outlook. Some are welcoming of strangers, offering assistance and willing to trade. Others are stand-offish and reclusive. Some are actively hostile to others, especially humanoids, using their powers to trick or harm those that wander into their territory. An anguana could leave her river to follow a human she has fallen in love with, though requesting the human not ever to reveal her secret to anyone (and cursing the human if they break this promise); other anguane can kidnap young, handsome men for a certain period of time, for reproduction purposes or just to have company. Productive Teachers. All anguanas have mastered a craft and are proficient in at least one set of tools. Some anguane are specialized in more than one type; the older the anguana, the more likely she has mastered more than one set of tools. If approached in a proper and respectful manner, anguane will sometimes teach other creatures their craft. Some of the best fisherfolk live near an anguana and have learned their craft from her. Water Protectors . An anguana considers the water where she lairs to be sacrosanct. Any creatures that defile or pollute a water source garner the wrath of any anguane that live in the area. They will band together to stop and drive away the offending creatures, as well as enlisting any beasts in the area to which they can speak.
