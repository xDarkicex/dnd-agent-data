# Marsh Giant

**description**: Giant In the Stolen Lands, hill giants are the most common of their kind. Small numbers of marsh giants can be found in the depths of Hooktongue Slough. Marsh Giant Marsh giants aren’t common in the Stolen Lands as the rare few who dwell there reside in Hooktongue Slough—typically in small groups numbering no more than a half dozen at most. A marsh giant stands 11 feet tall—small for giants, perhaps, but their eerie fishlike features and tendency for zealous cruelty help them to leave lasting impressions when encountered. Traits Amphibious. The marsh giant can breathe both air and water. Innate Spellcasting. The marsh giant’s spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 14). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: 3/day each: augury, bestow curse, fog cloud Actions Multiattack. The marsh giant makes three attacks: it attacks once with its gaff and twice with its slam or makes two thrown rock attacks. It can make a hook shake action in place of its gaff attack. +1 Gaff. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (3d6+7) bludgeoning or piercing damage, and the creature is grappled (escape DC 14). Until this grapple ends, the creature is restrained, and the marsh giant can’t use its gaff on another target. Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (3d4+6) bludgeoning or piercing damage. Thrown Rock. Ranged Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, range 60/240 ft., one target. Hit: 19 (3d8+6) bludgeoning. Hook Shake. The marsh giant shakes a creature it has grappled by its gaff, shaking it violently. The creature must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 28 (8d6) piercing damage on a failure and, if the target is underwater, it begins suffocating if it wasn’t already, as its breath is forced from its lungs.
