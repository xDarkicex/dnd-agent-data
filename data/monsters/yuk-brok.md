# Yuk-Brok

**description**: Background A skilled warrior, Yuk-Brok only ever wanted to crack skulls and cleave limbs. As an adventurer, he did plenty of both until a few years ago, when Yuk-Brok's friend and fellow adventurer, the wizard Laryn Lilyfield, had a mishap involving a Bag of Holding and a Portable Hole that created an astral portal in her home. Laryn and all the treasures she'd gathered over the years were sucked into the portal and scattered across the Astral Plane. As luck would have it, Laryn had designated Yuk-Brok as her sole heir. At first, Yuk-Brok didn't know what to do with the house and the portal that still churned within it. Then, aided by Laryn's owl familiar, he discovered that when valuables are poured into the portal, it spits forth one of the objects that were lost within it. Figuring that there might be a way to get Laryn back, Yuk-Brok opened the Dispensary, and now tries to convince adventurers to pour valuables into the portal to hopefully bring back his lost friend. Roleplaying Yuk-Brok Yuk-Brok is a simple fellow. He likes ale, he likes brawls, and he likes good food – or any food, really. He is friendly and quick to trust others, but has the temperamental mood common to both bugbears and barbarians. If he believes he's being insulted, Yuk-Brok is quick to anger ("what'ya say, lad? You takin' the piss, yeah?”) but quickly calms down if he gets an apology. Yuk-Brok isn't alone in the shop; Laryn's owl familiar, Whitefeather, keeps a watchful eye on visitors. Imbued with magical sentience and supernatural intelligence, the owl can not only speak, but does so with much more eloquence and care than its bugbear companion. The owl intervenes if anyone tries to harm or cheat Yuk-Brok, or if the volatile bugbear is about to fly off the handle ("Calm, Yuk-Brok! I'm shu-uh-ure these fine folk meant no offense.”) For his part, Yuk-Brok has enormous respect for the bird's wisdom and usually defers to it.
