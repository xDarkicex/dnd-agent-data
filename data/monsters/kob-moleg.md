# Kob Moleg

**description**: The leader of a band of notorious hill giants, Kob Moleg’s alliance with the king of Pitax forces him into conflict with the PCs’ kingdom when war between the two nations begins. Kob Moleg is so much more powerful than the standard hill giant that he arguably works better as a unique foe in a campaign than just a clan leader—perhaps he might serve as the primary ruler of all hill giants in an entire region in your game. Traits Kob’s Ruinous Strike. When Kob Moleg scores a critical hit with his greatclub, the target becomes frightened of him for 1 minute. Actions Multiattack. The giant makes three greatclub attacks. Striking Greatclub +2. Melee Weapon Attack: +16 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 24 (3d8+11) bludgeoning and piercing damage. The greatclub has 1d10 charges (minimum of 3) at the start of the encounter. When the giant hits with a melee attack, he can expend up to 3 of the club’s charges. For each charge it expends, the target takes an extra 7 (2d6) force damage. The greatclub otherwise works as a staff of striking but is sized for a Huge giant. Wide Swing (Recharge 5–6). Kob Moleg swings his greatclub in a powerful circle. Every creature within 10 feet of the giant must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw. On a failed saving throw, a creature takes 24 (3d8+11) bludgeoning damage and is knocked prone. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn’t knocked prone. The giant can expend charges from its greatclub of striking to increase this damage. Reactions Catch Rock. Kob Moleg can attempt to catch a rock thrown at a target that is within 10 feet of itself. The giant makes a Dexterity saving throw against the attack roll of the attacking creature. If it succeeds, the giant catches the rock and the intended target takes no damage.
