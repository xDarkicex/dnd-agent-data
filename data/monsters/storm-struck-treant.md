# Storm-Struck Treant

**description**: While treants (also known as arboreals) have a reputation for being benevolent (if stubborn) guardians of the woods, those who are struck by lightning or subjected to ages of cruelty can twist and deform into more powerful variants capable of using the very energies that harmed them as weapons. Storm-struck treants look like dead trees—their trunks scarred by blasts of lightning or even charred as if by the flames of a recent forest fire—until they lurch to attack. Traits False Appearance. While the storm-struck treant remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from a normal tree. Innate Spellcasting. The storm-struck treant’s innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 18). The lightning treant can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: 3/day: lightning bolt 1/day: chain lightning Lightning Absorption. Whenever the lightning treant is subjected to lightning damage, it takes no damage and instead regains a number of hit points equal to the lightning damage dealt. Siege Monster. The storm-struck treant deals double damage to objects and structures. Actions Multiattack. The storm-struck treant makes three slam attacks. Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 24 (5d6+7) bludgeoning damage.
