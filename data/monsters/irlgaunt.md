# Irlgaunt

**description**: Irlgaunts resemble titanic spiders or crabs, but with cephalopod-like tendrils erupting from the tips of their chitin-armored legs. Their jagged gray shells allow them to blend into the rocky walls of the high mountain passes and deep ravines that serve as their hunting grounds. While one might easily mistake an irlgaunt for a simple brutish beast, they have a keen intelligence and use devious hunting strategies. They set traps for travelers and are fond of using gems and magical items taken from previous victims as bait. They have been known to start rockslides or otherwise block passages to reroute explorers into their clutches. They also use spewed gastroliths (melon-sized clusters of rocks enveloped in coagulated digestive enzymes) to direct the movements of their prey, forcing victims into dead ends at the edge of chasms or cliffs. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The irlgaunt’s innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 17). The irlgaunt can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: meld into stone, stone shape Stone Step. The irlgaunt ignores difficult terrain composed of rocks and stone. Actions Multiattack. The irlgaunt makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its stomps. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (3d10+7) piercing damage plus 10 (3d6) acid damage. Stomp. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (2d8+7) bludgeoning damage. Regurgitate Gastrolith (Recharge 5—6). The irlgaunt violently regurgitates a melon-sized clot infused with digestive enzymes at a point it can see within 30 feet of it. Each creature within 20 feet of that point must make a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw as the clot explodes on impact, taking 21 (6d6) piercing and 21 (6d6) acid damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
