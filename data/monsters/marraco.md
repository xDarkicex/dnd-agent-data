# Marraco

**description**: These spiny, wingless dragons have broad, tall heads and enormous mouths that split into gruesome quadrate jaws, with a toothy maw large enough for a human to stand in and tongues of flame licking out past their massive fangs. These lesser cousins of true dragons prefer hilly, forested regions. Unable to fly like their greater kin, the marraco stalks its prey on foot, willing and able to track a scent for days. Marraco are voracious and greedy eaters, swallowing their prey mostly whole, up to and including horses and elk. An adult marraco averages 32 feet in length from the tip of its snout to its thick, short tail. Its heavy scales are mottled purplish gray on its top and sides, darkening to a muddy brown on its underside. A row of black spines crests the dragon’s head, running the length of its back and tail. Firebreather. The marraco can breathe fire, though not with the heat and intensity of a red dragon. A marraco is cunning enough to use its fire breath in hunting, sometimes setting brush aflame to scare potential prey into the open. Some marraco appear to prefer cooked meat, and will scorch prey with their flame breath before gulping down the corpse.
