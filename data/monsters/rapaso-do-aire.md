# Rapaso do Aire

**description**: Spirits of those who committed terrible crimes to prolong their own lives, in death these dark souls become raposa do aire, doomed to wander the land seeking out death and despair, feeding on it to prolong their miserable undead existence rather than face final death. Raposa do aire are naturally invisible and rarely reveal their true form. When one is seen, its body appears as a tattered skin or fur, whipping and lashing in an unfelt gale. Atop this form is a vulpine head, with an unholy fire burning in its eyes and mouth. Feeds on Fear. The raposa do aire feeds on the fear of living mortals as well as the energy of dying souls released from their mortal coils. It will often plague those near a dying person with strange and unusual sounds, produced by its Mimicry ability. Cawing ravens, sinister whispers, yowling or hissing cats, and other unpleasant sounds will often “haunt” the area, unsettling those within. Harbingers of Death. While the raposa do aire is often responsible for deaths in the immediate area, it is always drawn there, sensing the inevitable demise of creatures and wanting to feed on the despair and fear. Phantasmagoria. A rapaso do aire usually keeps itself hidden but manifests to other senses. It often comes in the form of a roar, a moan, or a shuddering howl that runs through every corner of the house inhabited by the person who is about to die. Nothing can be seen, but a definite, hostile presence can be felt, making the air around feel heavy and oppressive. It can be heard in the night, in the air, on the ground and even within oneself and sounds like the sound of cats, crows, roosters and even the cries of children calling to the living. When the scream sounds, those who hear often feel that they are already dead.
