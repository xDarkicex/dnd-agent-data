# Aeetos

**description**: Gorgon Gorgons are magical, foul-tempered creatures, and while their skin is covered with metallic plates they are flesh and bone beneath. They derive nutrients from the consumption of minerals, particularly the stone of their petrified victims, and any statues they create are likely to be gnawed thoroughly. They cannot digest metal or gems, so their dung (which resembles bitter-smelling gray powder) often contains small, raw crystals and nuggets of ore. Their aggression toward all other creatures means that there are few to no predators or other prey animals in their grazing grounds. Each herd is led by a dominant bull; solitary khalkatauroi are usually adolescent bulls driven out of their herd by the lead bull. The females are just as dangerous as the males, and the two sexes appear identical. A typical gorgon stands 6 feet tall and 8 feet long. It weighs 4,000 pounds. Aeetos. While the hide of most gorgons resembles dull iron, that of the aeetos is like burnished bronze. These brazen bulls radiate an inner heat, some say sparked within them from the forges of an ancient god who crafted them from ingots before he breathed life into them and set them loose on the world as a gift to a mighty king. Unlike other gorgons, an aeetos can digest metal after reducing it to slag in their gullets.
