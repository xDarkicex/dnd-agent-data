# Thunder Guard Bully

**description**: The Thunder Guard are the elite enforcers of the city governor, Shosuro Hyobu. Operating with the authority of lower-ranked samurai despite being commoners, they are instantly recognizable by their black-and-crimson heavy armor and long pikes. While the Thunder Guard were technically founded to keep the peace, they are not magistrates and do not enforce the law. Their roles are purely to prevent violence from spilling into the streets and to be the governor’s steady right hand as needed. They are usually only deployed in significant numbers during riots and similar violence. While they are loyal to the governor, they are just as corruptible as other city officials, accepting bribes or working enforcement jobs on the side. Traits Corrupt. Depending on the severity of their offense, players may pay the Thunder Guard bully to look the other way and not punish them for perceived wrongdoing. This costs 4 sp (2 bu) per caught player, but can cost several times more for serious crimes. A Thunder Guard can’t be bribed this way if they are in the presence of a higher ranked magistrate that does not have the corrupt trait. Looming Presence. Hostile creatures within 5 feet of the Thunder Guard bully have disadvantage on Charisma (Intimidation) and Charisma (Persuasion) checks targeting the Thunder Guard bully. Rank Fighting. When the Thunder Guard bully makes a melee weapon attack, if the attack misses, they may choose a friendly creature within 10 feet. The target deals an additional 1d6 damage on its next melee weapon attack. Actions Multiattack. The Thunder Guard bully makes two melee attacks. Crescent Halberd (two-handed). Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d10 + 3) slashing damage. Crossbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d8 + 1) piercing damage. Reactions Parry. The Thunder Guard bully adds +2 to their AC against one melee attack that would hit them.
