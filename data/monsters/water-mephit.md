# Water Mephit

**description**: Elemental, Water Elementals are outsiders formed of living matter, be it from air, earth, fire, or in the case of the creature below, water. Water Mephit Mephits are little bipedal elementals with bat-like wings who serve stronger elementals or, in some cases, spellcasters capable of conjuring them as familiars. Many different types of mephits exist beyond the water mephit detailed below. Mephits are often called upon by spellcasters to serve as familiars; see the entry for Lintwerth for one such example. Traits Death Burst. When the mephit dies, it explodes in a burst of high-pressure water. Each creature within 5 feet of it must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw or be knocked prone. False Appearance. While the mephit remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from an ordinary puddle of water. Innate Spellcasting (1/Day). The mephit can innately cast stinking cloud , requiring no material components. Its innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 12). Actions Multiattack. The water mephit makes two attacks with its claws. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (2d6+2) piercing damage and 4 (1d8) acid damage. Acid Breath (Recharge 6). The mephit exhales a 15-foot cone of acid. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw, taking 13 (3d8) acid damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
