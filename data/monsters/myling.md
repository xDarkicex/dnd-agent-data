# Myling

**description**: Denizens of the Underworld Many of the undead of Grimnir serve a somewhat different role than they do in other worlds. While on the surface skeletons and zombies exist as dangers to the living, their existence is more nuanced. Many of the dead are agents of Hel and scour the surface world at her bidding. They gather information, they help her collect the unfortunate and forgotten, and they defend her interests—and those of Grimnir. Yet, despite this semblance of civility, the undead are still frightening and the raiders avoid them. The walking dead are something they simply don’t understand and some fear that one day the undead will rise to help consume the world. This is a worrying concept and one that is often on the mind of any raider who travels near territory haunted by the once-living. That is not to say that undead are never vile, never evil. There are many examples of the dead disregarding Hel’s mercy and love. These tend to cause the same sorts of disruptions that one might expect in more typical worlds. Pretty much any undead that might be found elsewhere can be found in Grimnir and Hel does not control them—even within the Underworld they have free will. And some decide to be terrible. In addition to standard undead, the world of Grimnir has spawned several unique flavors, from the cursed knight to the pyrewights. Many of these are created because of the breaking or bending of an oath, or because a duty was not honored. Undead Nature. Like undead in other realms, these undead do not require air, food, drink, or sleep. Myling The phantasmal incarnations of the souls of children, mylings are forced to roam the earth until they can convince (or force) someone to properly bury their body. They have not been taken to the Underworld simply because they somehow evaded Hel’s notice. Unwanted Companions. Mylings tend to avoid violence when possible. Instead, they climb onto a sleeping humanoid and attach themselves with their Clutch ability. They do not use their Bite attack unless they are being attacked. If an aggressor ceases attacking, the myling will respond in kind—they simply want a proper burial. Unwanted Quest. Mylings guide their victims to a graveyard so that they might be buried properly. Once they reach the location, the myling detaches itself and watches, waiting for its victim to grant its final request.
