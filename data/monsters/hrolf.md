# Hrolf

**description**: Hrolf In many ways, Hrolf is the idealized raider—he is charming, handsome (if scruffy looking), and daring. While his half-elf features mark him different from many raiders, he is never aloof. He generally wears his light brown hair long and like many raiders grows a respectable beard. Hrolf belongs to the Hallharn raiding clan, but his exploits have been such that he is well respected by all raiders. He is good natured, a capable warrior, and a natural leader. Over his long career sailing the Serpent Sea he has collected numerous stories so a journey across land and water with him is never lacking in boastful tales of great deeds and glory earned. Many years ago, Hrolf offered his own eye to the Wisdom Well, hoping to gain some awareness of what the future would offer. Whether it worked or not, he has never said. Roleplaying Hrolf Celebratory. Hrolf is seldom without a curved horn of mead to share with his companions. He loves telling stories—and hearing new tales—and believes this is best done around a roaring fire with strong mead and interesting companions. Self-important. Though Hrolf seldom speaks of this, he does believe that he is important, perhaps even vital, to preventing Ragnarök. This is not an unusual belief among raiders, but for Hrolf it is of paramount importance. Even if rejected by the heroes, he still attempts to accompany them, though from afar. Vengeful. Though open-minded, regarding raider unity, the attack by the Whar at the beginning of the story sets him on a determined path to eradicate the Whar clan, which may complicate the heroes’ attempts to unify the clans.
