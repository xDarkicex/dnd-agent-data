# Hooktongue

**description**: The legendary monster of Hooktongue Lake is no myth. While you can use these statistics to represent a standard lake monster, they work best in the role of a legendary creature the PCs have heard rumors about for many sessions before finally facing it in combat. Traits Amphibious. Hooktongue can breathe air and water. Inexorable. Hooktongue ignores difficult terrain. Actions Multiattack. Hooktongue makes two attacks, one with its jaws and one with its tongue. Jaws. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 31 (4d12+5) piercing damage and the target is grappled (escape DC 18). Until the grapple ends, the target is restrained and has disadvantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws, and Hooktongue can’t use a jaws attack on another target. Tongue. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 27 (4d10+5) piercing damage plus 14 (4d6) poison damage. Venom Spray (Recharge 5–6). Hooktongue sprays venom from his throat in an 80-foot line. Each creature in that area must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw, taking 45 (10d8) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Swallow. Hooktongue makes one jaws attack against a Medium or smaller target it is grappling. If the attack hits, the target is swallowed, and the grapple ends. While swallowed, the target is blinded and restrained, it has total cover against attacks and other effects outside Hooktongue, and it takes 27 (6d8) acid damage at the start of each of Hooktongue’s turns. Hooktongue can have only one creature swallowed at a time. If Hooktongue takes 30 damage or more on a single turn from the swallowed creature, Hooktongue must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw at the end of that turn or regurgitate the creature, which falls prone in a space within 10 feet of Hooktongue. If Hooktongue dies, a swallowed creature is no longer restrained by it and can escape from the corpse by using 15 feet of movement, exiting prone.
