# Vicious Army Ant Swarm

**description**: Army Ant Swarm Army ant swarms are already much feared for their capacity to ravenously consume anything that lies in their path, but even they pale in comparison to particularly vicious army ant swarms. These swarms are often found in desolate tombs, sinister dungeons, or other underground places steeped in dangerous and unsettling traditions. Traits Swarm. The swarm can occupy a creature’s space and vice versa, and the swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Small ant. The swarm can’t regain hit points or gain temporary hit points. Actions Multiattack. The swarm can attack every creature in its space with swarming bites. Swarming Bites. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target in the swarm’s space. Hit: 14 (4d6) piercing damage, or 7 (2d6) if the swarm has half of its hit points or lower, and the target must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 7 (2d6) poison damage and is poisoned until the start of the swarm’s next turn. On a success, the target takes half as much poison damage and is not poisoned. Reactions Cling. When a creature leaves the swarm’s space, the army ant swarm can use its reaction to cling to that creature. If it does so, the amount of the swarm that was in the target’s space moves with the creature, splitting the swarm if the creature is Medium or smaller. Each part of the swarm acts on the same initiative. The clinging swarm has one-quarter of the swarm’s remaining hit points, removing those hit points from the swarm’s current total. Any separated portion of the swarm can continue to make swarming bite attacks. A creature can use its action to brush the ants off with a contested grapple check. If underwater or in high winds, the creature has advantage on its ability check. With a success, the creature removes itself from the swarm’s area, and the remaining ants return to the main swarm without provoking an attack of opportunity.
