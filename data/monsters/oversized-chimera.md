# Oversized Chimera

**description**: Chimeras are common beasts in the Stolen Lands, particularly in the western hills and badlands, where they can grow particularly muscular and deadly. While not large enough to be classified as a Huge creature, none would mistake an oversized chimera for a typical specimen, as it stands nearly 8 feet tall at the shoulder and is nearly 15 feet long. Traits Three Heads. The chimera has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks and on saving throws against being blinded, charmed, deafened, frightened, stunned, and knocked unconscious. Any ability that would sever a chimera’s head (such as a critical hit with a vorpal weapon) severs one head at random. Losing a head doesn’t kill the chimera (as long as it has at least one head left), but it does prevent it from making attacks with the lost head (reducing its overall number of attacks). If the chimera loses the dragon head, it loses the Dragon Breath ability. Triple Opportunity. The chimera gains 2 extra reactions each round that it can use only to make attacks of opportunity. It must use a different head for each reaction, and it can’t use more than one on the same triggering action. If it loses one of its heads, it also loses the corresponding extra reaction. Actions Multiattack. The chimera makes four attacks, one with each head and one with its claws. Dragon Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (3d6+5) piercing damage plus 10 (3d6) damage based on the color of the dragon head: fire for red, poison for green, cold for white, lightning for blue, and acid for black. Lion Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (3d8+5) piercing damage and the target must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or be grappled. Goat Horns. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d8+5) bludgeoning damage and the target must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone. Claws. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (3d6+5) slashing damage. Dragon Breath (Recharge 5–6). The chimera breathes a 60-foot cone or line from its dragon head. All creatures in that area must make a DC 17 Dexterity save, taking 45 (13d6) damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful save. The color of the chimera’s dragon head determines the shape of the Dragon Breath and the type of damage it deals: fire cone for red, poison cone for green, cold cone for white, lightning line for blue, and acid line for black.
