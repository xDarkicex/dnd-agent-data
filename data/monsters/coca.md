# Coca

**description**: Coca Cocas are bodiless entities. Similar to hermit crabs, they take possession of a receptacle (of natural origin) and transform it into their body or home, often a simple coconut shell. Once occupied by the coca’s spirit, the occupied container subtly shifts its appearance to resemble a sort of head, to which it adds limbs made of earth or plants, animating the whole by the power of its spirit. Cocas tend to hide in the shadows, lurking about seeking those they may snare and devour. Their fell reputation is invoked by elders to caution misbehaving youngsters with stories of the creature, telling them, “If you don't behave well, the coca will devour you!” During autumn harvest festivals, creatures often place gourds, pumpkins, and even severed heads near crossroads and at the entrances to villages to placate a coca known to live nearby, giving it an easy place to inhabit in the hopes it will not vent its bile on the locals. Those who fear a cocas wrath struggle to keep all hostile thoughts from their minds, as anger and hatred attract its attention as it imbibes the thoughts of those around it and unleashes warped visions of their hidden thoughts upon them in a wave of terror. Murucututu . Not all cocas are inherently evil. Some are more like creepy guardian angels, guarding pathways and punishing those with ill intentions. These spirits, called murucututu, are skilled sentinels and mostly benevolent and may take the form of large owls with dark, shadowy wings, perched along ridgelines and at wayside markers at crossroads.
