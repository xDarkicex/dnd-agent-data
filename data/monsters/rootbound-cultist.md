# Rootbound Cultist

**description**: Rootbound The cultists become bound to a wooden object—typically a living tree, but it’s also possible to be bound to the wooden structure of a building. These cultists can’t venture more than a few miles from the object they are bound to. They use the statistics of dryads with the following changes: Their creature type is both Plant and Aberration. They speak Deep Speech and the languages they knew in life. They can’t cast druidcraft or shillelagh , but they know the primal savagery and acid splash cantrips (spitting acid from their mouth). Their Fey Charm action doesn’t affect Beasts, and they can charm one Humanoid and up to three Plants or Aberrations at a time. Traits Magic Resistance. The cultist has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Speak with Beasts and Plants. The cultist can communicate with beasts and plants as if they shared a language. Tree Stride. Once on her turn, the cultist can use 10 ft. of her movement to step magically into one living tree within her reach and emerge from a second living tree within 60 ft. of the first tree, appearing in an unoccupied space within 5 ft. of the second tree. Both trees must be large or bigger. Innate Spellcasting. The cultist's innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 14). The cultist can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: primal savagery, acid splash 3/day each: entangle, goodberry 1/day each: barkskin, pass without trace Actions Club. Melee Weapon Attack: +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: (1d4) bludgeoning damage. Fey Charm . The cultist targets one humanoid, plant, or aberration that she can see within 30 feet of her. If the target can see the cultist, it must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or be magically charmed. The charmed creature regards the cultist as a trusted friend to be heeded and protected. Although the target isn't under the cultist's control, it takes the cultist's requests or actions in the most favorable way it can. Each time the cultist or its allies do anything harmful to the target, it can repeat the saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success. Otherwise, the effect lasts 24 hours or until the cultist dies, is on a different plane of existence from the target, or ends the effect as a bonus action. If a target's saving throw is successful, the target is immune to the cultist's Fey Charm for the next 24 hours. The cultist can have no more than one humanoid and up to three plants or aberrations charmed at a time.
