# Golan

**description**: Appearance and Personality: Golan has pale, ghostly skin and a bright red beard. He has gone almost completely bald even at his young age (for a dwarf). When the PCs first encounter Golan, he is in the midst of a desperate battle. His body and gear show the damage from the recent battle. He is, however, determined and surprisingly upbeat. He believes that the PCs’ assistance is a sign that their fortune is changing, and he embraces the notion for the good of Pardrum. Golan is excited to hear any news of the outside world, and relentlessly and determinedly tries to find the positive in it. He is shockingly optimistic, especially for a dwarf. Partly because of this, his enthusiasm and determination seems to be infectious. He honestly believes that the war is not yet lost, and seems to feel that he and his companions can make a difference to improve everyone’s lot. Traits Dwarven Resilience. Golan has advantage on saving throws against poison. Bonus Actions Indomitable Spirit (1/Day). As a bonus action, Golan can bolster injured creatures. Each creature of Golan’s choice that can hear him within 30 feet of him regains 1d6 + 3 hit points if it has no more than half of its hit points remaining. Actions Multiattack. Golan makes two attacks with his warhammer. Warhammer. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft, one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 2) bludgeoning damage. Spellcasting. Golan casts one of the following spells, using Charisma as his spellcasting ability (spell save DC 14): 2/day each: bless, cure wounds, heroism 1/day each: aid, lesser restoration.
