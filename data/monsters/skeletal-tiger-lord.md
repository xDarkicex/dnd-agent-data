# Skeletal Tiger Lord

**description**: Skeleton Undead skeletons are a well-known but relatively minor threat to those who would explore ancient tombs, but in the Stolen Lands, more powerful variants of these monsters lie in wait. Some of them even retain shreds of their personalities in life, and are capable of much more complicated tasks than the typical necromantic monster most assume they face when a frame of clattering bones rises up to oppose them. Skeletal Tiger Lord These blood-dripping skeletal champions were raised from the bones of Tiger Lords, and while they’ve lost their ability to benefit from a barbarian’s rage, they remain quite dangerous using their bows, blades, and aptitude for slaughter. Traits Regeneration. The skeletal Tiger Lord regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn. If the skeletal Tiger Lord takes radiant damage, this trait doesn’t function at the start of the skeletal tiger lord’s next turn. The skeletal Tiger Lord only dies if it starts its turn with 0 hit points and doesn’t regenerate. Rejuvenation. If the skeletal Tiger Lord is destroyed, it regains all of its hit points in one hour unless the runes in Armag’s tomb have been destroyed. If the skeletal Tiger Lord was destroyed by radiant damage, this trait does not function. Actions Multiattack. The skeletal Tiger Lord makes three melee attacks or two ranged attacks. Greatsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (2d6+2) slashing damage. Longbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, range 150/600, one target. Hit: 7 piercing (1d8+3).
