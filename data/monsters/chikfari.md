# Chikfari

**description**: Created by dwarves deep in the Underworld as a beast of burden, chikfari quickly proved to be a species as malleable as iron to a skilled breeder. Now chikfari serve the dwarves not only as beasts of burden, agriculture, and travel, but as mounts of war, guard “dogs,” and even the rare pet. While they are for sale to travelers and traders, a chikfari rarely, if ever, stays with a non-dwarf owner for more than a few weeks. Many non-dwarves suspect this behavior is somehow bred into the beasts, giving rise to the Underworld saying, “As loyal as a chikfari” to mean one who can be trusted, but only for a short while. Chikfari that find cause to move on from their current masters invariably seek to make their way back to a deep dwarf settlement. Domesticated. Chikfari are a thoroughly domesticated species. When encountered in the wild, the insect surely distressed and struggling to survive, but often it’s only the gentle, calloused hand of a grey dwarf that can rehabilitate a displaced chikfari. Those insects the grey dwarves breed are something else. They herd like sheep, they guard like dogs, they jump like a hare, they ride like camels, and they kick like a mule. —Myris Firebrand, dweorg merchant
