# The Dream-Devouring Demon, Nocturne (Moth Yokai)

**description**: It/They/Them The yōkai, a Dream-Devouring Demon called Nocturne, has a husked carapace and hairy wings featuring many functional eyes. It prefers the cover of night but will venture out during the day if needed. If seen in full light, its human-skull-like head appears covered with needle-thin spikes matched to the teeth in its maw. Its claw-tipped arms extend from the bottom of its torso with the upper torso controlling the ever-watching wings. The carapace covers the whole front of the body and even seems to be integrated into the wings as a form of protection. Traits Silent Flight. Nocturne’s wings produce no sound from flying. Other creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to detect it via hearing. Sunlight Sensitivity. While in sunlight, Nocturne has disadvantage on attack rolls, as well as on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight. Dim-light Invisibility. When Nocturne begins its turn in dim light or darkness, it becomes invisible. Nocturne remains invisible until it performs an attack or moves. Actions Multiattack. Nocturne makes two attacks: one with its bite and one with either its claw or grab. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (1d10 + 4) piercing damage plus 3 (1d6) poison damage. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d6 + 4) slashing damage plus 3 (1d6) poison damage. Grab. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one Medium or smaller creature. Hit: the target is grappled (escape DC 13). Until the grapple ends, Nocturne's flying speed is halved. Bewildering Presence. Eye markings on each wing begin to shift in hypnotic and chaotic patterns. One creature Nocturne can see within 30 feet of it must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or becomes bewildered for 1 minute. A bewildered creature rolls 1d10 at the start of each of its turns, and acts according to the results below before taking the rest of its turn: 1: The creature uses 20 feet of its movement to move in a direction of the GM’s choice. It does not provoke opportunity attacks as part of this movement. 2–6: As their bonus action, the creature hurls one weapon or item of the GM’s choice that it is holding in a direction of the GM’s choice. The item flies a number of feet in that direction equal to 2d10 + its Strength modifier. 7–8: As their bonus action, the creature must make an unarmed attack against one creature of the GM’s choice within 5 feet. 9–10: The creature can act and move normally, but has disadvantage on attack rolls. A bewildered creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. A creature that saves against the effect is immune to Bewildering Presence for the next 24 hours.
