# Grig

**description**: Sprite Elusive, flighty, and ebullient, sprites are what many first imagine when they hear the terms “fey” and “fairy.” While there are a wide range of sprites, the largest of them rarely match the size of even a small child—and the vast majority of these wee fey are much smaller. Grig Grigs are kindly musicians of the fey, often getting themselves into trouble due to their penchant for confronting evil well beyond their ability to vanquish. Even so, they fight bravely and with great cunning, using their magic and ranged sonic attacks while flying and leaping using their wings and powerful cricket-like lower torsos to stay out of reach. The grig’s upper torsos appear humanoid, but their ears are long and pointed and they have a pair of gossamer dragonfly-like wings on their backs. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The grig’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 12). The grig can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: druidcraft, minor illusion 3/day each: disguise self, entangle, invisibility (self only) Leaper. The grig has advantage on Dexterity (Acrobatics)checks. Actions Shortsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 3 (1d3+2) piercing damage. Shortbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, 40/160 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d4+2) piercing damage. Fiddle. The grig rubs its legs together, creating a surprisingly pleasant tune that sounds like it’s played on a fiddle. Creatures within 20 feet of the grig must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw or be compelled to dance in place. A dancing creature must use all its movement to dance without leaving its space and has disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws and attack rolls. While the target is affected by this spell, other creatures have advantage on attack rolls against it. As an action, a dancing creature makes a Wisdom saving throw to regain control of itself. On a successful save, the effect ends. Creatures that can’t be charmed are immune to this effect.
