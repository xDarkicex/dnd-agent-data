# Swarm of Ochre Droplings

**description**: Dropling Within the field of oozology, it’s a generally accepted fact that oozes require a critical mass to retain functionality. That’s why the discovery of droplings—tiny blobs each about the size of a fist, spawned from larger oozes—made massive ripples in the Slime Times newspaper. No one is quite sure of the circumstances or reasons that lead to their creation; experiments on a large variety of oozes have reproduced dropling spawning with only a twenty-three percent success rate. Pseudogelphobic Swarm. Perhaps the most peculiar trait of droplings is their tendency to form swarms instead of combining to form their progenitor oozes. Respected oozologists hypothesise droplings that retain their size for an extended period of time undergo changes to their surface membrane compositions that inhibit their ability to merge with other droplings, all while maintaining the pseudo-hivemind need for consolidation. Amateur oozologists, in much fewer words, like to think they’re becoming individuals. Ochre Droplings This strain of droplings originates from ochre jellies. Ochre droplings are similar to their black dropling cousins in many ways, but lack their highly acidic composition, a trait that makes them desirable jar pets.
