# Spell Savant

**description**: Masters of the arcane, most often located in grand places of learning and acting as teachers for aspiring mages. They often share a passion for education, as well as an immense talent for the manipulation of magic. These gifted spellcasters often belong to institutions of learning within the most cultured metropolises in the land. At times they serve as advisors to kings or even found their own schools in areas where wealth is not abundant. Humanoids Humanoids are the most plentiful creatures amongst the creations of the gods. They create vast kingdoms, they make unbelievable technological advances and even achieve breakthroughs in the art of magic. Traits Arcane Absorption. Whenever a spell is snuffed out by counterspell or a magical effect is removed by dispel magic , the savant recharges their arcane deflection . Persuasive Knowledge. The savant has a bonus to their Charisma (Persuasion) checks equal to their Intelligence modifier. Spellcasting. The battle-mage's Spellcasting Ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 17, +9 to hit with spell attacks). It can cast the following spells: At Will: mage armor, mage hand, minor illusion, shield 3/day each: counterspell, dispel magic, misty step 2/day each: enhance ability, fireball, fly, invisibility, lightning bolt 1/day each: banishment, cone of cold, polymorph, telekinesis Rituals: comprehend languages, find familiar, identify, leomond's tiny hut, rary's telepathic bond Unrivaled Concentration. The savant can concentrate on two spells at once. However, if they are hit while concentrating on two spells all concentration checks are done at disadvantage. If concentration is lost, both spells cease to function. Actions Arcane Burst (Melee). Melee Spell Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d10 + 5) force damage. Arcane Burst (Ranged). Ranged Spell Attack: +9 to hit, reach 120 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (3d10 + 5) force damage. Reactions Arcane Deflection (Recharge 5-6). Whenever a creature casts a spell with a single target that the savant can see, they can use their reaction to choose a new target for the spell. The possible targets can include the original spellcaster or the savant themselves.
