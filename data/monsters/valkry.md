# Valkry

**description**: Valkry The valkry are unusual Celestial beings that apparently manifested at the moment of Exodus, but not on Grimnir like other primal creatures. Instead, they arose only on Grimnir’s moon, Valholl. Their histories are tied up with those of Thonir though the valkry apparently predate his arrival. Given the nature of time itself in Valholl, it is a web near impossible to disentangle. The valkry most raiders are familiar with are those that live in Thonir’s halls, yet there is a large community of them that live on a island far to the south of the Halls of the Slain. These valkry know little about the raiders or any other denizens of Grimnir, though are curious to meet newcomers. Carriers of the Dead. Thonir’s valkry watch for conflict across Grimnir. Like sharks scenting blood, they fly to those conflicts and observe—those among the dead that are most skilled are then taken to Valholl so they might feast at the Halls of the Slain. Touched by the Celestial. Valkry are technically Celestials, yet they do age, though their lifespans are measured in centuries. As they age, they begin to diminish, showing similar signs of aging as one might expect from a mortal. But no one has actually seen a valkry die of old age—they just become old and then they vanish. Where they go when they die is a mystery that no one can answer. Wardens of Ragnarök. What is known, is that the valkry have an inborn sense of dread—that a great evil ever-so-slowly approaches. The valkry that live far from Thonir’s hall also share this dread, but they do not understand it, nor can they fathom how they might do anything about it. However, those valkry that assist Thonir believe they must prepare Valholl’s army of the living dead to fight alongside the mortals when that day comes. The valkry are skilled in a variety of different fighting styles and so the warriors of Valholl are well served to have such astute and capable teachers. Variant: Shieldmaiden This valkry has 120 hit points but does not have the Multiattack, Spear, Spear Throw, or Strike of Vengeance features. Instead she wields a +2 shield (AC 20) and while she uses the Intercept ability, she grants a +2 bonus to the AC of the target she is defending. She also gains Shield Throw. Ranged Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, range 20/60 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d10 + 3) magical bludgeoning damage. At the end of her turn the shield teleports back into the shieldmaiden’s hand but while it is gone, she loses the +2 bonus to her AC.
