# Cleansed Cultist

**description**: Cultist Just as bandits are drawn to the Stolen Lands, all manner of cultists find this region ripe for hideouts and hidden shrines to deities and demigods whose worship has been outlawed in most cities and towns. Some of these cultists are solitary members of groups with influence throughout the world, while others, such as the Cultists of the Bloom, are entirely regional threats. Note that these cultists, along with the more powerful specialized cultists presented later in this book under the entry for Thresholders, can be used together as different agents of a sprawling sect of sinister worshippers in your game simply by changing their descriptions to match whatever nefarious deity or evil faith you’ve decided they might serve. Cleansed Cultist The cultists of the Kingdom of the Cleansed seek to build a new society from the bones of the old. They excel at recruiting newcomers to their faith with promises of transcendence. That they worship the demon lord Yhidothrus is a secret known only to the higher ranking members and their half-fiend leader, a mysterious figure known to them only as the First Faithful. These cultists have all survived a dangerous ritual that grants them the power to release the “inmost worm,” a mental projection of their faith. Those who fail this ritual become bodaks instead. Traits Dark Devotion. The cleansed cultist has advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. Sneak Attack. Once per turn, the cleansed cultist deals an extra 7 (2d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the cleansed cultist that isn’t incapacitated and the cleansed cultist doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Actions Multiattack. The cleansed cultist makes three attacks: two with its shortsword and one with its dagger. Shortsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6+3) piercing damage. Dagger. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d4+2) piercing damage. Release the Inmost Worm (Recharge 5–6). The cleansed cultist spends an action to expel a bloody worm from the scar on its forehead, targeting a creature within 30 feet. The creature must succeed at a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or the worm burrows into the creature’s head, dealing 21 (6d6) psychic damage and incapacitating the target until the beginning of the cleansed cultist’s next turn. On a successful save, the worm deals half damage and falls to the ground, vanishing, and the target is not incapacitated.
