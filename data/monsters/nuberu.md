# Nuberu

**description**: The nuberu represents two sides of the same coin, being both wicked and evil as well as benevolent and helpful. Believed by some to be demigods—or even a single unique demigod—nuberus represents the danger and raw power of storms, rain, and thunder, and each appears as a giant with wild hair, a long beard, wears goat leathers and a big hat, and carries an axe. Sometimes they are seen wearing dark pelts and traveling in a wolf-pulled chariot. Nuberus often are blamed for destroying crops and homes. Their appearance is also associated with dark skies, shipwrecks, flooding, and damaging lightning strikes. Despite all the harm that is credited to nuberus, he is often called upon by farmers and shepherds to bring rain to their crops and pastures, especially during times of drought. This duality of the nuberu’s nature makes sense to those who are affected by their actions. They know the nuberu to be unpredictable, bringing both large disasters and great prosperity, depending on his moods. Because of these wild mood swings, wise folk choose carefully whether to approach the nuberu for help, as angering him is likely to bring destruction down on everyone. Close to Nature. The nuberu is believed to make his home in the mountains and is sometimes depicted as a hermit or a shepherd. He is known to have a fondness for smoking a pipe, and often enjoys a stiff drink. In many stories from the cultures familiar with him, he is portrayed as a wise old man who can provide guidance and advice to those who seek it. Also associated with the natural world, he is said to have a deep connection to the animals and plants of the oceans, forests, and mountains. Amused by Destruction. Despite the often positive things he does for people, Nuberu is known for his explosive fits. Sometimes he destroys a village for simple amusement. When he is shown a lack of respect, he can become quite vengeful. Some light candles and ring bells during terrible storms beause they believe it will scare him away. Arrived on a Cloud. The nuberu did not always live in the lands where he is now known. It is said he came riding a cloud. Due to extreme bad luck, he fell and landed on the ground, at which point he appealed to the locals for food and shelter. At first, he was turned away, but eventually a peasant offered aid. As a reward, Nuberu brought water to his fields which led to bountiful harvests. It is said that some years later, the peasants wife was planning to marry another man because he was believed dead after he had been absent for many years. He approached Nuberu for help, and they rode a cloud back to his wife where they were able to prevent the wedding.
