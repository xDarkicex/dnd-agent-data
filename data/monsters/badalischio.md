# Badalischio

**description**: Basilisk, Badalischio Man-sized serpents crowned with horn-like projections circling their heads, a badalischio’s eyes are lit from within with a red glow. These adaptable predators can attack from the land, water, or air, as their preference dictates. Badalischios are commonly found in forested regions near large bodies of fresh water. They can be found lairing in caves on hillsides, hollow trees, or in the beds of lakes or rivers. A typical badalischio is between 7 and 9 feet in length, with a wingspan of about 14 feet. Their scales have a reticulated pattern of green, brown, and gold. Attracted to Luster . Badalischio are attracted to things that glitter and shine, often decorating their lairs with treasure. With no acumen for telling the value of objects, this “treasure” may simply be glass or cheap metals as often as things of actual value. However, the badalischio has enough intelligence to understand that other creatures value such things, and often uses them as lures to attract prey.
