# Swarm of Metal Spiders

**description**: Content Warning: Torture Chain spiders are created from the corpses of giants and humanoids, returning them to a semblance of life (so similar that they are not even classified as undead by most scholars), though without an intact mind or soul. An incision is made above the corpse’s left orbital, and a chunk of the prefrontal cortex is removed using an iron or steel tube. The piece of brain is infused into a piece of metal through a dark ritual, and is then bound to the skull on the exterior, forming a small metal horn near where the incision was made. Horrifying Transformation. Over the next few days, the creature’s body undergoes painful and horrifying changes as shards of metal sprout and grow throughout its bone and muscle. By the end of the transformation, the creature becomes capable of harnessing its pain and suffering into supernatural abilities. It gains the ability to animate chains by transforming pieces of the chain into tiny metal spiders, and can move along them like a 4-limbed spider on a web. Mindless Intelligence. Although chain spiders lack the mind of the creature they once were, the rituals used to create them do give them enough intelligence and mental capacity to serve their creators and even communicate with them if needed. No semblance of the body’s original mind remains, though occasionally pieces of old memories and information slip through. Tactics. Though each chain spider's original abilities are unique, their tactics with their newly developed abilities are to begin by using Animate Chain in order to gain an ally in their fight, after which they will attempt to bind a foe with their Chain Web before using Inflict Pain. If there are no chains around, they first use Chain Web, animating those chains only after the target has broken free. Traits Swarm. The swarm can occupy another creature's space and vice versa, and the swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny spider. The swarm can't regain hit points or gain temporary hit points. Spider Climb. The swarm can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check. Web Sense. While in contact with a web or chain, the swarm knows the exact location of any other creature in contact with the same web or chain. Web Walker. The swarm ignores movement restrictions caused by webbing. Actions Bites (swarm has more than half its hit points). Melee Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target in the swarm's space. Hit: 10 (4d4) piercing damage. Bites (swarm has half its hit points or less). Melee Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target in the swarm's space. Hit: 5 (2d4) piercing damage.
