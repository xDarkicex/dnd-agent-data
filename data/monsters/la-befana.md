# La Befana

**description**: One of the most fabled and powerful denizens of the First World was born a human (nobody knows on which world). Some legends say she’s the meeker sister of Baba Yaga, always trying to remedy her evil doings. Other talk about a long travel of penitence that an ancient witch had to undertake to atone for her sins or search for her lost child. She was supposedly burned at the stake more than once, always escaping thanks to her fire resistance. At last she found a gate for the First World, and her long stay there granted her faerie powers. La Befana mostly resides in her own demiplane, where time flows very erratically and can even go backwards. She cares deeply for mortals, and for children in particular. Both loved and feared, in long winter nights near the solstice she is able to travel immense distances (maybe going back and forth between her demiplane and more than one world on the Material Plane), entering people’s houses to reward the worthy and admonish the unworthy. La Befana’s judgment is unfailing: nice children receive gifts and socks full of sweets, naughty children only receive charcoal. Though she can fly for herself if needed (without any wings), she prefers her bonded artifact broom. "She comes at night with her broken shoes. All hail! She comes with her ragged blouse! She leaves on the mantelpiece gifts in the socks, Then goes up the chimney after making a bow!" - Ancient folk rhyme
