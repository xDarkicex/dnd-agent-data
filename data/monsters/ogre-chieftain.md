# Ogre Chieftain

**description**: The ogre chieftain is the largest and most intimidating ogre in its tribe. While it sees itself as a commander and tactician in battle, it is not a particularly good one as it merely bullies its troops into action. Traits Legendary Resistance (1/Day). If the ogre fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead. Actions Multiattack. The ogre makes two melee attacks. Spiked Greatclub. Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d8+ 5) bludgeoning damage plus 4 (1d8) piercing damage. Javelin. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage. Skull Crush (Recharge 5–6). Melee Weapon Attack: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 14 (2d8+ 5) bludgeoning damage plus 4 (1d8) piercing damage, and if the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 16 Constitution saving throw or be knocked unconscious for 1 minute. The unconscious target repeats the saving throw if it takes damage and at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. Legendary Actions The ogre can take three legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The ogre regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Move. The ogre moves up to half its speed. Club (Costs 2 Actions). The ogre makes a Spiked Greatclub attack. Bark Command (Costs 3 Actions). The ogre targets up to two allies it can see within 30 feet of it. If the target can see and hear the ogre, the target can use its reaction to make one weapon attack with disadvantage on the attack roll.
