# Conch Tree

**description**: Several stalks of thick seaweed rise from the ocean floor, with large round stones decorating the silt nearby. Cadaverous Reproduction. Conch trees reproduce by implanting a single seed in a partially digested corpse. The corpse is then expelled so the seed can absorb nutrients before sprouting and taking root. Conch trees never stop growing, but an average adult specimen is 20 feet tall. Sea Treants. Conch trees are frequently cultivated as living barriers, unsporting hedges against unwanted incursions. After centuries of breeding and experimentation, they developed an instinct for discerning regular travelers among their groves from visiting prey.
