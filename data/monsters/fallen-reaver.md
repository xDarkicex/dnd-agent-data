# Fallen Reaver

**description**: Traits Reckless. The reaver can choose to gain advantage on its attack rolls until the end of its turn. If it does, attack rolls against it have advantage until the start of its next turn. Join the Fallen. Whenever the reaver reduces a creature to 0 hit points, inflicts an Injury, or scores a critical hit, all creatures within 120 feet that can hear or see the target of its attack must succeed on a DC 8 Wisdom saving throw or gain a Mindscar. A creature who gains three Mindscars begins to lose its mind. During its next long rest, it will begin to scar its flesh with whatever tools are available to it. Once its rest is completed, it becomes one of the Fallen. Mindscars can be removed by any process that can remove a curse, although mental trauma will still remain. If the mental trauma is overcome, the Mindscars are removed as well. Relish the Pain. Whenever the reaver suffers an Injury, they gain a +1 bonus to damage rolls until the Injury is removed (up to their proficiency bonus), and gain temporary hit points equal to ½ the threshold of the Injury gained. The reaver never has damage loss regardless of its hit point tier for Injury thresholds, and always suffers an Injury when it takes enough damage to become Injured (the Injury it gains is chosen by the damage dealer). Actions Multiattack. The reaver makes two Reaving Swing attacks. Reaving Swing. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, one target, reach 5 feet. Hit: 10 (1d12+3) slashing damage, and another enemy within 5 feet of the reaver must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 7 (2d6) slashing damage. Finisher (recharge 6). Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, one Injured target, reach 5 feet. Hit: 29 (4d12+3) slashing damage, and the target must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
