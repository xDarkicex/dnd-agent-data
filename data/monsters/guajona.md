# Guajona

**description**: This nocturnal creature is often mistaken for a hag or vampire, but related to neither. A thin, humanoid figure with wrinkled, yellow skin, the guajona is a hematophage, feeding on the blood of living humanoids. While humanoid in form, several features set a guajona apart. Her feet are bird-like and sport deadly talons. Instead of hands, strands of thorny brambles protrude from her wrists, twining together into an approximation of fingers. The eyes of a guajona shine with an inner light, and her most prominent facial feature is a single long, curving fang that descends down over the lower lip, coming to a point near the chin. Creature of the Night. A guajona spends the daylight hours burrowed under the ground or in a convenient cave or abandoned building. Exposure to sunlight immediately begins to burn the guajona’s skin, and prolonged exposure causes death. At night, the guajona comes forth to hunt. Anesthetic Spittle. The guajona’s saliva is a powerful anesthetic, deadening sensation upon contact. When hunting, the guajona typically picks a sleeping victim, anesthetizing an area on their body before biting in with its tooth and draining blood from the victim. The guajona places the bite in a place not easily seen, to hide evidence of its attack. Often the victim is assumed to be ill, becoming more tired and drained as the guajona feeds on consecutive nights, until the victim dies.
