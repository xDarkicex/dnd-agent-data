# Lac’Kra’Tah

**description**: Lac’Kra’Tah burrow deep into heavy snow, or even frozen lakes, in order to meet their need for an environment that is “solid liquid” or else they begin to deteriorate mentally and physically. Once a suitable den has been found, the lac’kra’tah lowers itself into a cryogenic suspension, allowing it to adopt the outside temperature so its form is indistinguishable from its surrounds. Then, just before it ceases most functions temporarily, it raises the tips of its feelers and tentacles as close as it can to the surface, across as wide an area as possible, and waits for prey to appear. As something walks past the tentacles, the lac’kra’tah begins to stir; but the time it crosses the feelers, the beast is fully awake and rises to attack. Of course, it make take an age for prey to wander into the lac’kra’tah’s reach, but these creatures have been known to lie near-dormant for decades. In fact the older they get, the longer they can enter this closed-down state. It is suggested by those who have studied the lac’kra’tah that the largest, most ancient examples may not move for 1,000 years or more, so long as they remain below the surface. Indeed, settlements may come and go for generations and be just out of reach of a lac’kra’tah, never knowing such an aberration is almost within striking distance.
