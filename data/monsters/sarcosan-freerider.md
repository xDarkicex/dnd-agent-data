# Sarcosan Freerider

**description**: Appearance and Personality: The freeriders have sun-­worn complexions, and their clothing is covered in dust, dirt, and sweat stains from their unending time in the saddle. They consider their mounts family and are visibly confused in the face of people journeying across the plains afoot. They are initially wary of outsiders but are generous hosts in the presence of those they consider to be allies. While they are all proven warriors, they remain enthusiastic for opportunities to test their mettle against new foes. Traits Born in the Saddle. The freerider has advantage on saving throws made to avoid falling off their mount. If they fall off your mount and descend no more than 10 feet, they can land on their feet if they’re not incapacitated. Finally, mounting or dismounting a creature costs a freerider only 5 feet of movement, rather than half their speed. Skilled Rider. The freerider can force an attack targeted at their mount to target themself instead. In addition, if the freerider’s mount is subjected to an effect that allows it to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, it instead takes no damage if it succeeds on the saving throw, and only half damage if it fails. Actions Lance (mounted). Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 10ft., one target. Hit: 9 (1d12 + 2) piercing damage. A freerider has disadvantage when using a lance to attack a target within 5 feet of them. Cedeku. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 2) slashing damage. Short bow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage.
