# Elder Xorn

**description**: TRAITS Earth Glide: The xorn can burrow through nonmagical, unworked earth and stone. While doing so, the xorn doesn’t disturb the material it moves through. Stone Camouflage: The xorn has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in rocky terrain. Treasure Sense: The xorn can pinpoint, by scent, the location of precious metals and stones, such as coins and gems, within 90 feet of it. ACTIONS Multiattack: The xorn makes three claw attacks and one bite attack. Claw: Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (1d8 + 5) slashing damage. Bite: Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 18 (3d8 + 5) piercing damage. LEGENDARY ACTIONS The xorn can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature’s turn. The xorn regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Claw Attack: The elder xorn makes a claw attack. Earth Glide: The elder xorn burrows up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks. Elemental Stoneskin (Costs 2 Actions): For 1 minute or until its concentration ends (as if concentrating on a spell), the xorn is immune to bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage that is nonmagical. LAIR ACTIONS While in its lair, the xorn can invoke the ambient magic to take lair actions. On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the xorn can take a lair action to cause one of the following effects: Rockslide: A 20-foot-square section of ceiling that the xorn can see within 60 feet of it collapses. The ground directly beneath the ceiling becomes difficult terrain. Creatures in that area must make a successful DC 17 Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, the creature takes 11 (2d10) bludgeoning damage, is knocked prone, and is restrained. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn’t knocked prone or restrained. A creature can use an action to make a DC 17 Strength check, freeing a restrained creature on a success. Rocksong: The xorn causes the rocks in its lair to vibrate. Each creature within 60 feet of the xorn that can hear its Rocksong takes 9 (2d8) thunder damage and must make a successful DC 17 Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of its next turn. Rock to Mud: The xorn chooses a 20-foot-square section of floor that it can see within 60 feet of it, magically transforming it to mud. Creatures in that area must make a successful DC 17 Strength check or be grappled (escape DC 17) as they sink into the mud. While grappled, the target is also restrained. Movement through the mud costs 3 feet for every 1 foot traveled.
