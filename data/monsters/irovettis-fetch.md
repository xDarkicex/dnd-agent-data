# Irovetti's Fetch

**description**: Most fetches are relatively similar in power, but in the case of Castruccio Irovetti, his fetch is somewhat different than most other fetch stalkers. If you use Irovetti’s Fetch’s statistics for another purpose in your game, these stats can be used as an evil version of him that seeks to replace his role in your campaign unless the PCs can stop it. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The fetch’s innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 18, +10 to hit with spell attacks). The fetch can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: produce flame (4d8) 1/day: dispel magic, finger of death, plane shift (to or from the First World only) Magic Resistance. The fetch has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Regeneration. The fetch regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn. If the fetch takes fire or necrotic damage, this trait doesn’t function at the start of the fetch’s next turn. The fetch dies only if it starts its turn with 0 hit points and doesn’t regenerate. Tongues. The fetch can understand all spoken languages and can be understood by any creature that has a language. Actions Multiattack. Irovetti’s fetch makes three attacks with its fetch weapon. Summoned Fetch Weapon. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (4d6+6) slashing damage plus 7 (2d6) psychic damage and 10 (3d6) fire damage. When Irovetti’s fetch summons his fetch weapon, he always makes it appear as the unique ranseur the living Irovetti wielded.
