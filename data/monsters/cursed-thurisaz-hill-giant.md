# Cursed Thurisaz Hill Giant

**description**: Hill giants are the most nomadic of all the humanoid giant species, preferring to travel from one settlement to the next in order to raid and pillage. Though it could hardly be said that hill giants are cowardly, they seem to be acutely aware of their diminished capacity for strategy, thus they prefer to fight from high, rocky outcroppings, where they can pelt opponents with rocks and boulders while limiting the risk to themselves. First Giants of the Old World “The first inhabitants of the north were giants, but not the mortals of enormous size we know today. Not truly immortal—they could be killed, after all—but they were beings of immense life span, measured in eons, not years. They moved about among the lesser creatures, teaching them the ways of the smith, the farmer, and the diplomat. Often these giants would assume the form of the people they sought to guide—human, elf, dwarf, or orc—taking positions of leadership and governance. They were known by many names, among them jötunn, þursar, eóten, and thurisaz, but “giants” is the word in the common tongue that has stood the test of time. Today’s giants are but a shadow of these lost titans, but their legacy still manifests from time to time in some of these erstwhile thurisaz. These giants are more inclined toward civilized discourse with adventurers who wander into their territory, rather than bombarding them with rocks. Those cursed giants who embrace their more peaceable nature find a rekindled control over their ability to blend in with the more fragile races.” —Sage Yuri Statel
