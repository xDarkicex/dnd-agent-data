# Briny Mimic

**description**: TRAITS Shapechanger: The briny mimic can morph into any object it has encountered in the water—this includes small vessels (rowboats, rafts, etc.), barrels (2d6 for quantity that appears), and crates (1d8 for quantity and size: 1-4 two medium crates, 5-8 one large crate). The creature changes back to its jellyfish-like form when traveling beneath the water and when it dies. Changing form requires two consecutive actions and a successful attack by a character interrupts a form change. Water Lurk: The briny mimic has advantage on all Stealth checks in the water when in its true form. ACTIONS Bite: Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (3d8 + 4) piercing damage. Stinger Weapons: The briny mimic possesses two stingers, each 10 feet in length (typically camouflaged as fishing poles, ripped nets, spears, or even small anchors). The stingers are able to deliver one of the following attacks per turn: Grapple: Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d4 + 2) bludgeoning damage, and the victim is grappled (escape DC 13). The briny mimic has advantage on all attack rolls against any creature grappled. Poison: Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d6 + 5) poison damage (DC 15 Constitution saving throw to take half damage).
