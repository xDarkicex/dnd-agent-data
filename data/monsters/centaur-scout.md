# Centaur Scout

**description**: Centaur The half-human, half-equine centaurs of Iobaria represent a diverse collection of peoples. Members of the Nomen tribe dwell in the westernmost reaches, living nomadic lives. They rely upon their scouts—spear-wielding centaurs who prefer to observe and report on potential threats rather than directly engage—to patrol their territories. Small groups of explorers (such as adventuring parties) often prove the exceptions to this tendency, as centaur scouts who come upon such travelers often choose to confront them at a distance while warning them to turn back. Centaur Scout Traits Charge. Once per turn, if the centaur scout moves at least 30 feet straight toward a target and then hits it with a melee spear attack on the same turn, the target takes an extra 10 (3d6) piercing damage. Actions Multiattack. The centaur scout makes two attacks: one with its spear and one with its hooves. Spear. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 10 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one creature. Hit: 7 (1d6+4) piercing damage, or 8 (1d8+4) piercing damage if used with two hands to make a melee attack. Hooves. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 11 (2d6+4) bludgeoning damage.
