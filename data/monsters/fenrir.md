# Fenrir

**description**: Fenrir the Great Wolf Fenrir is a wolf-like Yoten trapped within the mountain range known as Turnfjall. The reason this range continues to grow year after year is because this wolf is growing within it. Fenrir is a tormented beast. Not knowing where Fenrir’s loyalties might lie, Boða has kept the wolf in a perpetual sleep since shortly after arriving. Boða has filled the wolf's head with many disturbing dreams, nurturing in Fenrir a fear of both mortals and the Vanir. Hel has not known of Boða's interference with Fenrir, or why the wolf fell asleep. But she has tended to her sibling and her kind words, whispered to the sleeping wolf, have also been heard. Fenrir is confused and troubled and has never known more than their own dreams and nightmares. Fenrir is massive, standing over 100 feet tall at his shoulder. Roleplaying Fenrir Haunted. When he wakes, Fenrir will not initially know whether he is still dreaming. He will not understand the waking world and will lash out at those around him. In his dreams he has either been tormented, with little control, or has acted on all his whims, becoming almost god-like. Shy. The waking world frightens Fenrir, and he does not know how to interact with other living things. Saga Actions Maws of Death (1x). When Fenrir is below 300 hit points and his Fated Foe is within Fenrir’s movement range, this ability triggers. Fenrir acts immediately and uses as much movement as is necessary to be within 10 feet of his Fated Foe. He then takes an extra turn. He gains advantage on all attacks against the Fated Foe and scores a critical hit on a roll of 17 or higher until the end of this turn. Regret. Upon the death of his Fated Foe, or when Fenrir is reduced to less than 125 hit points, this ability triggers. Run the The Unnecessary Foe event in Chapter 12.
