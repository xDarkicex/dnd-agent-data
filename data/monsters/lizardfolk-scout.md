# Lizardfolk Scout

**description**: Lizardfolk The lizardfolk of the Stolen Lands tend to keep to themselves, but when a violent overlord seizes control they can be worked into a dangerous frenzy. Humanoid in shape, but with green or bluish scaled skin and frill-like adornments on their heads, lizardfolk are known as iruxi among their own people. Lizardfolk Scout It’s not uncommon for adventuring parties to encounter lizardfolk scouts, for these explorers spend most of their lives on the move, constantly patrolling the territories held by their enclave. If intruders are driven off, it is the scout’s responsibility to track them, observe them, and learn their ways and weaknesses so as to report back to the community to aid in the defense against potential reprisals. Traits Hold Breath. The lizardfolk can hold its breath for 15 minutes. Sneak Attack. Once per turn, the lizardfolk deals an extra 3 (1d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the lizardfolk that isn’t incapacitated and the lizardfolk doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Terrain Adept. The scout has advantage on attacks and contested ability checks against non-lizardfolk in difficult terrain, or against targets in water that lack a swim speed. Actions Multiattack. The lizardfolk makes one jaw attack and one tail attack. Jaws. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6+2) piercing damage. Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6+2) bludgeoning damage. Blowgun. Ranged Weapon Attack: +2 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: 1 piercing damage and 6 (1d12) poison damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 hour. If the saving throw fails by 5 or more, the target becomes disorientated and clumsy, and attacks on the target have advantage while poisoned in this way.
