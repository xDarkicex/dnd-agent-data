# River Elasmosaurus

**description**: The elasmosaurus is typically an ocean-dwelling creature, but a larger, more dangerous variant of this primeval reptile has adapted to life in some of the larger waterways of the River Kingdoms. Like its ocean-dwelling kin, the river elasmosaurus has a barrel-shaped body with long flippers and an exceptionally slender neck that ends in a many-toothed, snakelike head. Traits Long Neck. An elasmosaurus’s long neck allows it to interact with the surface while its body remains submerged underwater. While submerged no deeper than 15 feet underwater, an elasmosaurus can still stick its head up to breathe. An elasmosaurus gains three-quarters cover against attacks made against creatures who are above the water’s surface while it is underwater, even if its head is above the surface. Actions Multiattack. The Elasmosaurus makes one jaws and two paddle attacks per round. Jaws. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 26 (4d8+6) piercing damage. On a hit, the elasmosaurus attempts to drag its opponent below the water’s surface. The opponent must make a Strength or Dexterity saving throw contested by the elasmosaurus’s Strength (Athletics) check. If the elasmosaurus succeeds, the foe is grappled and pulled 5 feet toward the elasmosaurus’s body. If the target fails by 5 or more, it is moved 10 feet toward the elasmosaurus. Until this grapple ends, the creature is restrained, and the elasmosaurus can’t grab another target. Paddle. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 19 (3d8+6) bludgeoning damage. Thrashing Retreat. A swimming elasmosaurus thrashes the area around it as it attempts to flee. It makes two paddle attacks, each of which must be against separate targets. It then swims up to its swim speed, not provoking attacks of opportunity when it swims out of an enemy’s reach.
