# Cyrene Basilisk

**description**: Basilisk, Cyrene Cyrenes are black-scaled serpents that glide through the undergrowth, plants withering and yellowing in their wake. At first, it might be mistaken for a common grass snake. However, it travels with its head raised, like a cobra, rather than on the ground like a grass snake. It also has distinctive white markings on its head resembling a ring or crown. Its aggressive and poisonous nature quickly dispels any notion of it being harmless. A common mistake made, given its name, is that creatures die when meeting its deadly gaze. In fact, it is the very air around the cyrene that is dangerous, as the creature releases unseen, poisonous vapors into the air around it, but it is true that the mystical gaze of the cyrene’s lambent eyes focused and intensify the pernicious poison that surrounds it like a cloak. Toxic Touch . Every part of a cyrene is toxic, from its venomous fangs to its shimmering scales. Plants coming into contact with the basilisk as it travels wither and die in its wake, making the trail of the basilisk easy to follow. Likewise, a cyrene’s lair is marked by surroundings wasted and dead from constant exposure to the creature. The poisons leached into the soil from extended stays in its lair kill all life nearby. Alchemical Creation. The first basilisk of this kind was created by a sinister wizard from an ancient city. He created a creature so foul that it was deadly to all life it touched. For this and other atrocities, the wizard was eventually killed and all record of his name erased from history. His worst creation is remembered only by his city of origin, long since cast into ruin.
