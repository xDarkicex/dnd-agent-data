# Sylphise

**description**: Ethereal, unsettling, and above all alien, Sylphise serves as Anthracia’s second-in-command. Those who glimpse this forest nymph recall the sight with more fear than awe, retelling a riveting nightmare of impossibly long limbs, taloned fingers, and wide, soul-piercing eyes. Sylphise treats most other creatures as insects beneath her notice or toys for her amusement. Even elves and fey find her hard to fathom, and many whisper to each other that the nymph’s chaotic nature is a liability—but none would suggest this to Anthracia, as the dryad and nymph are utterly committed to each other. And no one can argue with her results on the field. Sylphise strikes fast as a snake, weaving through combat like a dancer, leaving terror in her wake. Traits Fey Countenance ( 1 /Turn). When a creature who can see Sylphise targets her with an attack, power, or spell, the creature must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or be blinded until the start of their next turn (no action required). Speak with Beasts and Plants. Sylphise can communicate with Beasts and Plants as if they shared a language. Supernatural Resistance. Sylphise has advantage on saving throws against powers, spells, and other supernatural effects. Actions Multiattack. Sylphise makes three Claw attacks or uses Sibilant Whispers twice. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d6 + 5) slashing damage plus 3 (1d6) psychic damage. Sibilant Whispers. One creature Sylphise can see within 60 feet of her must succeed on a DC 16 Charisma saving throw or take 10 (3d6) psychic damage. Etherealness. Sylphise magically enters the Ethereal Plane from the Mundane World, or vice versa. Reactions Too Slow! If a creature misses Sylphise with an attack, she can move up to her speed without provoking opportunity attacks from that creature.
