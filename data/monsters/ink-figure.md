# Ink Figure

**description**: Actions Violent Reflection (Recharge 4–6). As an action, the ink figure can copy an action used against it the previous turn that involved an attack, spell attack roll, or spell save DC, copying the value of the modifiers of the original action but can choose a new target. It forms any objects needed to perform the action out of ink. Blood to Paint. The ink mimic reaches out to touch a creature within 5 feet of it. The creature feels a sharp prick, takes 2 (1d4) piercing damage, and gains the bleeding condition. The creature can make a DC 16 Charisma saving throw to resist the ink figure’s influence. On success, the creature takes 8 (2d8) psychic damage. On failure, the creature is instead forced to re-live a type of memory based on the number of times it has failed this saving throw and suffer the accompanying effect. See Table A–1: Inky Memories. The ink mimic's appearance gains more color and detail, and it loses a bit of its nightmare quality. 3 The moment of its greatest personal achievement The creature regains 9 (2d8) hit points and gains advantage on its next attack roll. The ink mimic gains a picturesque likeness of the creature. It retreats into the nearest wall or stone, leaving that likeness upon the surface of that object.
