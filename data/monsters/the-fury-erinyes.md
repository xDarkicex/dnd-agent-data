# The Fury (Erinyes)

**description**: In Dante’s Inferno the furies appear before, and not behind, the gates of Dis. In our version of Hell, these demonesses guard and torture the souls in the sixth Hell circle. In Greek mythology, the three furies – also called Erinyes – are the embodiment of vengeance and remorse. They are sisters of Lyssa, the guardian of the fifth Hell circle, but even more horrifying. These demonesses have black, leathery skin, the wings of bats, feet of birds, pointy teeth, sharp nails and coarse, extendable tongues. The furies’ heads are covered in black, writhing snakes in place of hair. They have red eyes and blood is pouring from the deep scratch-wounds in their chests that they constantly inflict on themselves. Each of the otherwise naked furies is strapped with multiple belts across their torsos, holding a set of black knives for each of them as well as the bloody scalps of their victims. The furies emit a strong putrid smell. Megaera, Alecto and Tesiphone speak Greek, Latin, the Infernal language and bits of other languages, but are not big on conversation. The Erinyes are each wearing a bracelet with one of Lucifer’s hairs interwoven with hair from their victims. Megaera’s wristlet is made of red hair, Alecto’s of black and Tesiphone’s of blond braids.
