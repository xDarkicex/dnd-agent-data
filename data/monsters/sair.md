# Sair

**description**: Called the Flying Mind Screamer by the rare survivors of Abomination attacks, Sair has the wings and tail of a dragon, the feet of a dinosaur, and the head of an owl. From the sky, they scout prey and find new hunting grounds. Sair’s creators imbued psionic abilities in the flying Abomination. Sair uses these gifts to confuse prey, herding them into the waiting claws of their siblings. Among the Abominations, Sair is particularly cruel and is often scolded by Ceemal for playing with their food. The winged Abomination loves to taunt their prey before death, sometimes taking victims up to the sky and threatening to drop them. Traits Flyby. Sair doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks when they fly out of an enemy’s reach. Actions Claws. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) slashing damage. Psionic Bolt ( 1 st-Order Power). Ranged Power Attack: +6 to hit, range 120 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (3d6) psychic damage. If the target is Large or smaller, they are pushed 5 feet away from Sair. *Mind Scream ( 1 /Day; 3 rd-Order Power). Sair psionically pierces the mind of each enemy within 30 feet of them. Each target must make a DC 14 Intelligence saving throw, taking 17 (5d6) psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. A creature reduced to 0 hit points by this damage is automatically stabilized. Sair’s Got Range Sair’s flying speed paired with the 120-foot range of them Psionic Bolt attack means they never need to engage the characters in melee. While most groups have at least a few characters with far-reaching spells and weapons, there are some that may find it frustrating if Sair just keeps attacking from an unreachable position. If you’re running the Abominations against a group that is mostly focused on melee and short-range attacks, give them a fighting chance and have Sair stay closer to the ground.
