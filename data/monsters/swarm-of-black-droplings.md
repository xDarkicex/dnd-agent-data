# Swarm of Black Droplings

**description**: Dropling Within the field of oozology, it’s a generally accepted fact that oozes require a critical mass to retain functionality. That’s why the discovery of droplings—tiny blobs each about the size of a fist, spawned from larger oozes—made massive ripples in the Slime Times newspaper. No one is quite sure of the circumstances or reasons that lead to their creation; experiments on a large variety of oozes have reproduced dropling spawning with only a twenty-three percent success rate. Pseudogelphobic Swarm. Perhaps the most peculiar trait of droplings is their tendency to form swarms instead of combining to form their progenitor oozes. Respected oozologists hypothesise droplings that retain their size for an extended period of time undergo changes to their surface membrane compositions that inhibit their ability to merge with other droplings, all while maintaining the pseudo-hivemind need for consolidation. Amateur oozologists, in much fewer words, like to think they’re becoming individuals. Black Droplings This variety of droplings are descended from black puddings. They retain their acidic composition, though it is much less potent. The larger collective surface area of the swarm allows it to latch onto targets more effectively, immobilising them.
