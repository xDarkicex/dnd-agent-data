# Red Housedrake

**description**: Housedrake In societies where draconic fauna is common, it isn’t unusual for a family to have a pet housedrake in place of a dog or cat. Biomanced to be more docile than their dragon cousins, they are known for their beautiful scale coats, bright eyes, and unquestioning loyalty. Chromometallic. With colouring and affinity as broad as true dragons, wealthy households often keep whole teams of housedrakes as status symbols, trying to have at least one of each hue. Indeed, it can be useful to have access to all their elemental affinities; a well-trained white housedrake can chill a beverage or provide a cool breeze, while a brass housedrake can boil water or aid with insomnia. Draconic Packs. Unlike true dragons, which value independence, feral housedrakes roam the wilderness in large packs, drawn together by survival instinct. Unlike wolves, housedrake packs have no leader, acting more like a hivemind swarm. They can be driven by remnant instincts from their draconic ancestry or fall under the subtle sway of a nearby powerful dragon.
