# Crazed Drider

**description**: The process for becoming a drider is a torturous one. Not only is the physical change painful—ritual grafting and mutative elixirs—but driders carry a societal burden they will never escape and can never fulfill. Paragons of the Spider Goddess. To the drow, the ideal drider is a soldier, both servant and leader, the embodiment of power, grace, and ambition. At creation it would seem a new-made drider has all the traits needed to achieve this lofty ideal. As they age, they evolve. Their innate poison becomes more potent, their jaws distend and open to become mandibles. Their spider carapace grows layered and sharp. But as their form adapts to suit the drow ideal of what a drider should be, their minds begin to fracture. Doomed to Insanity. Whether it is the weight of cultural expectation, the imperfect magic of their creation, or their bestial evolution, a drider eventually succumbs to insanity. Especially male driders, who carry the added burden of rising above the failings of their former position within drow society. As a drider comes closer to being the paragon they’re expected to become, less of what drove them to become a drider in the first place remains, replaced by instinct to consume, impulse to ambush, and the desire to control.
