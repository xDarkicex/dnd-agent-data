# Cursed Knight

**description**: Denizens of the Underworld Many of the undead of Grimnir serve a somewhat different role than they do in other worlds. While on the surface skeletons and zombies exist as dangers to the living, their existence is more nuanced. Many of the dead are agents of Hel and scour the surface world at her bidding. They gather information, they help her collect the unfortunate and forgotten, and they defend her interests—and those of Grimnir. Yet, despite this semblance of civility, the undead are still frightening and the raiders avoid them. The walking dead are something they simply don’t understand and some fear that one day the undead will rise to help consume the world. This is a worrying concept and one that is often on the mind of any raider who travels near territory haunted by the once-living. That is not to say that undead are never vile, never evil. There are many examples of the dead disregarding Hel’s mercy and love. These tend to cause the same sorts of disruptions that one might expect in more typical worlds. Pretty much any undead that might be found elsewhere can be found in Grimnir and Hel does not control them—even within the Underworld they have free will. And some decide to be terrible. In addition to standard undead, the world of Grimnir has spawned several unique flavors, from the cursed knight to the pyrewights. Many of these are created because of the breaking or bending of an oath, or because a duty was not honored. Undead Nature. Like undead in other realms, these undead do not require air, food, drink, or sleep. Cursed Knight These undead knights tend to return to the world of the living after a great and tragic conflict. At first, they haunt deserted battlefields, at times re-enacting a glorious fight or watching for trespassers. They tend to adhere to the ideals they honored in life, but the ideals become exaggerated and eventually twisted, sometimes even vile. Self-Deluding. Cursed knights are intelligent but can frame their reality in a way that aligns with their beliefs. While they can set traps and outwit opponents, they ultimately exceed best at deceiving themselves. The Twisted Quest. Eventually an idea–or compulsion–forms in them, a twisted version of the duty they held in such high esteem during their lives. They take their ideals to the extreme and become a force of unbending destruction as they seek to make good on their twisted and distorted beliefs.
