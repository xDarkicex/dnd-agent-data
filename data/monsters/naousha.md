# Naousha

**description**: This slender bird resembles a black-plumed ibis, though with a striated pattern of bright, bloody red. Its neck is exceptionally long and thin, as is its curved beak. When standing fully erect, a naousha can reach over 10 feet and a wingspan of nearly 20 feet, but it is agile in the air, often flying with its wings partly furled. It likewise walks in a sinister crouch across rooftops, its neck coiled and ready to strike almost like a viper, snatching its prey and then carrying it off to be devoured. City Stalker. This malicious bird dwells in cities and villages, hiding its nest on rooftops or high, rocky clefts from where it shelters during the day and remains out of sight. At twilight, it takes wing and glides stealthily over the city looking for open windows, as these intelligent avians have learned that small, soft prey is often hidden inside. Its cruel heart savors the piteous cries it leaves behind when it snatches a small victim and makes good its escape to feast.
