# Davik Nettles

**description**: Davik Nettles rose from death as a unique undead creature, and now haunts the site of his murder as a relentless force. If you choose to use these statistics to represent an entirely new category of undead monster, you’ll want to adjust the specifics of Davik’s rejuvenation to match the lore you build for them. For example, you might create a “river revenant,” an undead creature known to haunt the shore of the waterway where it was murdered. Swapping out his ranseur for other types of weapons is another easy way to adjust this stat block to fit other cultures or types of descriptive flavor. Traits Dream Haunting. Davik can cast dream , without using a spell slot or requiring components, to enter the dreams of someone who destroyed him. This link can be ended as though it were a 5th-level spell or by anything that can end a curse, preventing him from casting dream against that target again. Rejuvenation. If Davik is reduced to 0 hit points, his bones and gear melt into water that runs back into the river or seeps into the ground, leaving nothing behind. The next night, he rises again, completely healed, but with his unholy anger now directed at those who previously destroyed him as well as at the Stag Lord. Davik can be put to rest permanently only when his unfinished business is taken care of. Rotting Stench. A creature that is not undead that begins its turn within 10 feet of Davik must succeed at a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or become poisoned until the end of its next turn. Creatures with advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks based on scent have disadvantage on this saving throw. On a success, a creature is immune to Davik’s stench for 1 minute. Sure Possession. Davik possesses a magical ranseur (a glaive +1 ). If disarmed of the ranseur, or if he drops it, it melts into water; he can retrieve it by pulling it out of any body of water. If Davik is put to rest permanently by fulfilling his quest, his ranseur loses this ability and can be claimed by someone else. Water Walk. Davik can walk on water without sinking. Actions Multiattack. Davik can use his Fearsome Gaze. He then makes two melee weapon attacks. Ranseur +1. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (1d10+5) slashing damage. Fist. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (2d4+4) bludgeoning damage. Fearsome Gaze. Each creature of Davik’s choice that is within 30 feet of him that he can see must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature’s saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to Davik’s Fearsome Gaze for the next 24 hours.
