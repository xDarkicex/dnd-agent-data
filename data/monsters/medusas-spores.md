# Medusa's Spores

**description**: This fungal hive mind produces clouds of blue-green spores that seek out water, especially wet organic matter. Its host mushrooms grow in humid environments and present with large, brown-topped white shelf mushrooms with long, woody "vines" that grow along the ground and any nearby walls, rocks, or trees. Creatures that come into contact with the spores swiftly turn to stone, then disintegrate, as the Medusa Spore Touch disease rapidly progresses through their bodies. Petrivore. The Medusa Spore fungus primarily feeds on rock, and gains the most nutrition from petrified organic matter. Its ability to petrify creatures therefore allows it to rapidly convert them to its most valuable food source, and, in fact, their powdered remains makes them even faster to digest. Deep Infestation. When destroyed, the spore cloud regenerates from the host mushrooms within 1d3 days. Destroying the host fungus requires the burning of all its mushrooms and "vines" - a tedious task that requires successful DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) and Wisdom (Perception) checks, or the devotion of 16 hours of strenuous activity. Subtle Threat. While the medusa’s spores do not immediately appear to be a threat, their ability to rapidly progress the disease toward its Lethal IIIII end is a dire threat that should not be underestimated. The slightest overconfidence can spell an adventurer or explorer’s doom. Tactics. The spore swarm is not particularly motivated to spread the disease far and wide, and will prefer to advance the disease as quickly as possible on a single target. Traits Spore Swarm. The spores automatically succeed Strength, Intelligence, and Charisma saving throws. Additionally, the spores can enter another creature's space and end their turn there, and do not provoke opportunity attacks. The spores cannot grapple other creatures. Impervious Body. The spores cannot be Injured except when dealt fire damage, and only fire damage counts toward their Injury thresholds. Actions Spore Touch. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 0 ft., one target. Hit: 2 (1d4) necrotic damage, and the target must make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be afflicted with the Medusa Spore Touch disease. A creature that is already afflicted has the disease progress to the next stage. Undead have disadvantage on this saving throw and are not immune to this disease.
