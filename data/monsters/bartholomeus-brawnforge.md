# Bartholomeus Brawnforge

**description**: Background Though the young Bartholomeus Brawnforge was in many ways a dwarf much like any other, he never really fit in. Where most of Clan Darkrock were simple dwarves, content with spending their days toiling in the mines or forges and their nights drinking ale by the barrel, Bart was always quiet and bookish. When an encounter with an umber hulk rendered him blind, the clan's clerics failed to cure his blindness. Desperate, Bart's mother sent Bart to the College Arcana to see if arcane magic might prevail. Not only did the wizards manage to grant Bart magical sight, they also showed him a new world of possibility. To his mother's chagrin, the young Bart chose to remain at the college to study the arcane arts. If Bart had hoped to fit in among the wizards at the College Arcana – most of whom were humans, elves, halflings, or gnomes – he would have been disappointed. Nearly all his peers discounted the quiet, burly dwarf as a simpleton who was out of his element. It soon became clear to all, however, that the soft-spoken Bart was more than he appeared: the dwarf had an undeniable talent for arcane magic and an unquenchable thirst for knowledge. When Bart left the College Arcana, he did so with honors and marks of excellence. Still not satisfied, Bart sought to expand his theoretical knowledge with practical experience, and embarked on several adventures. Delving into ancient ruins and monsters' lairs, sometimes alone and sometimes alongside companions, Bart gathered an impressive hoard of treasures and arcane knowledge. Finally, once his wanderlust had been sated, Bart settled down and opened up The Arcane Anvil , where he trades and enchants magic items. Roleplaying Bartholemeus Bartholomeus Brawnforge is something of an enigma; quiet, observant, and stoic, he seems to take in everything around him while giving little of himself. He moves slowly, speaks softly, and always seems to think for a long while before he acts, but burns within with a bright intelligence and a passionate spirit. Though Bart rarely speaks unless he is spoken to, he has a habit of suddenly delivering insightful observations as statements of fact ("You are a loyal companion” or "You fear you won't be strong enough for what lies ahead”). While some are taken off-guard by these comments and suspect him of being sarcastic or deliberately insulting, this is simply Bart's awkward attempt to communicate more (something his mother always urged him to do!) Bart also has a quirky habit of talking to his wares, almost as if they were pets or trusted comrades. Imbued with a minor sentience, the items can't speak but may respond to his compliments ("Who's a good sword? Who's a good sword? You're a good sword”) by shaking gleefully or proudly floating about as he presents them to customers ("This shield here is a true beauty – and a great listener too”).
