# Swarm of Brambleweeds

**description**: Brambleweed Brambleweeds were once exclusive to their native desert in the Wild Sands, where the scarcity of water kept the population in check. Traders passing through the area were instructed to check their caravans for errant brambleweeds carried by the wind, which would attach themselves to the bonnets of the wagons with their thorns. A few careless merchants were all it took to turn this dangerous plant into a widely spread invasive species. Now present in warm biomes across the world, brambleweeds can be recognized by their bulbous leaves and woody vines, covered in barb-like, raking thorns. Much like quicksand, the trick to surviving a fall into a patch of brambleweeds is remaining calm—the more one moves, the deeper the thorns dig into one’s flesh. Overgrown Thickets. The introduction of brambleweed into regions where water is more abundant has allowed them to thrive unchecked. A gallon of water can turn brambleweeds into a mobile thicket overnight, and a river can transform them into a village-wide threat. Culling brambleweeds is a dangerous but necessary task, one often assigned as punishment to disobedient druids and rangers.
