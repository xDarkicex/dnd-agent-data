# Spirit of Stisshak

**description**: This cruel, manipulative will-o’-wisp has been influencing local lizardfolk for some time. The spirit’s ability to appear as a long-dead lizardfolk can be adjusted to give it the ability to appear as anything else you require, such as an ancient-but-revered king, a notorious villain, or even the visage of a deity. Traits Consume Life. As a bonus action, the spirit of Stisshak can target one creature it can see within 5 feet of it that has 0 hit points and is still alive. The target must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw against this magic or die. If the target dies, the spirit of Stisshak regains 10 (3d6) hit points. Ephemeral. The spirit of Stisshak can’t wear or carry anything. Incorporeal Movement. The spirit of Stisshak can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. It takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside an object. Variable Illumination. The spirit of Stisshak sheds bright light in a 5- to 20-foot radius and dim light for an additional number of feet equal to the chosen radius. The spirit of Stisshak can alter the radius as a bonus action. Actions Shock. Melee Spell Attack: +12 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 27 (6d8) lightning damage. Invisibility. The will-o’-wisp and its light magically become invisible until it attacks or uses its Consume Life, or until its concentration ends (as if concentrating on a spell). Invoke Stisshak. Once per day, the spirit of Stisshak can manifest a shimmering form, that of a regal lizardfolk king made of flickering, ghostly light. This image is Medium and must manifest within 10 feet of the spirit. Once manifested, the spirit can sustain the image by concentrating, and can speak through it and effect minor gestures, but can’t move the manifestation. The spirit gains advantage on Intimidation checks made to demoralize foes through this invoked image. Any damage inflicted to the spirit causes the invoked illusion to vanish immediately.
