# Immense Mandragora

**description**: Mandragora Normally small, humanoid-looking masses of blood-drinking plant life, the influence of the First World can transform mandragoras into lumbering behemoths or organize them into ravenous swarms. An individual mandragora can easily be mistaken for a root, burl, or tuber that grew into a vaguely humanoid shape. Immense Mandragora Most mandragoras are small in stature, but this truism does not hold in some corners of reality. These enormous mandragoras are fortunately found only rarely beyond their home plane of the First World, where their presence is always an indication of a powerful, sinister force holding sway over the region. An immense mandragora is typically just over 30 feet in height. Traits Regeneration. The mandragora regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn. If the mandragora takes fire or thunder damage, this trait doesn’t function at the start of the mandragora’s next turn. The mandragora dies only if it starts its turn with 0 hit points and doesn’t regenerate. Vulnerable to Supernatural Darkness. I n areas of magical darkness (such as those created by the darkness spell), the mandragora is slowed, as the slow spell. Actions Multiattack. The immense mandragora makes three melee attacks: one with its bite and two with its vine whips. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (5d6+6) piercing damage. Vine Whip. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (5d6+6) slashing damage plus 14 (4d6) poison damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned. The creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. Shriek (Recharge after Long Rest). The mandragora can give voice to an unsettling shriek. All creatures within 30 feet of the mandragora must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom save or become stunned for 1d4 rounds. Each creature in that area must also make a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw, taking 52 (15d6) thunder damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
