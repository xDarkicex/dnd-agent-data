# Karz Slug

**description**: Calcified horns protrude from this enormous slug at both ends. Its fleshy maw twitches, exuding viscous slime. A karz slug leaves a trail of amber slime in its wake. Karz slugs are rarely found alone. If they are separated from their kin they become withdrawn and docile–fleeing at the first opportune moment. However, in groups they become vicious and territorial, killing and consuming other creatures en masse. The name of these slugs is a corruption of karst, an cavity formed in dissolving limestone. It is thought the slug gets its horns by its slime reducing the soft limestone to an edible paste. Karz slug family groups also spend much of their time hidden in spaces seemingly much too small for them, and it is known that karz slug trails from food source to water to home are often the narrow paths created by seasonal floods. Slime Gland. A karz slug possesses a unique organ: its slime gland. When properly harvested and prepared a slime gland is prized by alchemists because it grants immunity to the charmed and poison conditions for those who can stomach it. Slime Trap. Ancient karz slugs are wise enough to cast their lure illusion on a layer of previously-deposited slime that forms a cover over pits and chimneys. The slugs themselves can easily climb out of these, leaving the disguised trap in their wake. The lurer then waits in a small space over the pit before lowering itself onto injured or unconscious victims., delivering spit slime damage with great efficiency and effectiveness.
