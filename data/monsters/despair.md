# Despair

**description**: When a mortal soul cannot find its way to the afterlife and the mortal in question died with a lonesome, grief-stricken heart, a despair is the result. Whether a brave adventurer on some distant plane of existence or a forsaken commoner, bereft of family and community, a despair appears physically much as they did in life. Indeed, many despair may not initially realize they’ve died. But when the despair interacts with their whilom, their true nature becomes apparent even to themselves. Whilom. A despair appears unchanged from their former selves until it encounters its whilom. A whilom is a humanoid that reminds the despair of their grief, of the misery and lonesomeness that has replaced a despair’s soul. A whilom could be simply any member of the despair’s former race, a familiar friend, or a specific person tied to the despair’s moment of death. Regardless of the source, the whilom becomes the focus of the despair’s anguish, the only relief for which is to ruin the whilom’s soul, just as the despair’s is ruined. Despair Dagger. When it encounters its whilom for the first time, a despair discovers it can manifest a psychic weapon: a despair dagger. Each dagger is unique to its despair. A wound from a despair dagger bombards the recipient with images of woe and misery, preparing the soul to become a despair. Former Adventurers. Occasionally, despairs from the same adventuring group gather together in a pitiful, yet deadly parody of their former band. The group is unstable, however, and the transformation from harmony to discord can happen in an instant. Groups of despairs hold together long enough to defeat any party they come across; the subsequent fighting over spoils is jaw-dropping, with many items, particularly those that allow inter-planar travel, being destroyed during the argument, so that no one can have them and leave the others behind. Undead Nature. A despair doesn’t require air, food, drink, or sleep.
