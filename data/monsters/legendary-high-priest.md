# Legendary High Priest

**description**: Traits Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the high priest fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead. Greater Spell Penetration. When the high priest casts a spell and is interrupted by an enemy's counterspell, treat the spell's level as 5 levels higher. Superior Focus. The high priest has advantage on saving throws it makes to maintain its concentration. Actions Quarterstaff. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) bludgeoning damage, or 6 (1d8 + 2) bludgeoning damage if wielded with two hands, plus 9 (2d8) radiant (good) or 9 (2d8) necrotic (evil) damage. Spellcasting. The high priest casts one of the following spells using Wisdom as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 18, +10 to hit with spell attacks): At will: guidance, light, sacred flame (4d8), thaumaturgy, word of radiance (4d6) 2/day each: banishment, cure wounds (as a 3rd-level spell), hold person, flame strike 1/day each: dispel evil and good, dispel magic, harm, heal, divine word, fire storm, holy aura, true resurrection Bonus Actions Divine Eminence (3/Day). The high priest causes its melee weapon attacks to magically deal an extra 10 (3d6) radiant (good or neutral) or necrotic (evil or neutral) damage to a target on a hit. This benefit lasts until the end of the turn. Legendary Actions The high priest can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The high priest regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. Move. The high priest moves up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks. Cantrip. The high priest casts sacred flame or word of radiance . Quarterstaff. The high priest attacks with its Quarterstaff. Cast a Spell (Costs 2 Actions). The high priest casts banishment, cure wounds, dispel magic , or hold person , if available.
