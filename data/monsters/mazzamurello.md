# Mazzamurello

**description**: A mazzamurello resembles a tiny man with a red suit and pointy hat and shoes. They rarely stand still and are most often spotted jumping and springing around the forest. In a few moments, they are gone, leaving only the silvery sounds of laughter behind. More good-hearted than their cousin the pooka, but as much fun-loving as it is, mazzamurelli (“they who hit walls with a mace”) hide in the woods, causing unwary youths to lose their way and return home with their hair in a mess, or party in abandoned houses, making loud sounds all the time but never being seen. Mazzamurelli are feared and cautiously loved by peasants, who try not to displease the little fey, afraid to draw bad luck on themselves. It is bad luck, for example, to untie a knot a mazzamurello tied. These creatures fancy protecting good humans and punish greed or noisy ones who don’t respect nature. One mazzamurello can throw into confusion an entire hostile army, causing it to retire and thus saving a human city he likes; of a band of them can fool evil people giving them plenty of gold pieces and gems that vanish in a few hours. If humans don’t show proper respect, mazzamurelli can keep them awake all night by banging with maces on a house’s walls. Regional Variants The mazzamurello is known in all Italy with many slightly different names (mazzariol, mazapegol, mazapedar, mazzarot, mazzamauro, mazzamureddu, mmazzamerielle, mazzamurille, mazzamurielle, scazamurrieddhru..). These fey are known to play any kind of jokes on humans, tying knot in the hair of humans and domestic animals alike, molesting girls and women, but also to help humans they like in houseworks or farm works, protect the woods, and ward treasures forgotton by dragons or brigands.
