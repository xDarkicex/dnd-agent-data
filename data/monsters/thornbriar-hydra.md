# Thornbriar Hydra

**description**: Named for the mythological creature they resemble, thornbriar hydras are a collective of animated thorny plants that have gained an animal level of intelligence, a thirst for blood, and a hunger for flesh. With tangled vines protecting their core from attack, thornbriar hydras form a low, rolling mass with long, grasping tendrils that seek out and devour prey. Though these tendrils end in biting masses and even seem to mimic animal heads, they are not truly vital pieces of its body. Without legs, it relies on many thorny vines, which lash out in the direction it wishes to move, grip the ground, and pull. Inside each mouth is an enormous red pitcher plant that the mass relies on to digest its prey. Hard to Kill. Unlike regular hydras, this creature does not die if it loses its heads. Killing a thornbriar hydra requires inflicting numerous Injuries until the creature finally succumbs to the wounds. Even the prevention of instantaneous regeneration with necrotic energy doesn’t keep the creature down for long. Tactics. The thornbriar hydra knows it is extremely hard to kill, and unless it faces fire or necrotic damage it does not back down from a fight. If it is at risk of losing its last head, however, it will attempt to flee. During the fight, it tries to keep enemies at the end of its reach, and encourages them to provoke opportunity attacks by moving away from melee opponents whenever it is reasonably safe to do so. Traits Plant Collective. The thornbriar hydra cannot be surprised, has advantage on saving throws against being charmed, frightened, paralyzed, and stunned, and cannot be put to sleep. Multiheadded. The thornbriar hydra has one additional reaction per head it has beyond one, which can only be used to make opportunity attacks. A thornbriar hydra typically has four heads when encountered. If the thornbriar hydra has no heads remaining, the DC for it to resist Death Blows increases by 5. Rapid Growth. Whenever the thornbriar hydra becomes Injured it ignores the effects of that Injury and loses a head. It also loses a head if it is reduced to 0 hit points, but does not die. Then, if it hasn't taken fire or necrotic damage since the end of its last turn, it grows two new heads and regains 10 hit points. Otherwise, if it has 0 hit points, it regains 1 hit point. Sentinel Heads. Whenever a creature enters the thornbriar hydra's reach, it provokes an attack of opportunity from the thornbriar hydra. Thorny Body. Whenever the thornbriar hydra is hit by a natural weapon, or by an attack made from within 5 feet of it, its attacker must make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw or take 7 (2d6) piercing damage. On a successful save the creature takes half damage. Actions Multiattack. The thornbriar hydra makes one Bite attack per head it has. Grow. The thornbriar hydra sprouts two heads if it has none remaining. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, one target, reach 10 feet. Hit: 8 (1d8+3) piercing damage.
