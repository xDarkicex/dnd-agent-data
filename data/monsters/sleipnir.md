# Sleipnir

**description**: Sleipnir Unlike the standard horses that the raiders use, these swift eight-legged beasts were discovered by the first settlers to Grimnir and as such are considered primal creatures. Like other such beings, they were formed by the sheer power released when the magician, Mirgal, was sacrificed to build this safe haven. Caretakers. Sleipnir care about the world and ensure that other beasts are not overtly exploited or mistreated. Though they do not understand why other horses accept domestication, they are especially fond of these “cousins” of theirs. Varied and Various. They live in small herds in several different regions of Grimnir. They are born with a variety of different colors—anything from the browns, whites, and blacks of standard riding horses to more vibrant reds, yellows, and greens. Wild and Free. There are only a handful of herds known to exist in Grimnir and these horses are near impossible to tame. Few mortals ever have the chance to ride them. The sleipnir alone decide whether they will carry others. Exceptional Mounts If a character wants one of these majestic creatures as mount, they should have to earn that right by performing good acts. But even if they accept a rider, the sleipnir is unlike any other riding horse. They will refuse to be mistreated and are likely to leave if this happens. Especially ornery sleipnir will even attack someone they feel is not worthy of them. A player that has a steed as part of their class archetype or subclass, may, at the GM’s discretion replace that steed with a sleipnir (assuming the previous criteria have been met). In this case the sleipnir loses its Multiattack feature and Cooperative ability. It is recommended that such a mount is not introduced until later into the campaign.
