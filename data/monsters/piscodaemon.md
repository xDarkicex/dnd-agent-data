# Piscodaemon

**description**: Daemon Daemons are sinister neutral evil fiends who form from the most despicable of souls and personify the most horrible ways one can die. Natives to the neutral evil outer planes, daemons lack the strict order and regiment associated with devils, yet also lack the almost gleeful addiction to spreading chaos and mayhem common to demons. They are, many scholars posit, one of the purest forms of evil. Daemons as a whole cover a wide range of power, and many are associated with the legendary demigods known collectively as the Horsemen of the Apocalypse. Piscodaemon Scions of death by poisoning, piscodaemons are cruel even by daemonic standards, delighting in slow and painful suffering. While poisons and venoms are piscodaemons’ preferred tools, their cruel claws are more than capable of tearing enemies asunder if necessary. A piscodaemon is vaguely humanoid in shape, with an octopus-like head, crab pincers for arms, reptilian legs, and a fish-like tail. Traits Amphibious. The piscodaemon can breathe air and water. Innate Spellcasting. The piscodaemon’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 15, +7 to hit with spell attacks). The piscodaemon can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: dispel magic 3/day each: fly, stinking cloud Magic Resistance. The piscodaemon has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. See Invisibility. The piscodaemon has a constant see invisibility effect as per the spell. Actions Multiattack. The piscodaemon makes two claw attacks and one tentacles attack. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 21 (3d10+5) slashing damage. Tentacles. Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (1d10+5) bludgeoning damage plus 14 (4d6) poison damage.
