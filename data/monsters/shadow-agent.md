# Shadow Agent

**description**: The transmigration of an immortal soul into the form of a shadow agent requires a carefully enacted ritual followed by the original creature’s death. The creature’s soul becomes cursed into a service that lasts beyond death. Whether it was willingly given in exchange for “immortality” or stolen and unjustly warped, the creature’s death triggers the dormant magic. Upon expiration, the creature’s soul migrates into its shadow. The admixture of magic and raw animus gives the resulting creature, a shadow agent, a semi-tangible form. This undying, untiring new vessel serves as a vector for the creator’s will. Fractured Animus. Shadow agents have been robbed of the capacity for empathy and are largely driven by the directives of their creators, making them excellent tools for all manner of wetwork. Their detached outlook enables them to enact the cruel and twisted without being distracted by ethics, emotion, or reason. In spite of their muted emotions and lack of true agency, shadow agents demonstrate the remarkable capacity to imitate the mannerisms, speech patterns, and habits of their living selves. One might even mistake the remnants of their original personalities for more than mere affectations. Rogue Agents. For a soul to metamorphose into a shadow agent, it must first be broken down into its salient components. However, the process is delicate, and the practitioner must take great care to ensure that the reconstituted persona is complete. The subject’s soul will never be whole again, but a less-than-complete reconstruction can leave the resulting agent with an unstable psyche. These agents are much more likely to question their creator’s orders, often with violent results.
