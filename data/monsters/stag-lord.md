# Stag Lord

**description**: The Stag Lord represents the first true “boss” of the Kingmaker Adventure Path . You can remove the Stag Lord’s Perpetual Hangover, restoring him to a CR 7 foe, and have these stats represent an entire group of stag helm–wearing troublemakers, perhaps led by an even more sinister ruler. Traits Assassinate. During his first turn, the Stag Lord has advantage on attack rolls against any creature that hasn’t taken a turn. Any hit the Stag Lord scores against a surprised creature is a critical hit. Evasion. If the Stag Lord is subjected to an effect that allows him to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, he instead takes no damage if he succeeds on the saving throw, and only half damage if he fails. Keen Hearing and Sight. The Stag Lord has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or sight. Perpetual Hangover. The Stag Lord is in a constant cycling state of being drunk or hungover. As a result, he has disadvantage on all Dexterity and Wisdom checks. Sneak Attack. Once per turn, the Stag Lord deals an extra 10 (3d6) damage when he hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the Stag Lord that isn’t incapacitated and the Stag Lord doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Actions Multiattack. The Stag Lord makes two melee or three ranged attacks. Longsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d8+2) slashing damage or 7 (1d10+2) slashing damage if used with two hands. Longbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, range 150 ft./600 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d8+4) piercing damage. Volley (Recharge 6). The Stag Lord makes one ranged attack against every enemy within 10 feet of a point he can see.
