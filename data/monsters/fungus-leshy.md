# Fungus Leshy

**description**: Where fungiants and funglets work to maintain vibrant, lush forests full of healthy mushrooms, fungus leshys work at the other end of the plant spectrum, although their role is no less important. They are the keepers of caverns full of rotting compost pits and dead matter, encouraging the growth of strange subterranean flora that sprout and die quickly, but are vital fertilizers for the rest of the Underworld. Much like other flora, fungus leshys take on the appearance of their charges, and so take on bizarre and alien forms that often arouse suspicion at best, and fear or loathing at worst. Though they are inarguably plants, their bizarre, asymmetrical bodies appear ooze-like with their ability to change shape and vary their form. Much like other fungifolk, leshys tend to adopt the color, shape, form, and abilities of the plants and mushrooms they are tending. Their spores tend to change the appearance of victims to that of someone with a virulent wasting disease or even undead traits. Similarly, the aroma left behind on a creature after an encounter with leshys is one akin to them rotting from the inside out, because while the skin may be healed, it has often done so over deep-set spores that take weeks to dissipate. The alien nature of leshys is enhanced by their method of reproduction, which involves pooling deceased leshys together to form new individuals from the decaying matter. Newborn leshys are entirely new; that is, they don’t retain any memories of the individuals who comprised their decay-birth pool, and any physical resemblance is mere coincidence. Periodically the corpses of other creatures are added to the pool to bolster growth, and for a time leshys born from that pool may display physiological or personality traits that could be associated with the seed-creature, such as wings, horns, hooves, or a predatory outlook. Fungus leshys thrive in the deep caverns of the Underworld, but are equally prevalent on the old-growth forests and fungi glens of the surface. Warmth, not light, is the driving force for leshy metabolism.
