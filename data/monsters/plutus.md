# Plutus

**description**: The demon Plutus was worshipped as the god of wealth in ancient Greece. He is often mistaken for Pluto, the Greek ruler of the underworld. His origins are shady – some say he is the child of Hades and Persephone, others that he is an offspring of Demeter and a mortal named Iasion, but in truth, Plutus was a mighty human warlock whose greed and love for wealth drove him to make a pact with Satan himself. Hell warped this man into a huge pale creature with distorted features, hunchbacked and with deformed limbs. His hands are grotesquely large. Apart from golden shinguards and a golden breastplate decorated with precious stones, Plutus is naked. He is carrying Lucifer’s hair in a golden amulet around his neck. It is clearly visible and may be taken from him if he is defeated. Plutus speaks the Infernal language, Greek and some kind of vulgar Latin resembling 15th century Italian, as well as bits of all kinds of other languages. Think of the monk Salvatore from the Name of the Rose movie and his gibberish, only make him less adorable. Despite his deplorable exterior and strange way of speaking, Plutus is actually one of the most intelligent demons in Hell, and he feels gravely punished by the undignified state in which he finds himself. If the characters are willing to talk to him, he might tell them how he himself had, during his own lifetime, already planted the myth of being of divine origin in order to encourage the simple folk to make plentiful donations to him. His mortal father Iasion was killed by the gods because his eye had fallen on Demeter. Unfortunately, though, his mother was a mere mortal companion of his father’s and not a goddess, but the young Plutus artfully exploited this story for his own good.
