# Cerberus

**description**: This ravenous three headed beast is the guardian of the third circle. Dante describes him as a great worm (dragon) with a big belly, claws and three dog’s heads. His sharp teeth and scaled tail make him a dangerous foe. Cerberus is the offspring of two ancient monsters: Echidna, a snake-woman and Typhon, a giant of the early days. Both are now lost but their son rules in Hell. He is responsible for watching over the entrance to this Hell circle, and whenever he is hungry, he will descend unto the sinners, tearing them limb from limb, opening bellies and inflicting deep wounds to sate his voracious hunger. He is guardian and punisher at the same time. Cerberus carries Lucifer’s hair within himself, more precisely: in his liver. One can harvest it by cutting him open, which would be a true mess with foul bile and black blood spilling out like a dark river. Cerberus does not speak, but he understands simple sentences and commands in the Infernal language. Traits Keen Smell. Cerberus has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell. Playful. Cerberus likes to play with his prey (normally lost souls). If he catches his target and it stops moving or he is distracted by something else, he won’t attack anymore and search for new prey. Actions Multiattack. Cerberus can either make three bite attacks or one claw attack, but remember as a GM that he is playing with his prey not aiming for the kill. Fire Breath (Recharge 5–6). Cerberus exhales fire in a 30-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw, taking 14 (4d6) fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 3) slashing damage.
