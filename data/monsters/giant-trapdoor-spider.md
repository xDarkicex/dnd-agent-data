# Giant Trapdoor Spider

**description**: Spider Giant spiders are feared by many, and those that dwell in the Stolen Lands are certainly adept at giving adventurers new things to fear beyond being merely poisoned or caught in a sticky web. Giant Trapdoor Spider Bulkier than the typical giant spider, the giant trapdoor spider builds a pit in the ground and conceals its entrance with a cleverly camouflaged lid. It then lies in wait just below this trapdoor, ready to spring out and seize anything that comes too close. Traits Spider Climb. The spider can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check. Trapdoor Lunge. If the spider surprises a creature and hits it with an attack during the first round of combat, the target takes an extra 7 (2d6) damage from the attack. The spider can then move up to its speed. Web Walker. The spider ignores movement restrictions caused by webbing. Actions Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 7 (1d10+2) piercing damage, and the target must make a DC 11 Constitution saving throw, taking 18 (4d8) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. If the poison damage reduces the target to 0 hit points, the target is stable but poisoned for 1 hour, even after regaining hit points, and is paralyzed while poisoned in this way.
