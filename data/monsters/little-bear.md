# Little Bear

**description**: Known for their immense strength, formidable stature, and status as apex predators in forests, mountains, and ice sheets, bears are revered (and feared) as embodiments of nature’s might. Ursine Variation. In our own world, bears vary in size and diet. The brown bear (a category that includes the grizzly bear and Kodiak bear) and polar bear are the largest land predators in the world—though despite their bulk, they can sprint toward their prey at terrifying speeds. On the other hand, the giant panda and much smaller sun bear are herbivores, while the odd sloth bear uses their long, curved claws to dig up ants and termites. Traits Exceptional Smell. The bear has a +10 bonus to Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell, and they can smell creatures even through dirt, snow, and water. Natural Camouflage. The bear has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made in their native terrain. Actions Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage, or maximum damage if the target is a Plant. Swat. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (2d6 + 3) slashing or bludgeoning damage (bear’s choice), and the target must succeed on a DC 13 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
