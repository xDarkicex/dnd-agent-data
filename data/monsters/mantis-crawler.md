# Mantis Crawler

**description**: This six-legged insectoid monstrosity's limbs each end in a sharpened blade, perfect for slicing through its prey. Known for their powerful leaps, mantis crawlers are capable of covering large distances in a single bound and pinning their prey with all six legs. Their chitinous exoskeletons are also exceptionally hard to pierce, but their brittleness makes them easier targets for clubs and other forceful weapons. Environment. Mantis crawlers live anywhere they can find food, and, not needing large amounts of water or sunlight, can even be found in the driest deserts and the deepest tunnels. Tactics. Mantis Crawler's open their hunt with a Slashing Leap, attempting to pin their prey before stabbing down at them with a Leg Slash. A mantis crawler fights fiercely in the absence of bludgeoning weapons, but will flee if too many of its foes seem capable of breaking its brittle exoskeleton. Traits Chitinous Body. The mantis crawler's Handicap III Injury threshold is increased by 5 for attacks dealing piercing or slashing damage, and reduced by 5 for attacks dealing bludgeoning damage. Additionally, when piercing or slashing damage fails to inflict an Injury, the mantis crawler takes 5 less damage. Powerful Leap. The mantis crawler can leap up to 30 feet forward and up to 10 feet in the air with a standing jump. Actions Multiattack. The mantis crawler makes two Leg Slash attacks against the same creature. Leg Slash. Melee Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 3 (1d4+1) slashing damage. Slashing Leap (recharge 6). The mantis crawler leaps at a creature within its jump distance, attempting to pin it down with its bladed limbs. That creature must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or be grappled, knocked prone, and take 10 (4d4) slashing damage. On a successful saving throw, the creature takes half damage and is not grappled or knocked prone.
