# Vrock Doomsayer

**description**: Vrock doomsayers are a very rare, special type of vrock that can perform a ritualistic dance that enables them to harness destructive energy. If left unmolested, this dance of ruin can destroy groups of non-demon enemies. It is no surprise then that demon hordes include several vrock doomsayers for this purpose alone, especially when invading the Nine Hells. Traits Magic Resistance. The vrock has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Actions Multiattack. The vrock makes two attacks: one with its Beak and one with its Talons. Beak. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage. Talons. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (2d10 + 4) slashing damage. Spellcasting. The vrock casts one of the following spells, requiring no material components and using Wisdom as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 13): At will: darkness, detect magic, heroism, mirror image 3/day each: polymorph (self only), telekinesis Spores (Recharge 5–6). A 15-foot-radius cloud of toxic spores extends out from the vrock. The spores spread around corners. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or become poisoned. Until this poison ends, the target takes 5 (1d10) poison damage at the start of each of its turns. A target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. Emptying a vial of holy water on the target also ends the effect on it. Stunning Screech (Recharge 6). The vrock emits a horrific screech. Each creature within 20 feet of it that can hear it and that isn't a demon must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of the vrock's next turn. Dance of Ruin (1/Day). While standing on the ground, a vrock joins hands with at least 2 other vrocks in a circle, chanting and performing a wild ritual dance that lasts for 3 consecutive turns. The vrocks share the same initiative, and take their turns at the same time. At the end of each turn of dancing, a violent wave of crackling, infernal energy explodes in a 60-foot radius, centered on the vrocks. Each creature in the area that isn't a demon must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) lightning damage and 21 (6d6) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. For each additional vrock that joins in the dance, the damage increases by 3 (1d6) lightning damage and 3 (1d6) necrotic damage, to a maximum of 35 (10d6) lightning damage and 35 (10d6) necrotic when seven or more vrocks are dancing. The dance of ruin can be interrupted if the number of performing vrocks is reduced to less than 3. A vrock that becomes incapacitated, dies, or is forced to break contact with the vrocks' circle can't perform the dance.
