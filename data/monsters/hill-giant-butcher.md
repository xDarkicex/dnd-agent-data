# Hill Giant Butcher

**description**: Giant In the Stolen Lands, hill giants are the most common of their kind. Small numbers of marsh giants can be found in the depths of Hooktongue Slough. Hill Giant Butcher The dreaded hill giant butchers of Glenebon are much feared by other hill giants and adventurers alike, for they take great pleasure in stalking their prey until an opportunity to slice their victim to bits presents itself. Hill giant butchers struggle to hunt successfully in small groups and often succumb to internal bickering that leads quickly to deadly expressions of their violent compulsions, leaving only one survivor. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The giant’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 13). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no components: At will: barkskin, entangle, longstrider Sneak Attack. Once per turn, the giant deals an extra 7 (2d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the giant that isn’t incapacitated and the giant doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Actions Multiattack. The giant makes a battle axe and hatchet attack. Battle Axe +2. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 24 (4d6+10) slashing damage. Hatchet +1. Melee Weapon Attack: +14 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 37 (8d6+9) slashing damage. Thrown Rock. Ranged Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, range 60/240 ft., one target. Hit: 30 (4d10+8) bludgeoning damage. Wide Swing (Recharge 5–6). The giant swings its battle axe in a circle around itself, and every creature within 10 feet of it must make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw. On a failed saving throw, a creature takes 24 (4d6+10) slashing damage and is knocked prone. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn’t knocked prone. Twin Butchery (Only a target under the effects of Menace Prey). So long as the giant is wielding two melee weapons, each in a different hand and hits his menaced prey twice in the same round it deals an additional 2d6 damage and the giant has advantage on its next attack roll against the target. Menace Prey. The giant designates up to two creatures it can see and hear, or one they’re tracking, as their prey. The target becomes marked. While marked, the giant gains a +2 bonus to Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Survival) checks to track the prey. If the giant scores a critical hit against the marked target they are automatically frightened of the giant for one minute. Targets are marked for up to 24 hours, or until the giant uses this ability again. Reactions Catch Rock. The giant can attempt to catch a rock thrown at a target that is within 10 feet of itself. The giant makes a Dexterity saving throw against the attack roll of the attacking creature. If it succeeds, the giant catches the rock and the intended target takes no damage.
