# Quelaunt

**description**: This three-armed, three-legged monster has no discernible eyes, nose, ears, or mouth and no visible means of ingesting food. Its limbs are distributed evenly across its body such that it is all but impossible to tell which way the creature is oriented at any given time. Few who witness the quelaunt reflect on its alien visage for long, as the most pressing concern quickly becomes an invasion of their minds when the quelaunt sows doubt, sorrow, and rage—drawing sustenance from the psychic trauma of these negative emotions. Traits Innate Spellcasting. The quelaunt’s spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 21, +13 to hit with spell attacks). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: wind walk, speak with dead, tongues 3/day each: bestow curse, calm emotion, fear, hideous laughter Magical Weapons. The quelaunt’s weapon attacks are magical. Psychic Feeder. When the quelaunt deals psychic damage to a creature, it gains 5 temporary hit points. The quelaunt can have as many as 40 total temporary hit points gained in this way. As an action, the quelaunt can remove any number of these temporary hit points to recover a number of hit points equal to the temporary hit points removed. Actions Multiattack. The quelaunt makes three melee attacks, or it casts two innate spells. Claws. Melee Weapon Attack: +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d8+7) piercing damage and 10 (3d6) psychic damage. Wracking Spiral (Recharge 5–6). The quelaunt emits a burst of emotion-altering energy in a 60-foot radius. Creatures in the area that aren’t aberrations must make a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw. Creatures that fail take 55 (10d10) psychic damage and gain one condition (described below) on a failed save. Those that succeed take half as much damage and don’t gain a condition. Wracking Spiral can cause a creature to gain the following conditions: Adoration. The creature becomes charmed by the quelaunt for 1 minute. Despair. The creature becomes frightened of the quelaunt, and is restrained for 1 minute. Delusion. The creature gains a short-term madness for 1 minute and is convinced that they are much stronger than they really are. The affected creature takes dangerous and foolish risks. Affected creatures can make another saving throw at the end of each of their turns, ending the condition on a success.
