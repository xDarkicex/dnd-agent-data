# Nightborn

**description**: The Nightborn are creatures appearing in cautious tales and nursery rhymes anywhere near the Bloodhaunt Forest. They're used to teach children to lock doors and windows before going to bed. Occasionally, Nightborn are seen farther away from the Bloodhaunt Forest, even beyond the regions of Delvaroth and the Ember Wastelands. These are very rare sightings, though. The voice of a Nightborn sounds hollow and distant, and they are known to be the only clear sign of individuality among these creatures: While their bodies are all extremely similar, each Nightborn has its own unique voice, ranging from high female to dark male voices. While Nightborn don't appear often, their presence usually ends with some kind of tragic and terrifying story. They always come for a specific person, often people of particular brilliance or creativity. Many Nightborn seem to enjoy frightening their victims. A few nights (sometimes just a few hours) before the creature comes for its prey, a scroll with the person's name on it might appear in their home. Even if it's burned or otherwise destroyed, the scroll just reappears the next night. The Nightborn always seem to find their chosen victims, unless they protect themselves from divination magic. Even then, these creatures are relentless hunters, rarely giving up on a prey. What drives them, and why they choose certain people, is unknown. When they find a person, they usually don't care about any other enemies - unless forced to. Their goal is to steal their victim's soul. Once they achieve that, they try to leave as quickly as they can. Rumors are that they create new Nightborn with the souls of their victims, but this might just be the fabrication of yet another scary tale. In the rare cases that someone managed to kill a Nightborn, their bodies burnt up in blue flames, leaving nothing but - curiously purple-colored - ash. There are also rumors that even slain Nightborn returned after a while, but those are probably just exagerrations to make the scary stories more interesting.
