# Drainberry Bush

**description**: Drainberry bushes are floating plants that originate from the First World, with long, thorny vines and dense clusters of bright-red berries. Their hollow thorns quickly siphon blood, which is how these carnivorous plants feed, and they rapidly turn consumed blood into fresh batches of delicious berries. Drainberry bushes exude a faint white glow that is a result of stored positive energy. Drainberry bushes exhibit unusually high intelligence and have an astute sense of value. They typically become insulted when creatures attempt to harvest their berries. Creatures that attempt to converse with drainberry bushes find that the plants telepathically convey only short and simple phrases such as, “Money please,” “Deal good,” “Deal no good,” “Want that,” (with a gesture toward an item it covets), “Thank you, customer,” and if necessary, “No refunds.” Though a drainberry bush considers the market value of its berries to be 250 gp each, it greatly prefers interesting art objects as payment—even ones of significantly lower value. Traits Hide in Plain Sight. While the drainberry bush remains motionless, it gains advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks to appear to be an ordinary bush. Nature Empathy. The drainberry bush can speak telepathically with animals and sentient plant creatures (as speak with animals ). Blood Berries. The drainberry bush must drain blood from living creatures for sustenance. This causes clusters of bright red, highly nutritious berries to grow among its branches. Each cluster of berries lasts for 1 day, and a drainberry bush typically has 3 (1d4+1) clusters when encountered. When consumed, a cluster restores 14 (2d8+5) hit points and removes one level of exhaustion. A creature can pluck a cluster of berries with a successful unarmed melee attack or Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check against the bush’s AC. (If the drainberry bush is not hostile, it is also possible to purchase blood berries from it at a typical price of 200 gp per cluster.) Consume Berries. As a bonus action, the bush draws nourishment from one cluster of its own blood berries, regaining 14 (2d8+5) hit points. That berry cluster wrinkles and dies. Drain Blood. As a bonus action, if it has grappled at least one creature, the drainberry bush can use its hollow thorns to siphon blood from the grappled creatures. For each grappled creature, a new cluster of blood berries immediately grows on the drainberry bush. All creatures grappled by the drainberry bush have some of their blood taken and must attempt a DC 16 Constitution saving throw to resist the effects of blood loss. On a failed save, a creature takes 14 (2d8+5) necrotic damage and gains one level of exhaustion. Actions Multiattack. The drainberry bush makes three attacks with its vines. Vine. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6+4) bludgeoning damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 14).
