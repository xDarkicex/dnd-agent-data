# Medici Guard

**description**: This is a very organised group of 50 well-trained fighters under the command of a captain. 20 of them will be in the palace at the time of the adventurers' arrival. Two guards are positioned in front of every closed gate and two are on the inside, ready to call for backup or to let visitors enter. There are an additional 4 guards in the garden to prevent anyone climbing over the walls at the back of the estate. A few more will be in the guard’s quarters. The rest can quickly be mobilised in the case of a severe attack, by sending a runner to the guards’ quarters nearby. An enemy might try to block or intercept such an attempt though, which would at least delay a response. The Medici guards are dressed in the family’s colors, yellow and red, decorated with the coat of arms: the famous palle – six red balls on a gold background – representing the supposed heritage of the family as medical practitioners. The guards are carrying swords and round shields and are equipped with chain mail hauberks. The captain will wear the same, unless they are called to arms with some advance warning, in which case he will don his plate armor. The captain is a distant relative of the family: Francesco Tornabuoni, a young proud man with long blond hair in a ponytail. Traits Protection. When a creature a guard can se e attacks a target other than him that is within 5 feet of the guard, the Medici guard can use his reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. Second Wind. On his turn, a guard can use a bonus action to regain 12 (1d10 + 7) hit points once per day. Battle Master (5 superiority dice). Once per attack, a Medici guard can use a superiority dice to perform the following maneuvers as the fighter class ability (if a saving throw is required the DC is 13): lunging at tack, maneuvering attack, parry, pushing attack, riposte. Actions Multiattack. The Medici guards make two weapon attacks. Longsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 3) bludgeoning damage.
