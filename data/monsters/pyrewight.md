# Pyrewight

**description**: Denizens of the Underworld Many of the undead of Grimnir serve a somewhat different role than they do in other worlds. While on the surface skeletons and zombies exist as dangers to the living, their existence is more nuanced. Many of the dead are agents of Hel and scour the surface world at her bidding. They gather information, they help her collect the unfortunate and forgotten, and they defend her interests—and those of Grimnir. Yet, despite this semblance of civility, the undead are still frightening and the raiders avoid them. The walking dead are something they simply don’t understand and some fear that one day the undead will rise to help consume the world. This is a worrying concept and one that is often on the mind of any raider who travels near territory haunted by the once-living. That is not to say that undead are never vile, never evil. There are many examples of the dead disregarding Hel’s mercy and love. These tend to cause the same sorts of disruptions that one might expect in more typical worlds. Pretty much any undead that might be found elsewhere can be found in Grimnir and Hel does not control them—even within the Underworld they have free will. And some decide to be terrible. In addition to standard undead, the world of Grimnir has spawned several unique flavors, from the cursed knight to the pyrewights. Many of these are created because of the breaking or bending of an oath, or because a duty was not honored. Undead Nature. Like undead in other realms, these undead do not require air, food, drink, or sleep. Pyrewight Pyrewights were raiders that attempted to deceive their way into the Halls of the Slain by having their corpse burned with weapons after they died dishonorably. Often the pyrewight themselves did not choose to dishonor the gods in this way—friends, family, or a grieving spouse might begin the ritual—yet they are made to suffer for all of eternity regardless. Pyrewights have thin, leathery skin over their face and necrotic energy emanates from their bodies. They often wield the same weapons and armor they did in life. Unending Pain. The pyrewight suffers continual, near agonizing pain. Those that have walked the world a long while have learned to master that pain and channel it into a Flameshade—a semi-corporeal version of themselves that can carry objects and even fight as an extension of the pyrewight itself. Intelligent Destroyers. A spellcaster might control a pyrewight and use them as a lieutenant, for these undead are as intelligent as they were in life—perhaps more so, as they have learned many new lessons. Voice of Warning. Any who speak to a pyrewight will often hear their pitiful tale and should take it as a warning not to follow in their steps.
