# Ozaena

**description**: The ozaena is a species of giant octopus of significant intelligence that has developed glands that release a foul oil whose stench both deters predators and handicaps prey. It resembles a purplish mass while in the water, its roiling tentacles giving off a rancid stench. The skin of the ozaena is thick and wrinkled, similar to an elephant’s, with colors ranging from deep blues to dark purples and blacks, typically in mottled patterns. The ozaena, like many of its cephalopod cousins, can change its colors to mimic its surroundings, allowing it to hide and ambush prey. The body of a full grown ozaena can be the size of a bull, with tentacles nearly twice the length of its body. Clever Hunter. An ozaena is a cunning predator. The creature can assess situations and triggers that attract the attention of prey, and use that knowledge to its advantage. For example, it may use bait to attract prey. This could be plants or other dead creatures set out to attract aquatic prey, or a loose boat it keeps close by holding a tow rope, to attract humanoids seeking to retrieve it.
