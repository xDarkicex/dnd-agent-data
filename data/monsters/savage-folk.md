# Savage Folk

**description**: The druids of the Death Marshes have a unique understanding of life. They see beauty in the Bloodthorn , shadows of the forest, and the idea of the elimination of the weak. When an outsider chooses to live with them, or when they capture an outsider and decide to change them into a weapon that can protect their forest, the druids transform the outsider into a force of nature through a series of druidic rituals. As creatures of nature and the shadows, the savage folk resemble humanoid bears made of shadows. They show no mercy to those who harm nature, especially in the Death Marshes. They are strongly connected to their creator druids and obey their commands because of respect, as if they are dominated by the druids. Savage folk cannot reproduce, but they can choose others for their family and love them just as a parent would a genetic child. Player Characters As Savage Folk A player character transformed into savage folk retains its statistics, except that their Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution scores become 18, Intelligence score becomes 4, Wisdom score becomes 12, and Charisma score becomes 4. Also, the character gains the savage folk’s speed, damage resistances, darkvision, traits, and actions. The character’s alignment becomes neutral evil, but the GM might choose to change this to better fit their game.
