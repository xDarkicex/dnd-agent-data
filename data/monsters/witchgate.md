# Witchgate

**description**: Witchgate While the witches believe that the Witchking and their Matron have their best interests in mind and seek, like they, to cover the world of Grimnir with ironheart trees and wrestle control away from the raiders, they are mistaken. The witches are simply tools used by Boða. And as tools, they may be utilized in many different ways. As an ironwood witch matures and grows in power, they become formidable and the essence of their power ripens. They also begin to become a threat to Boða, rivaling her in power. These witches always inevitably disappear, though other witches simply believe that they are traversing the world and furthering the coven’s agenda. Nothing could be further from the truth. Expendable. Boða corrupts these witches and transforms them into vessels, from which she draws forth excess vitality, as required. The most powerful of these vessels is known as a witchgate, requiring the sacrifice of six powerful ironwood witches. Gateway. A witchgate connects to another witchgate on the same plane of existence, allowing instantaneous travel between two locations (while all six witches remain alive). Doomed. Nothing can be done to save the witches once they have been transformed in this way. They will persist as a vessel, to be used. Once their life force is extinguished, they die. A Means to an End Witchgates are used in Chapter 9, as part of the plot involving blood shadows destroying the Sun and the Moon. Refer to page 276 to review these details.
