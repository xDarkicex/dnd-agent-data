# Masca

**description**: Masche are uncharacteristic non-evil hags who live in villages along humans or in isolated shacks in the country, with a gentle smile on their faces most of the time, though accompanied by an odd glint in their eyes. Though a masca (also called a village hag) can be capricious and vengeful, they don’t harbor any special desire to harm people. A masca could act as a hamlet’s wise woman, curing diseases and giving advice; indistinguishable from a common woman by day, she will leave her home at night to andare in masca (assume her true identity) and fare la fisica (practice magic), taking other forms and sending her soul to fly very far from her physical body. Masche are jealous of their art and don’t like to be disturbed while practicing it, receiving inappropriate questions about their magic and private life, or being insulted with names as “strega” (witch) or “megera” (hag). A masca will most likely curse someone who is excessively curious or rude. They are also able to see the past and future by browsing their book of command, and can warn people about danger or ill-willed spirits in their vicinity, though in doing so they risk being mistaken for the actual source of danger or evil. Country people can live along a masca for years, respecting her and turning to her for help, or can suddenly decide masche are evil and hunt them to burn at the stake, often killing many innocent women in the process. Family Matters. A masca will most often choose a husband and raise a normal family in a village. According to tradition, masche could be immortal if they didn’t pass their book of command on to a heir, but such a masca would only continue to grow older and frailer without the chance to end her sufferings. What is certain is that a masca will always ensure to find a worthy successor and transmit the book of command to her before dying. Sometimes the book is replaced by another symbol of power, such as a ball of wool, a broomstick, or a ladle. Typically, the masca’s heir is chosen among her family or descendants, like a daughter or granddaughter, and transforms into a masca when receiving her legacy. A masca could also select a man as her heir, but these masconi are rare and lack the ability to pass their power on in their turn, so a mascone’s power and bloodline finishes with him. Closed Covens. Masche only form covens with other masche and not with any other types of hags.
