# Murder of Crows

**description**: Crows are common in the Stolen Lands and do not normally pose a danger to explorers of these wilds. Yet when a particularly large flock of them gathers, this murder of crows can become a dangerous threat. Large gatherings of crows are particularly drawn to towns or villages that have been recently abandoned, whether or not there’s a large amount of carrion left behind to feed on. Traits Enraged Cunning. A murder of crows can hound its prey through most barriers. Simple latches, unsecured chimney flues, loosely shuttered windows, and similar obstacles rarely keep a murder of crows away. A murder of crows automatically bypasses many of these simple obstructions and can attempt a DC 15 Dexterity ability check to get through more complex obstructions (such as closed windows or simple barricades). Distracting Horde. Creatures that share the swarm’s space or are within 5 feet of it have disadvantage on attack rolls and Constitution saving throws to maintain concentration. The area of the swarm is considered difficult terrain. Swarm. The Swarm can occupy another creature’s space and vice versa, and the swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny beast. The swarm can’t regain hit points or gain temporary hit points. Actions Multiattack. The swarm can attack every creature in its space with swarming beaks. Swarming Beaks. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 0 ft., one creature in the swarm’s space. Hit: 16 (4d6+2) piercing damage, or 9 (2d6+2) piercing damage if the swarm has half of its hit points or lower, and the creature must succeed on a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw or become blinded until the end of the swarm’s next turn.
