# Bugbear Slaver

**description**: Bugbears tend to fight alongside the Shadow’s legions as mercenaries and irregular auxiliaries. Many tend to be happy to fight for pay, but as the occupation of Eredane has stretched on, many have turned to other work. Bugbear slavers roam the human lands looking for people to kidnap and sell as slave labor to the Shadow. Using other Goblinoids in Midnight The goblinoids presented here represent bugbears, goblins, and hobgoblins in some of their more iconic roles in Midnight . However, other profiles for bugbears and goblins especially are equally appropriate, and can also be used in the setting. GMs should be careful when using other hobgoblin profiles, since hobgoblins in other settings tend to be more martial and focused on their own legions. In Midnight, they are almost always auxiliaries to the orc legions, and do not form independent military units. Humanoids and Forces of the Shadow Humanoids make up the bulk of intelligent creatures found in Eredane. This broad categorization includes humans and all of the fey, orcs, and goblinoids. Many of the common folk go their entire lives without seeing a different type of creature—and they prefer it that way. Even adventurers, for all of their travels, are far more likely to encounter humanoids than any other creatures. There simply are not that many of the other types around. The history of the world can be said to be that of the humanoids, since it was they who kept the records and they who have performed the great deeds and participated in the great tragedies of the world. Humanoids of Eredane Many of the most numerous humanoids in Eredane are detailed earlier in this book ( Races of Midnight ). However, there are other intelligent humanoid cultures in Eredane, and a few of them are detailed here. In addition, most of the forces of the Shadow’s armies are some form of humanoid. So profiles for some of the more common of the Shadow’s minions are presented in this section. Traits Brute. A melee weapon deals one extra die of its damage when the bugbear hits with it (included in the attack). Kidnapper. The bugbear has advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks to grapple a creature who cannot see it. The bugbear can move with a grappled creature at their normal speed without provoking opportunity attacks. Actions Morningstar. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d8+2) bludgeoning damage. Net. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 5/15 ft., one target. Hit: If the target is large or smaller, it is restrained until it or another creature within 5 feet uses its action to make a DC 10 Strength check, or it or another creature within 5 feet deals 5 slashing damage to the net (AC 10).
