# Laapizan Footsoldier

**description**: These blue-scaled dragonblooded turn to stone on death, trapping the weapons of their enemies in their bodies. They are by far the most numerous and weakest of all dragonblooded, and serve as footsoldiers of varying quality for their creators. Gliders. Although dragonblooded have wings, the majority of them are incapable of true flight and are only able to glide. Except for the Voludan, a dragonblooded’s fly speed can’t be used to gain altitude, and they drop 5 feet per round that they remain aloft. Tactics. The tactics used by the dragonblooded vary by their type, but can be generally summarized as single-minded in service to their creator. Each dragonblooded seeks to make the best use of their death as possible, and may even hasten their own demise when escape seems impossible if it means harming a greater number of foes in the process. Traits Stony Entrapment. Upon death, the laapizan turns to stone, falling to the ground. Unless it was slain with a Death Blow or by an attack that would Injure it, if its killer attacked it with a melee weapon, that creature must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or have its weapon be trapped in the laapizan’s body. A weapon trapped in the body of a laapizan can be retrieved with a DC 15 Strength check, or by smashing the body to pieces over the course of several minutes with stoneworking tools. Actions Shortsword. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6+3) slashing damage.
