# Mazapegul

**description**: These tiny fiends were once benevolent fey, brownies or sprites, captured by demonic forces and twisted into their cruel forms by abyssal rituals. They enjoy terrorizing animals and humanoids, especially those in remote, rural areas. A mazapégul will often choose or “adopt” a humanoid or family of humanoids, committing all sorts of mischief to them, their household, goods, and livestock, and feeding upon the dreams of the humanoids at night. A mazapégul is about the size of an average house cat, with similar fur and markings, and curved retractable claws. Its actual form resembles a monkey, with prehensile tail, and long, dexterous fingers and toes. Its face is humanoid, wrinkled and old, looking, with bright, yellow eyes. Haunters of Sleep. The mazapégul can gain sustenance from the dreams of humanoid creatures. The mazapégul climbs on a sleeping victim’s torso, sits, and gazes at the sleeping humanoid, allowing its mind to enter the victim’s dreams, feeding on their psychic energy and giving the humanoid nightmares. Even if its victim awakens, the mazapégul can force its will onto the victim, holding it in place in a waking nightmare while it continues to feed. Fey Tendencies. Despite their fiendish nature, some mazapégul cling to the traditions of their ancestors. Like brownies, they may assist their “adopted” humanoids in exchange for the food they receive (even though it is taken without consent). A humanoid may find its sleep terrorized by the presence of a mazapégul, only to wake and find the house swept and cleaned, or other chores completed for them.
