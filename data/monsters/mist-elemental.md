# Mist Elemental

**description**: These creatures are considered strange even amongst elementals. They are only born in the Material Plane, near very magically-charged places where mist has settled. They are very slim compared to their fellow elementals, but are slightly taller, with a vaguely humanoid appearance except for their bottom half which becomes a trail of mist that fades into the air. This has led to many terrified commoners to incorrectly assume they’ve had an encounter with a ghost. Interestingly, they show magic prowess that no other lesser elemental possesses. Capable of casting arcane and divine magic to a small degree. However, they are notably peaceful and never attack unless they are defending themselves. Loose ties with Divinity. There are rumors that connect mist elementals with Hecate, the Titan-Goddess of Magic. It is unclear if the connection is real, but some worshippers seem to believe in it. Elementals These beings are intrinsically tied with the domains of various gods, even said to be divine servants at times. They are the object of study of many wizards and theologists for this reason. About the Greek Elementals These elementals very much resemble those present in the Monster Manual and work on similar mechanical structures. Elemental Nature: An elemental doesn’t require air, food, drink, or sleep. Subject to Conjuration: An spellcaster can summon these elementals through the conjure elementals spell, by using an appropriate spell slot to the Challenge Rating of each elemental. Traits Mist Form. The elemental can enter a hostile creature's space and stop there. It can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide without squeezing. Spellcasting. The protector's Spellcasting Ability is Charisma (spell save DC 15, +7 to hit with spell attacks). It can cast the following Spells, requiring no material components: At Will: fog cloud, misty step 3/day each: magic missile (as a 3rd level spell), shield 2/day each: bless, counterspell, dispel magic, guiding bolt, shield of faith, silence Magic Resistance. The bomber has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Actions Multiattack. The elemental makes two arcane palm attacks or two magic bullet attacks. Arcane Palm. Melee Spell Attack: +7 to hit, reach 5ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage plus 14 (4d6) force damage. The target loses concentration on a spell it has cast. Magic Bullet. Ranged Spell Attack: +7 to hit, reach 60/120ft., one target. Hit: 14 (3d6 + 4) force damage. Sapping Mist (Recharge 5-6). The elemental unleashes a 30-foot radius magical mist, which heavily obscures a spherical area around the elemental. Any breathing creature that ends its turn in the mist must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 21 (6d6) force damage and the elemental regains hit points equal to half the damage dealt.
