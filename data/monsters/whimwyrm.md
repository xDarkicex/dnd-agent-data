# Whimwyrm

**description**: Whimwyrms are unpredictable dragons native to the whimsically dangerous First World. While their coloration and appearance might evoke reactions of adoration, their moods shift quickly from one extreme to the other. Many who have assumed that their adorability and relatively small size (as far as dragons are concerned) equates to safety have met swift and grisly ends thanks to a whimwyrm’s fangs or magic. A whimwyrm is a slender, sleek dragon, not much longer than 7 feet from snout to tail, but its speed in combat is unnerving. When a whimwyrm attacks, be it with its fanged mouth or its long tail, it makes several quick, darting snaps or lashes at once. These attacks consist of multiple bites or lashings but resolve as a single Strike. A single attack from a whimwyrm can leave a creature covered in tiny but deep bloody wounds. Whimwyrms prefer to resolve conflicts with jokes or pranks, hoping to achieve a victory over a foe via humiliation without resorting to combat, but any damage or insult sent a whimwyrm’s way is a sure invitation for violent retaliation. A whimwyrm’s sense of humor vanishes immediately when it is the butt of a joke, or when anyone implies that they missed the point of a whimwyrm’s jest or didn’t get the punchline. Traits Fog Vision. Fog and mist, including from spells, don’t affect the whimwyrm’s vision. Innate Spellcasting. The whimwyrm’s spell casting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 21). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: dancing lights, message, minor illusion 3/day each: cloudkill, confusion 1/day each: prismatic spray Legendary Resistance (3/Day). If the whimwyrm fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead. Actions Multiattack. The whimwyrm makes four attacks: one with its bite and three with its tail. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 27 (5d8+5) piercing damage. Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 27 (5d8+5) slashing damage and the target must succeed on a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw or the whimwyrm can disarm the target of one object it is holding. The object falls up to 10 feet away from the target in a space of the whimwyrm’s choosing. Mind-Numbing Breath (Recharge 5–6). The whimwyrm exhales a 40-foot cone of sparkling magenta mist. A creature within the area must make a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw, taking 55 (10d10) psychic damage and becoming blinded for 1 minute on a failure. On a successful save, a creature takes half the amount of damage and is not blinded. A blinded creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. Additionally, any creatures in this area that fail their saving throw that were invisible become visible for 1 minute as if any effects causing the invisibility were paused; all such effects resume once the minute ends.
