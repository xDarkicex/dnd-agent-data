# Werendegar

**description**: Werendegar is a type of fey known as a gimmerling. His stats work great as is for a completely unique villain from the First World, with his skills and supernatural abilities making him an excellent choice for a doppelganger-like role in a high-level campaign. Traits Brute. A melee weapon deals one extra die of its damage when Werendegar hits with it (included in the attack). Hungersense. Werendegar has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks made against creatures that require food to live. Innate Spellcasting. Werendegar’s innate spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 21, +13 to hit with spell attacks). Werendegar can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components: At will: invisibility (self only), shield 3/day: dispel magic, irresistible dance, suggestion 1/day: heal, mass suggestion, plane shift, teleport, wish Magic Resistance. Werendegar has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Magic Weapons. Werendegar’s weapon attacks are magical. Shapechanger. Werendegar can use his action to polymorph into a Small or Medium humanoid he has seen, or back into his true form. His statistics, other than his size, are the same in each form. Any equipment he is wearing or carrying isn’t transformed. Werendegar reverts to his true form if he dies. Sneak Attack (1/Turn). Werendegar deals an extra 21 (6d6) damage when it hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of the creature that isn’t incapacitated and Werendegar doesn’t have disadvantage on the attack roll. Thorny Aura. Thorny roots and barbed vines writhe and twist in a 15-foot radius around Werendegar. When a creature moves into this area for the first time or ends its turn in it, the creature must make a DC 21 Dexterity saving throw or take 14 (4d6) piercing damage. Werendegar is not affected by his own thorny aura. Actions Multiattack. Werendegar makes three attacks: one with his rapier, one with a claw, and one with a bite. Rapier. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d6+6) piercing damage. Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d6+6) slashing damage. Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d6+6) piercing damage. Reactions Disarm. If Werendegar successfully hits a creature with his rapier and that creature is wielding a weapon, he can opt to deal no damage and instead cause the creature to make a DC 21 Strength saving throw. If the creature fails, they drop their weapon to the ground.
