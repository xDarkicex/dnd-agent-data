# Sleuth's Shadowcoat

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon (requires attunement) Component: undead (shadow) ethereal ichor Sometimes, a detective’s best tool is remaining undetected. The sleuth’s shadowcoat allows its wearer to take full advantage of shadows, to hide in plain sight, and to disappear in a puff of smoke (well… fog) when things go awry. Also, it has plenty of secret and obvious pockets in which to store things. Very useful. This item has 9 charges and regains 1d6 + 3 expended charges daily at dawn. Shadow Stealth. While wearing this coat in dim light or darkness, you can use a bonus action to expend 1 charge and turn invisible until the start of your next turn. This invisibility ends if you enter an area of bright light, or if you attack or cast a spell. If you are still invisible at the start of your next turn, you can choose to immediately expend 1 charge (no action required) to prolong the effect for another turn. Hide in Plain Sight. While wearing the coat, you can expend 2 charges to cast the chameleon skin * spell on yourself. Disappearing Act. While wearing the coat, you can expend 2 charges to cast the fog cloud spell centred on yourself. When you cast the spell in this way, it only lasts up to 1 minute, and you can immediately move up to half your speed. *See Appendix A
