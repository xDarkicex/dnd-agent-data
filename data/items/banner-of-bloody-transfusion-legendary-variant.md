# Banner of Bloody Transfusion (legendary variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement) Component: phial of humanoid blood For the courageous combat nurses of the Night Hospitaller, ward work is but a pastime between battles. Armed with a pure white banner emblazoned with a blood-red sigil, a Hospitaller war chaplain is a rallying point on the battlefield: their magic helps keep fear of death a distant nightmare. Crafted from a tough, stain-proof, white fabric, this velvet-trimmed banner measures 5 feet high and 3 feet wide. An embroidered depiction of a blood sac becomes a three-dimensional sac containing a blood-based potion while the banner is unfurled. It can be attached to a 10-foot pole to serve as a standard. Furling or unfurling the banner requires an action. While unfurled, the banner has the following properties: Mark of Vitality. You can touch the banner as a bonus action, causing it to emanate vitality. For 10 minutes or until the banner is furled, you and your allies have advantage on Constitution saving throws while within 20 feet of the banner. Once this property of the banner has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Stabilising Standard. While within 20 feet of the banner, you can use a bonus action to stabilise a creature you can see that has 0 hit points and is within 20 feet of the banner. Transfusion. You can touch the banner and mentally command its transfusion hose to connect with one willing or unconscious creature within 20 feet of the banner, choosing one of the following effects: Heal. As a bonus action, you cause the creature to regain 4d6 hit points. Restore. As an action, you cast the lesser restoration spell on the creature. After this property of the banner has been activated, roll a d6. On a 3 or lower, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Legendary variant: Increase the range of the banner to 30 feet, the duration of Mark of Vitality to 1 hour, and the hit points regained by the Transfusion property’s Heal option to 8d6. You can use the Restore option to cast greater restoration instead.
