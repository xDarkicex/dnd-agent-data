# Taz's Zealous Retribution (rare variant)

**description**: Wand, uncommon (optional attunement by a spellcaster) Component: construct lifespark A common field tool, the TZR, as it is referred to by FBI agents, is the creation of the Bureau’s very own inventor: Tazon Regalton. Designed primarily for incapacitating fleeing evil-doers, the TZR also packs a punch when necessary. Stun Baton. You can use this wand as a simple melee weapon with the finesse property, dealing 1 piercing damage on a hit. Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack using the wand, you can cause it to discharge in one of the following ways: Sap. The creature must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, its speed is reduced to 0 feet and it can’t take bonus actions or reactions until the end of its next turn. A creature immune to being stunned automatically succeeds on this saving throw. Zap. The creature takes an extra 2d8 lightning damage and can’t take reactions until the start of its next turn unless it is immune to being stunned. Optional Attunement. If you are a spellcaster, you can attune to this item, and you can use the Stunspell property while you remain attuned to it. OA: Stunspell. You know the hold person and lightning wave* spells, and you always have them prepared. While holding the wand, you can cast one of those spells without expending a spell slot, using your spell save DC. Once this property of the wand has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Rare variant: Increase the DC to 15 and the damage to 3d8. Add stunbolt * to the list of spells in the Stunspell property. Once you use the Stunspell property, roll a d6. If the result is equal to or lower than the spell’s level, this property can’t be used again until the next dawn.
