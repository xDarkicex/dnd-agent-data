# Poison Pod

**description**: Pizmutiaas, rare Even in still caverns, the bulbous stalks of the poison pod fungi gently sway on imperceptible Underworld breezes. The germinated spore pods alight with a soft glow separate from the stalk, and float away on the faintest of air currents. Light Aura. Individual pizmutiaas spore pods cast little light on their own, less than a normal candle. But a stalk in full bloom emits a green phosphorescent illumination that acts as a beacon to creatures in the darkness of Underworld in the hopes of dislodging is spore pods and distributing its seed. Spore Cloud. At the slightest touch or impact, the pods burst into a scintillating spore cloud of caustic vapor in a 30-foot radius. A character who inhales the caustic vapor of a pizmutiaas spore cloud becomes poisoned, is unable to breathe, and takes 3 (1d6) poison damage on its turn. A character poisoned from caustic vapor can make a successful DC 15 Constitution saving throw at the end of each turn to end the ongoing damage and regain its breath, but the character remains poisoned for the next 24 hours. Pizpods. Capturing these spores without rupturing them is nearly impossible, but certain funglet alchemists have mastered the technique. By bottling them in a brittle skein, the pods become moderately durable, enabling them to be used as a weapon. As an action, you can throw a pizpod up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. Make a ranged attack against a creature or object, treating the pzipod as an improvised weapon. On a hit, the target is subject to a caustic vapor spore cloud.
