# Umbrasand Armor

**description**: Created by the thieves’ guilds of Tar-Jurani, the Umbrasand Armor is designed to help rogues. Umbrasand is a pitch black material magically created from desert sand. When a masterwork leather armor is rubbed with Umbrasand for eight hours, it is slowly imbued with the sand’s properties. Armor of a lesser quality would be destroyed in the process. The leather turns pitch black, and dark mists constantly surround the armor. The mists grant magical protection, increasing the armor class, as well as granting advantage on Stealth saving throws to avoid visual detection. As an action, the wearer can expand these mists into a sphere with a five to ten feet radius centered on the armor. While light exists normally within the area of effect, allowing the wearer to see, it is blocked from leaving the sphere. For the caster, this means they cannot see beyond the area of effect. From the outside, the effect resembles a Darkness spell. Expanding and retracting the dark mists as an action can be repeated indefinitely. There is no time limit on how long the mists can stay expanded. Base Armor Class: 14 Luay kneeled in front of the door, his lock pick slowly moving in the keyhole. The night was extraordinarily dark, thick clouds hiding the emerald moon. The glowing light of the Flamegazer Halfling’s body provided him with enough illumination to see what he was doing. He tried to calm himself. Breaking into a prison was stupid and dangerous, but who else was supposed to free his friends? Then he felt it. The first lock mechanism had moved, and the door was almost open. Just a few more seconds… but then he heard the voices. Two of the guards had just come out the front entrance. He quickly moved away from the door, deeper into the dark alleyway. There was nothing to hide behind, but he had his Umbrasand Armor. Luay whispered the command word, and the sphere of darkness spread around him. Everything beyond five feet away from him was hidden behind a pitch black orb of magic. The guards came closer, their armor giving away every step they made. They approached the entrance to the alley. “Hey, wait”, one of them said. Luay silently cursed, and reached for his dagger. “What’s up?” “That alley… it’s strange. It’s so dark over there.” “Seriously?” The other guard sneered. “It’s a dark alley on a dark night. Don’t be paranoid. Come on, I’m hungry.” A few more seconds passed. Then the guards moved on. Luay took a deep breath. Yes, he loved his Umbrasand Armor. He wasn’t proud about how he acquired it, but he’d never give it away again.
