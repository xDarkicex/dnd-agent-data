# Sawbones' Bonesaw Longsword

**description**: Weapon (any sword that deals slashing damage), very rare (requires attunement) Component: giant tooth Speckled with rust and blood, this magical saw has a wickedly keen, serrated edge. It is said to be the instrument of choice of back alley doctors and black market suppliers. Amputation operations can be conducted almost instantaneously; plenty of time before the town guard started sticking their nose in. You have advantage on Medicine checks made to perform surgical procedures using this sword. Amputation. When you hit a creature with this weapon, you can speak its command word, causing it to momentarily become incredibly sharp and able to cut through limbs with even a glancing blow. The target must make a DC 16 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes an extra 2d6 slashing damage and loses a randomly determined limb (use the table below). On a success, it takes half as much damage and doesn’t lose a limb. Creatures with legendary resistances automatically succeed on this saving throw. Once this property has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. How the creature is affected depends on the creatures’ anatomy and is up to the GM’s discretion. If the GM decides the creature can’t lose a limb, this property deals an extra 6d6 slashing damage instead of 2d6. Below are examples for a humanoid. d20 Limb Effect 1-9 Leg The target’s walking speed is halved and it has disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws. 10-19 Arm The target drops whatever it is holding in this hand (if any) and has disadvantage on attack rolls using its arms. If the target has a feature or trait that allows it to make multiple attacks using its arms, it makes one fewer. 20 Head You cut off one of the creature’s heads. The creature dies if it can’t survive without the lost head. Avoiding Amputation If you don’t want to include amputation in your game, replace the slashing damage with psychic damage and have the Amputation property instead temporarily disable the target’s limb until it finishes a short or long rest.
