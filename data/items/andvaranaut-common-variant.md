# Andvaranaut (common variant)

**description**: Ring, uncommon Component: celestial bone Glowing with tiny motes of pulsing light, this brilliant golden ring is inscribed with runes of wealth and prosperity. Two legends follow this ring. The first tells of humble folk using the ring to lead modest lives free from discomfort and hardship. The second tells of mighty leaders brought low by their own greed. Which is true—if either—is anyone’s guess. Andvari’s Gift. As a bonus action, you can speak the ring’s command word—“plunder”—to form a trail of softly glowing golden dust that leads to the nearest source of gold worth at least 5 gp that isn’t currently carried or worn by a creature. The trail extends out 100 feet from you, growing as you follow it. The trail can’t pass through solid objects but will navigate through holes as small as 1 inch in diameter. If all paths to the gold source are blocked by solid objects, the trail ends as close to the gold as possible. The trail disappears immediately if the gold source is destroyed, transported to another plane, or picked up by a creature. Curse. This ring is cursed with Andvari’s avarice. When you use the Andvari’s Gift property, if it’s the third time it’s been used in the last 24 hours, you become attuned to the ring, extending the curse to you until you are targeted by the remove curse spell or similar magic. If you are already attuned to the maximum number of magic items, you must choose one uncursed item to end your attunement to. As long as you remain cursed, you are unwilling to part with the ring, keeping it within reach at all times. In addition, you suffer from an affliction known as dragopathy, which causes you to become uncompromisingly greedy, using all your cunning to keep as much treasure for yourself as possible, and grants you disadvantage on Wisdom checks and Wisdom saving throws. Common variant: Remove the curse. The Andvari’s Gift property only forms a trail to a source of gold if it is within 100 feet of you, and once it has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn.
