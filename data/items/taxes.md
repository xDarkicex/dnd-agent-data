# Taxes

**description**: Weapon (Light Hammer), Rare (Requires Attunement) This light hammer has a smooth, round pewter handle and a head made from a stack of copper coins. It is cold to the touch and surprisingly heavy for the hammer’s size. When you attune to this magic weapon, you gain the flaw “I am stingy with my money.” You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. The weapon has 3 charges, and regains all expended charges when you finish a long rest. When you hit a creature with an attack using this weapon, you can expend 1 charge to cause 1d4 gold coins to fall to the ground with a distinctive ring. If you expend a charge when scoring a critical hit, a small purse filled with 4d6 gold pieces falls instead. When the coins fall, the target must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target is distracted by the coins, immediately falling prone and spending their next turn trying to retrieve the coins. Encounter Ideas Privileged Predicament. A high-ranking gnome official named Hicktor hires the characters to find Taxes, which was stolen from their treasury. They promise to pay a handsome reward for finding the hammer and returning it. Rough Tax Bracket. A few exasperated townsfolk tell the party a tale of a local tax collector named Krohn (orog), known for his particularly cruel collection techniques. If someone can’t pay, he (literally) beats the money out of them using Taxes. The scared citizens offer the characters a reward for setting the tax collector straight. Avaricious Armorer. Tabitha, a dwarf blacksmith in town, works late into the night, using the hammer to amass a small fortune while keeping everyone within earshot of the store awake. Not only are the townsfolk fed up with the noise, but she’s also recently raised her rates to the point of being unaffordable.
