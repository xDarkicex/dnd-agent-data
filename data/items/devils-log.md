# Devil's Log

**description**: At first glance, this appears to be nothing but a wooden piece you’d burn in a campfire or a fireplace. The only thing that gives away this log as something special are the two strange horn-like etchings at one side of the log. They constantly glow with a slight red light. Touching them sets the log ablaze without consuming it, which is why these items were highly desired in the ice desert of Varua several hundred years ago. That is, before people realized the negative effects that came with a Devil’s Log... It’s said that the items were created by a powerful devil as his part of a deal with a local warlord called Jhuun. The mortal wanted to become king of Varua, but first he wanted to weaken his enemies. Jhuun succeeded in conquering a few villages, but he was stopped by a group of heroes before he ever got close to his goal. His legacy isn’t a kingdom, it’s the Devil’s Logs that have spread throughout the world. Cursed Item Magic schools: Conjuration Cost: 800 gp Magic properties: This cursed item seems highly practical for cold nights before you find out about its negative effects. As a bonus action, you touch the horns etched into the wood, and the log ignites in a warm fire. It also ignites when getting in touch with other fire. It will burn for 8 hours and cannot be quenched by anything short of a Dispel Magic spell. The problem is that the magic doesn’t just ignite the wood. The horns turn into a fiery miniature gate to the domain of the devil who created them. Hellfire streams through the portal, engulfing the wood. The seemingly harmless fire has several effects: Any food cooked with this fire loses its nourishment. You still feel stuffed when eating it, but your body will crave for more. If you keep eating food cooked with this fire, you will starve well-fed. Anyone sleeping in a ten feet radius around the fire has to succeed on a Constitution Saving Throw against DC 10, or suffer from heavy nightmares. Add one level of exhaustion when they wake up. The fire attracts unnatural and evil creatures in a one mile radius. If you are in a forest surrounded by nothing but animals, you are fine. But if there are monsters around, they will be drawn to the fire. Getting rid of a Devil’s Log isn’t as easy as it sounds. If you just leave it behind or throw it away, it keeps appearing close to the person who activated it the last time. You might notice the log again lying in your campfire even though you never put it there, or it might just lie on a pile of other logs ready to be burned. The only way to get rid of the log is by casting a Remove Curse spell or a Greater Restoration spell. The log still isn’t destroyed, but it won’t appear near you anymore. Hunting vampires could lead you into the worst of places. Delandra stared into the vast white desert ahead of her. Snow, ice, and more snow. Varua really wasn’t a place she ever wanted to go to. At least, she was close to her goal now. The vampire hideout was less than a mile away. She would rest here for the night. Tomorrow at dawn, when the vampires were at a disadvantage, she would attack. And this night had already started promising: Delandra had found an old campfire, with a magic log just lying in the middle! It began to burn simply by touching it. A good start for her hunt.
