# Passion, the Bloodsword

**description**: Passion, the Bloodsword Melee weapon (martial, katana), Level 3 Awakened Magic Item (requires attunement) 1d8 slashing damage, Finesse, versatile (1d10). You gain a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon. Curse of Jealousy. This weapon is cursed. Wielders of Passion slowly become obsessed with the weapon. After you become attuned to Passion, if you aren’t already proficient in katanas, you gain proficiency in katanas. You are able to sense the blade’s location and direction relative to yourself from any distance as long as they are both in the same realm of reality. This curse persists even if another person attunes to the weapon. While another creature is attuned to Passion, each living creature previously attuned to it has –20 max hit points. Soul Render. While you are attuned to this item, when you reduce a creature to 0 hit points using this weapon, that creature is killed instantly. If it has a soul, its soul is trapped within the blade and spiritually devoured. That soul does not pass on to the realms of the dead, nor can it be reached with supernatural abilities that affect the dead. In addition, you instantly regain 3d8 hit points. Vile Sentience. This weapon is sentient and telepathically communicates with its wielder or any strong individuals it deems worthy of wielding it within 100 feet. It seeks to be wielded by the most powerful person it can find. At the GM’s discretion this can cause individuals near the wielder to become hostile and try to claim the sword for itself. Finesse. When making an attack with a finesse weapon, you use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls. Light. A light weapon is small and easy to handle, making it ideal for use when fighting with two weapons. Damage: 1d8 Damage Type: Slashing Modifiers: Melee Attacks +3, Melee Damage +3 Properties: Finesse, Versatile Weight: 3
