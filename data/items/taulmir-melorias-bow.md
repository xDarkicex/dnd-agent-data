# Taulmir, Meloria’s Bow

**description**: Taulmir, Meloria’s Bow Weapon (Longbow), Artifact (requires attunement) A gift of Mother Nature to her daughter Meloria before the age of mortals, Taulmir is an ageless weapon made to defend mortalkind against horrors that defy the natural order. Since the rise of the warring gods, the bow had lain dormant in the form of a seemingly mundane seed, waiting for a spark of nature magic to reawaken. This magic bow grants a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it and deals an extra 2d8 piercing damage. It scores a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20. Lifewood Weapon. Taulmir is made of eternally living wood and draws its power from Otherwood, the mystical realm of Nature. The bow is light enough to float on water, yet it is indestructible outside of Otherwood. Primeval Essence. While attuned to Taulmir, you have advantage on initiative rolls, and on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. Additionally, you are immune to disease, poison, and the poisoned condition, and have resistance to necrotic damage. Sharpleaf Arrows. While holding the bow, you can use a bonus action to create 20 sharpleaf arrows from the wood of the bow. These arrows ignore half cover and resistance to piercing damage. After 10 minutes, sharpleaf arrows bloom and transform into ordinary flowers. The Circle of Life. Taulmir is eternal and ever-living. While holding the bow, you can use an action to transform it into a plain-looking seed that is impervious to damage. If you plant this seed in dirt or sand and then water it, it sprouts a treant (CR 9) that is friendly to you after 1 minute. The treant holds Taulmir in its branches and will hand it back to you if asked. Once it gives you the bow, the treant transforms into an ordinary tree as its spirit returns to Otherwood.
