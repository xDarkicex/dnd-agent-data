# Rod of Immortals

**description**: Rod, very rare (requires attunement) These rods are crafted by the best mortal artisans over many years for the immortal overlords of the cities of Tolia. Most of them are acquired by others before any rulers can get their hands on them. Being in proximity to this rod is the closest the masses can get to feeling like the vampires and sorcerers that rule them. While attuned to this rod, you gain the following benefits: Immortality. You don’t age, and don’t require food, water or sleep to survive. Instead of sleeping, you can perform light activities during a long rest. Spellcasting. You choose two 1st level spells and one 2nd level spell from any spell list. Your spellcasting ability for these spells is Charisma. While holding the rod, you can cast these spells. Once you cast a spell in this way, you can cast it again after you finish a long rest. Drain. You can use your action to touch a creature with the rod, making a melee weapon attack against it. You are proficient with this weapon, and the weapon has the light and finesse properties. On a hit, the rod deals 1d6 blood damage and you regain hit points in the same amount. Curse. This rod is cursed with unending hunger. At the end of each long rest, roll a d20. On a 1-5, you feel immense hunger and thirst. Nothing can satiate this hunger and thirst except the life force you consume with the Drain feature, which puts the hunger on hold for 6 hours. You have disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while your hunger is not satiated. On a 6-20, nothing happens.
