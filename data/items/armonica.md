# Arm'onica

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon (requires attunement by a creature missing an arm) Component: phial of humanoid blood Pipes ‘Fourarms’ McGruff: infamous bardtender, caring cat-lover, and middling musician. Renowned for serving customers, petting his cat, and playing the harmonica simultaneously, his world was turned upside down in an unfortunate mimic-related piano tuning accident. Fortunately, a young Anton Spark was quick to Pipes’ rescue, developing a tool capable of calming rowdy clients and ousting tricksy mimics. Inspired by local folk tales of a ridiculous, six-armed, top-hat-wearing creature, this prosthesis is known as the “Arm’onica”. You must have a missing arm to attune to the arm’onica. Once attuned to it, it takes the place of your missing arm and can’t be removed against your will. In addition, you can use it as a spellcasting focus for your bard spells, and if you are a bard, you gain a +1 bonus to your Bardic Inspiration rolls. Dissonant Harmonies. As an action, you can blow into the arm’onica, choosing one of the effects below (save DC 13): Harmony’ca. You cast the calm emotions spell, which affects creatures in a 30-foot cone originating from you instead of a sphere originating from a point within range. Harm’onica. You cast the thunderwave spell at 2nd level. Once this item has been used to cast a spell, roll a d4. If the result is equal to or lower than the spell’s level, the arm’onica becomes clogged and this property can’t be used again until the next dawn, when it magically cleans itself. Very rare variant: Increase the Bardic Inspiration bonus to +2, the DC to 16, the level at which thunderwave is cast to 5th, and the d4 to a d10. Replace calm emotions with dominate person.
