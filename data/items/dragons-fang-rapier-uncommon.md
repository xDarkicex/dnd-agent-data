# Dragon's Fang Rapier (Uncommon)

**description**: Dragon's Fang Weapon (Rapier), Uncommon (Requires Attunement) You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. The bonus is increased to +2 against Dragons. This magic weapon acts as a dowsing rod for valuables, granting advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Perception) checks to find treasure. Breath Weapon. You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. The size, shape, and damage type of the exhalations you can produce are determined by the colors of the dragons whose breath weapons have progressed the sword’s rarity, as seen in the Breath Weapon table below. When you use your breath weapon, each creature in the area of the exhalation must make a DC 13 saving throw, the type of which is also determined by the dragon’s color. A creature takes 3d6 damage on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. This property recharges after you finish a short or long rest. Breath Weapon Dragon Damage Type Size/Shape/DC Black Acid 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save) Blue Lightning 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save) Brass Fire 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save) Bronze Lightning 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save) Copper Acid 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save) Gold Fire 15 ft. cone (Dex save) Green Poison 15 ft. cone (Con save) Red Fire 15 ft. cone (Dex save) Silver Cold 15 ft. cone (Con save) White Cold 15 ft. cone (Con save) Progression The Dragon’s Fang progresses when tempered in the breath of a dragon—provided the wielder survives the damage. A wyrmling’s breath increases the rarity from common to uncommon, a young dragon’s breath from uncommon to rare, and so on.
