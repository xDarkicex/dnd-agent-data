# Indigo Lotus

**description**: wondrous item, rare Found in shady pools of calm water, the indigo lotus grows on broad, flat-floating leaves, and it smells like spoilt milk. When you consume it, you have disadvantage on Wisdom ability checks and saving throws until you finish a long rest. If you are a spellcaster, you can use one application of Lotus Infused ability to gain one of the following: Increase the DC of an illusion spell you cast by 2 Cast an illusion spell as if you used a spell slot one level higher than the one you expended. Cast the spell as if you had applied one of the following metamagic benefits to it, whether or not you know the particular metamagic: Distant Spell or Subtle Spell (when stacking uses, each metamagic benefit may be chosen only once). You can use one to three uses of the indigo lotus as part of the action of casting a spell. When using the same ability boost more than once, the effects stack. If you aren’t a spellcaster, you can expend a use as a reaction when you fail a saving throw against an illusion spell. Reroll the saving throw and use the new result.
