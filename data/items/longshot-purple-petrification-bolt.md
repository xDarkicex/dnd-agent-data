# Longshot Purple Petrification Bolt

**description**: Ammunition (arrow or bolt), varies Component: phial of aberration mucus During the veinworm plague, a cult of trypanophobic disease-worshippers refused to get vaccinated, becoming reservoirs in which the pathogen could reproduce and adapt. The Night Hospitaller, a sect of nocturnal combat nurses, developed a syringe-like ammunition as a means of delivering vaccinations from afar. Since then, this clinical tool has taken many guises, including the magically self-filling longshot. When you hit a creature with a ranged weapon attack using this ammunition, the elixir within is injected into the creature, which suffers its effects depending on the type of longshot ammunition used, as described below. A creature can choose to make itself an easy target against this ammunition, giving you advantage on the attack roll. If the attack misses, the elixir is ejected and wasted. Either way, the ammunition behaves like ordinary magical ammunition until the next dawn, when it is magically refilled. Red Restoration; Uncommon. On a hit, the attack doesn’t deal any damage. Instead, the target is cured of one disease or condition (target’s choice). The condition can be blinded, deafened, paralysed, or poisoned. Teal Torpor; Rare. On a hit, this ammunition deals the attack’s normal damage. In addition, the creature must succeed a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or suffer the effects of the hold person spell until the end of its next turn. Blue Benevolence; Rare. On a hit, the attack doesn’t deal any damage. Instead, the target regains 4d4 + 4 hit points and gains the benefits of the bless spell for 1 minute. Indigo Insanity; Very rare. On a hit, this ammunition deals the attack’s normal damage. In addition, the creature must succeed a DC 16 Constitution saving throw or suffer the effects of the confusion spell for 1 minute. An affected creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. Purple Petrification; Legendary. On a hit, this ammunition deals the attack’s normal damage. In addition, the creature must succeed a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or suffer the effects of the flesh to stone spell. You must concentrate on this effect as you would on a spell.
