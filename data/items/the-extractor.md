# The Extractor

**description**: Like a fisherman shucking an oyster, the Extractor pries away layers of armor to get to the meat inside. Legend The Extractor is a maul designed to pry heavily armored foes out of their proverbial shell. The spikes attached to the head of the maul hook and tear away bits and pieces of armor, occasionally creating gaping holes, leaving even the most well-armored adversary susceptible to attacks. Location The maul was last known to be owned by Osaris Bleed, an orc who travelled the realm while earning her wage as a freelance gladiator. Osaris was last seen leaving a large city after winning a pot of gold in the arena. That was several years ago. Some believe Osaris decided to retire. She often spoke about settling down, using her winnings to purchase lush land and build a farm. Encounter Ideas Disarming Defender. The adventurers hear of an orc woman defending a small village, her weapon shredding her foes’ armor. Shredding Scofflaws. The adventurers come upon a group of bandits; one carries The Extractor and Osaris’s journal. Farm Trouble. The adventurers stumble upon Osaris’s small farm while she is out tending to her crops. The maul hangs on a wall above a stone fireplace in her humble home, booby trapped with a poison darts trap (DC 17), activated when the maul is lifted from the mantle. Progression The Extractor progresses as the creature who is attuned to it progresses. Whenever the creature’s Strength modifier increases, the weapon’s rarity also increases. Statistics Weapon (Maul), Rarity Varies (Requires Attunement) Common. Shuck the Oyster. When you score a critical hit with this magic weapon against a creature wearing magical or nonmagical ar­mor, roll a d6. On a 1, the armor is partially damaged and takes a permanent and cumulative −1 penalty to the AC it offers. Armor reduced to an AC of 10 offers no protection until it is repaired. Uncommon. Shuck the Oyster now reduces AC on a roll a 1 or a 2. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon and it deals an extra 1 piercing damage on a hit. Rare. Dig Deep. When you hit a creature that is wearing armor with an attack using this weapon, it deals an additional 1d6 piercing damage to the target. In addition, on a critical hit, the next attack against the target has advantage. Very Rare. The bonus to attack and damage rolls increases to +2 and the additional piercing damage dealt to armored creatures increases to 2d6. In addition, Shuck the Oyster automatically reduces AC on a critical hit. Legendary. The bonus to attack and damage rolls increases to +3 and Shuck the Oyster reduces an armored creature’s AC by 2.
