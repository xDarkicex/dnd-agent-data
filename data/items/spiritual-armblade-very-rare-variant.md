# Spiritual Armblade (very rare variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement by a cleric, druid, monk, paladin, ranger, or warlock missing a hand) Component: celestial hair These magical prostheses were gifted to the warrior-servants of the divine in recompense for limbs lost in battle. You must have a missing hand to attune to this item. Once attuned to it, it wraps itself around the site of your lost hand and can’t be removed against your will. Summon Armblade. As a bonus action, you can summon a simple melee weapon made of spiritual energy from the prosthetic, or cause it to disappear. You are proficient with this weapon and use your spellcasting ability for the attack rolls made with this weapon (or your Wisdom modifier if you are a monk). The weapon deals 2d8 damage on a hit. Its appearance and damage type are linked to your alignment, as detailed below: Good Alignment: A golden blade that deals radiant damage. Neutral Alignment: A translucent-white weapon that deals force damage. Evil Alignment: A shaft of bruised mauve that deals necrotic damage. Shield. When a creature you can see attacks you while the weapon is summoned, you can use your reaction to shift it into a shield, gaining a +2 bonus to AC against the triggering attack. The shield reverts to a weapon immediately after. Uncommon variant: Reduce the bonus to AC to +1. Very rare variant: Increase the damage to 2d10. Deity-Specific Items Why not have spiritual armblades linked to specific deities? You can then restrict who can attune to the item to those with a similar alignment to the deity.
