# Toy Raiders Set

**description**: Toy Raiders Set Wondrous item, legendary (requires attunement) Thonir’s family used to collect special carved toy warriors for him so that he might play out large-scale battles as a child. Once Thonir’s set was complete Mirgal enchanted it for him. But over the years the toy warriors were lost, and the magic faded. Each warrior is the size of a child’s thumb and when a warrior is found, it has a glum expression on its face. If at least twelve warriors are reunited, the set’s magic returns and this item can be attuned to. At this point each soldier grins broadly. Thereafter, if any piece is separated by more than 200 feet from the rest, the warriors once again become sad. Each warrior can be used, much like a magical charge. When expended, the warrior “dies”—it takes on a stricken expression, its tongue hanging out of its mouth. 1d4 warriors are ‘brought back to life’ each dawn. The soldiers can be expended in the following ways: As an action, a steed is conjured as if the phantom steed spell was cast (requires 3 warriors). As a reaction to an attack that damages the attuned or any ally within 10 feet, the target ignores that damage (requires 9 warriors). As a reaction to seeing an enemy, the user can use the toy warriors as a distraction. That enemy is automatically surprised (requires 6 warriors). As a reaction to hitting an enemy with an attack or a damaging spell, the user adds 1d6 force damage for each warrior they expend.
