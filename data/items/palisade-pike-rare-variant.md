# Palisade Pike (rare variant)

**description**: Weapon (any polearm), uncommon (requires attunement) Component: construct plating This polearm looks to have been shaped from the wrought iron of a graveyard’s fence. Legend says the fence was magically enchanted by a wandering cleric, Sister Victoria, so that it would keep incarcerated the walking dead. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magical weapon. Fencing. As an action, you can stab palisade into the ground, causing a 1-inch-thick wrought iron fence to burst from the earth at a point you can see within 60 feet of you, twisting into a latticework of thorny vines. It can be either 30 feet long and 10 feet high or form a ringed wall up to 20 feet in diameter and 20 feet high. Each 5-foot-square section has an AC of 10, 15 hit points, and immunity to psychic and poison damage. The wall doesn’t block line of sight, but Small or larger creatures can’t move through it. When the fence appears, each creature within its area is pushed to one side of the fence (your choice) and must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or take 2d8 piercing damage. A creature attempting to climb the fence must make a DC 13 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check, with advantage if it has a climbing speed. On a failure, the creature slips, taking 2d8 piercing damage and falling prone at the base of the fence. Ranged attack rolls that pass through the fence are made with disadvantage. The fence persists for 10 minutes, until you lose concentration (as if concentrating on a spell), or until it is destroyed, whereupon it crumbles into dust. Once you have used this property, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Rare variant: Increase the attack and damage bonus to +2 and the DCs to 15.
