# Griffon's Cloak (very rare variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement) Component: pouch of monstrosity (griffon) feathers The act of crafting this feathered cloak is seen as a test of skill and patience by the followers of a powerful astral griffon. In doing so, they prove themselves worthy of carrying out the griffon’s designs across the planes, and pass down the secrets of the cloak’s creation to other like-minded souls with the heart and passion of true artisans. This cloak has 3 charges and regains 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn. Planar Passport. When this cloak visits a plane of existence, it becomes capable of acting as the material component for the plane shift spell, attuned to that particular plane. If found, the cloak is already attuned to 1d4 different planes of existence. Controlled Blink. While wearing the cloak, you can expend 1 charge to cast the blink spell. When the spell is cast in this way, you roll a second d20 at the end of each of your turns and choose which result to take. Uncommon variant: Remove all charges. Using the Controlled Blink property doesn’t require a charge, and once it’s been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Very rare variant: The cloak regains all expended charges daily at dawn. The cloak has the One Way Ticket property. One Way Ticket. While wearing the cloak, you can cast the plane shift spell (save DC 17). After you do so, roll a d20. On a 1, the cloak loses this property. On any other result, this property can’t be used again until the next dawn.
