# Rex Fang (Legendary)

**description**: Weapon (Club), Legendary (Requires Attunement) Common Earth Shaking Stomp. As an action, you can slam this weapon into the ground. Creatures in a 20-foot cone must succeed on a DC 10 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone. Once used, this property can’t be used again until you finish a long rest. Uncommon The DC of Earth Shaking Stomp increases to 12. While you hold this weapon, shimmering scales cover your flesh, granting a +1 bonus to AC. Rare The DC of Earth Shaking Stomp increases to 14. Saurian Pack. Holding this weapon aloft and letting out a primal scream, you can use an action to expend a number of charges to summon dinosaur allies. You summon a number of allosauruses or plesiosauruses (your choice) equal to the number of charges expended. They appear in unoccupied spaces that you can see within 60 feet. Each dinosaur disappears when it drops to 0 hit points or after 10 minutes. They act at the end of your turn, obeying any verbal commands that you issue to them (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any commands to them, they defend themselves from hostile creatures, but otherwise take no actions. Once used, you can’t used this property again until you finish a long rest. Very Rare The DC of Earth Shaking Stomp increases to 16. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon and deal an additional 2d6 thunder damage on a hit to creatures whose size is larger than yours. Legendary Obstinacy. You can expend four Hit Dice to use Earth Shaking Stomp again without needing to finish a long rest. Hit Dice expended this way do not recover hit points. Legendary The DC for Earth Shaking Stomp increases to 18. Become the Thunder Lizard. As an action, you magically assume the shape of a dinosaur. You can become a pteranodon for 4 hours, a plesiosaurus for 2 hours, or a tyrannosaurus rex for 1 hour. You can choose to extend the duration of the transformation by expending five Hit Dice. You make this choice when your transformation is about to end. This change otherwise follows the rules of a druid’s Wild Shape feature. Once used, this property can’t be used again until you finish a long rest.
