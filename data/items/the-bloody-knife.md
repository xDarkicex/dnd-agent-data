# The Bloody Knife

**description**: Weapon (dagger), Covenant Item (requires attunement) This knife was used by a halfling slave called Aio the Light Foot to take revenge upon the orc invaders that killed his family. As the body count of orcs killed in their sleep mounted, Aio’s rage and determination became a part of the blade itself. Though Aio was eventually captured and executed, he managed to train several fellow slaves in the ways of stealth, deception, and assassination, creating the first avenging knives. The Bloody Knife appears to be nothing more than a well-­made kitchen knife, but anyone who wields this dagger finds that it has several magical abilities. It is said that if its wielder ever dies (usually by execution at the hands of his masters in punishment for his crimes), the knife will mysteriously disappear, only to end up in the hands of halfling slave who is in a position to wreak havoc among the Shadow’s minions. Table B-­3: The Bloody Knife Character Level Covenant Item Properties 1 You have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet. In addition, while in darkness you are invisible to any creature that relies on darkvision to see you in the darkness. 2-4 You can cast the pass without trace spell, using the Bloody Knife as a spellcasting focus. Once you cast that spell with the Bloody Knife, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest. 5-10 You have advantage on attack rolls with the Bloody Knife against any creature that hasn't taken a turn in the combat yet. In addition, you can deal an extra 3d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack using the Bloody Knife, if the target of the attack has not yet taken a turn in the combat. 11-16 You can cast the invisibility spell targeting only yourself, using the Bloody Knife as a spellcasting focus. Once you cast that spell with the Bloody Knife, you can't do so again until you finish a long or short rest. 17+ Once on each of your turns, when you deal damage with the Bloody Knife to a creature allied with the forces of the Shadow, the creature must make a DC18 Constitution saving throw, taking an extra 6d6 piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much extra damage on a successful one.
