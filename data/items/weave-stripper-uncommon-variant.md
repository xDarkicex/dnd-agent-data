# Weave Stripper (uncommon variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare Component: aberration tentacle Part trap, part torture device, part magic removal mechanism, the weave stripper is a gold-banded, lead-lined box that houses a portal to a remote, antimagical corner of the Abyss. The denizens of this void feed on the Weave, forcibly removing it from the creatures around which it is woven. Pandora’s Box. As an action while holding this box, you can place it down and unlock it, causing it to slowly and eerily creep open. At the end of your turn, black mist spews forth in a 20-foot radius sphere centred on the box, soon followed by writhing, ethereal purple tentacles that flail and thrash wildly. Each creature that starts its turn in the area or moves into it for the first time on its turn must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. If the creature is under the effect of a spell or concentrating on one, it has disadvantage on the saving throw. On a failure, a creature takes 4d8 psychic damage, it loses concentration if it is concentrating on a spell, and any magical effect or spell of 5th level or lower that is affecting it is dispelled. On a success, a creature takes half as much damage and no additional effects occur. As an action, a creature within reach of the box can attempt a DC 15 Strength check, closing it on a success. Otherwise, after 1 minute, these abyssal creatures satiate themselves, retreat back into the box, the effect ends, and the box can be closed normally. Once this property of the item has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Uncommon variant: Reduce the DCs to 13, the damage to 3d6, and the level of spell that can be dispelled to 3rd level or lower.
