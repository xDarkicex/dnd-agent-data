# Skycrystal Armor

**description**: Skycrystal Armor Armor (Plate), Artifact (requires attunement) Forged by legendary giant blacksmiths in ages past and infused with the unending magic of creation, this suit of armor allows mortals a glimpse of true immortality. While wearing this armor, you have a +3 bonus to AC and advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects. The Skycrystal Armor has 10 charges and regains 1d8 + 2 charges daily at dawn. If you expend the last charge, roll a d10. On a 10, the armor regains 1d4 + 1 charges. Spell Absorption. While wearing this armor, you can use your reaction to absorb a spell that is targeting only you. The armor absorbs the magic of the spell, canceling its effect and gaining a number of charges equal to the absorbed spell’s level, up to the armor’s maximum charges. If you are targeted by a spell that the armor can’t store, the armor has no effect on that spell. Spells. While wearing this armor, you can expend 1 or more of its charges to cast one of the following spells from it, using your spell save DC and spellcasting ability: absorb elements (1 charge), globe of invulnerability (6 charges), invulnerability (9 charges), or shield (1 charge). Timelessness. While attuned to this armor, your body is unaffected by the passage of time. You stop aging, and any effects that would age you are suspended for you. If your attunement to the armor ends, these effects, including accumulated biological aging, affect you immediately.
