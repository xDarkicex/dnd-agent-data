# Thunderbolt Charger

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement) This magically propelled war chariot is fashioned to look like a blue dragon, complete with a blue dragon’s head, neck and wings that enclose the carriage from the two sides. This wondrous item has 10 charges and it regains 1d10 expended charges daily at dawn. The armored vehicle has a Strength score of 23, AC 18, 60 hit points, a damage threshold of 10, immunity to poison and psychic damage, and damage resistance to piercing and slashing damage. It has a speed of 60 ft. and can move on its own while controlled by a rider attuned to it. The rider gains half cover while positioned in the carriage of the magic chariot. The rider does not need vehicle proficiency to ride the chariot. As a bonus action, you can make one of the following melee attacks with the thunderbolt charger while riding it: Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (2d10 + 6) piercing damage plus 5 (1d10) lightning damage. Gore. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (2d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage. If the thunderbolt charger moves at least 20 feet straight toward a target and then hits it with a gore attack on the same turn, the target takes an extra 9 (2d8) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 16 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone. Wheel Blades. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., multiple targets within reach. Hit: 13 (2d6 + 6). The thunderbolt charger can attack targets with its wheel blades that are within 5 feet of it but only if it has moved during the same turn. You can use a bonus action and expend 1 charge to make the thunderbolt charger take to the air with a flying speed of 60 ft. for 1 round. You can use a bonus action and expend 3 charges to make the thunderbolt charger exhale lightning in a 30-­foot line that is 5 feet wide. Each creature in that line must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw , taking 22 (4d10) lightning damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. You can use a bonus action and expend 6 charges to make the thunderbolt charger exhale lightning in a 60-foot line that is 5 feet wide. Each creature in that line must make a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw , taking 55 (10d10) lightning damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
