# Lance of the Crab Knight

**description**: Weapon (lance), rare (requires attunement) Component: beast (giant crab) pincer The chitin of the giant centenary crab is some of the hardest found in the ocean, formed under the extreme pressure experienced thousands of feet below sea level. Hydrodynamic. Being underwater doesn’t impose disadvantage on attack rolls you make with this weapon. Crab Knight. While holding this weapon, you can cast the phantom steed spell. The steed always takes the form of a giant centenary crab, which has a swimming speed equal to its walking speed. Once this property of the lance has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Clawful. Once on each of your turns, you can replace one of the attacks you make with this lance as part of the Attack action with an attempt to capture a creature with it, causing the missing half of the crab’s claw to appear as a spectral, shimmering force and close itself around the target. The target must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be grappled by the lance for 1 minute (escape DC 15), until you release the target, or until you are no longer holding the lance (no action required). While you’re grappling a creature with the lance, you can only use the lance to attack that creature, crushing it with the claw. These attacks deal bludgeoning damage instead of piercing damage, deal an extra 1d6 bludgeoning damage on a hit, and don’t suffer from disadvantage as a result of you being within 5 feet of the target.
