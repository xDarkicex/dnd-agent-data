# Bottle of Hurricane

**description**: Wondrous item, rare This glass bottle is adorned with intricate patterns resembling swirling clouds and lightning bolts. It feels cool to the touch. When held up to the light, tiny storm clouds can be seen swirling within. When the stopper is removed and a command word is spoken, it unleashes a torrent of powerful wind until the start of your next turn. You can choose from the following three options: Gale: A 60-foot long and 10-foot wide line of strong wind blasts from the bottle. Each creature that starts its turn in the line must succeed on a DC 12 Strength saving throw or be pushed 15 feet away from you in a direction following the line. Any creature in the line must spend 2 feet of movement for every 1 foot it moves when moving closer to you. The wind disperses gas or vapor and extinguishes small unprotected flames such as candles and torches in the area. It causes protected flames, such as those of lanterns, to dance wildly and has a 50 percent chance of extinguishing them. Tempest: A 60-foot cone of strong wind blows from the bottle. Each creature that starts its turn in the cone must succeed on a DC 14 Strength saving throw or be pushed 25 feet away from you in a direction opposite of the bottle. Any creature in the area must spend 3 feet of movement for every 1 foot it moves when moving closer to you. The wind disperses gas or vapor and extinguishes medium or smaller unprotected flames such as campfires in the area. It causes protected flames, such as those of lanterns, to dance wildly and has a 75 percent chance of extinguishing them. Tornado: A 60-foot radius stationary whirlwind is created around the bottle. Each creature in the area must make a DC 16 Strength saving throw . On a failed save, a target takes 3d8 bludgeoning damage and is flung up 1d4 times 10 feet away from the middle of the whirlwind, and gets knocked prone. If a thrown target strikes an object, such as a wall or floor, the target takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet it was thrown. If the target is thrown at another creature, that creature must succeed on a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw or take the same damage and be knocked prone. On a successful save, the target takes half the bludgeoning damage and isn't flung away or knocked prone.
