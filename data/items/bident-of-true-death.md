# Bident of True Death

**description**: Bident of True Death Weapon (Spear), Artifact (requires attunement) Myth has it that this magic bident once belonged to an ancient deity that guided mortal souls to the realm of the dead. As eons passed and new gods arose, the bident fell into the hands of mortals. Throughout history, heroes wielded the bident to protect their realms from undead hordes, while villains leveraged its power to utterly vanquish their foes. This indestructible bident grants a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it. When you hit with an attack using it, the target takes an extra 3d6 piercing damage. True Death. Any creature that has been killed by the Bident of True Death can’t become undead and can be restored to life only by a wish spell. Mastery over Death. While wielding the Bident of True Death, you can use an action to cast power word kill from it. This property can’t be used again until 30 days have passed or until you have slain a mighty undead foe (CR 15 or higher). Undead Bane. Undead creatures can only regain hit points lost to the bident’s damage through a long rest, rather than by regeneration, magic, or any other means. Undead creatures are destroyed if the bident’s damage reduces their hit points to 0. Nyctos on Bident of True Death “Hey, this is so old tridents hadn’t even been invented yet! What? Yeah, and before that, like, before history, mortals used monodents, also known as spears!”
