# Poison of Oblivion

**description**: Poison of Oblivion Wondrous Item, Very Rare Harvested from elusive giant spiders that dwell in enchanted caves, this poison has the power to send even the mightiest foes into a deep, dreamless sleep. A vial of Poison of Oblivion holds a single dose. As an action, you can coat one slashing or piercing weapon, one piece of ammunition, or a trap that deals slashing or piercing damage with this poison. Once applied, the poison retains its potency for one year or until a creature takes slashing or piercing damage from the item that was coated with it. A creature that takes slashing or piercing damage from a weapon, a piece of ammunition, or a trap coated with Poison of Oblivion must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or become poisoned for 4d6 hours. While poisoned this way, the creature is unconscious and immune to the dream spell and can only wake up if it takes 10 damage or more on a single round. Optional Rule: Arcane Anesthesia Dark elf surgeons use Poison of Oblivion as anesthetic to perform ambitious and often life-saving surgeries. At the gamemaster’s discretion, player characters can use an action to insert the contents of a Poison of Oblivion vial in a syringe. In that form, the effects of Poison of Oblivion can be applied to a willing creature without the creature having to take damage.
