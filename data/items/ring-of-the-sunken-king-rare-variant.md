# Ring of the Sunken King (rare variant)

**description**: Ring, uncommon (requires attunement) Component: aberration tentacle This barnacle-encrusted band of rusted metal is always cold and wet to the touch. The way the metal twists into itself has a certain ‘tentacle’ quality that is only enhanced by the way it adheres to your skin when you don the ring. Tentacle Lash. As an action, you can speak the ring’s command word to cause your arm to elongate into a long, wet, suckered tentacle for 1 minute. As part of that action, and as an action on each of your turns, you can perform a melee spell attack (+5 to hit) against a target within 30 feet of you. On a hit, the target takes 4d8 bludgeoning damage and, if it is a Huge or smaller creature, it must make a DC 13 Strength saving throw. On a failure, you can choose one of the following effects: The target is knocked prone. You disarm the target; you take one item or weapon the target is carrying or wielding (and that isn’t fastened, e.g. armour) into your tentacled hand. If your tentacled hand isn’t free, the item falls at your feet instead. Once this property has been used, roll a d6. On a 1-2, it can’t be used again until the ring spends 1 continuous hour submerged in water. Rare variant: Increase the DC to 15 and the damage to 6d8.
