# Staff of the Life Herald

**description**: Staff of the Life Herald Staff, Artifact (requires attunement by a bard, cleric, or druid) Myth has it that this staff once belonged to the ancient deity who first conceived medicine as a profession. Throughout history, legendary healers wielded the Staff of the Life Herald to heal entire armies, spare the dying, cure plagues, and even bring heroes back from the dead. However, a few greedy individuals across the ages have used the staff to gain political leverage and amass tremendous fortune from those in need of its magic. Herald of Health. While attuned to this staff, you are immune to disease and poison, and have resistance to necrotic damage. Life Magic. The staff has 20 charges. While holding this staff, you can use an action to expend 1 or more of its charges to cast one of the following spells from it, using your spell save DC and spellcasting ability modifier: beacon of hope (3 charges), cure wounds (1 charge per spell level), heal (6 charges), mass cure wounds (5 charges), regenerate (7 charges), or resurrection (7 charges). The staff regains 2d8 + 4 expended charges daily at dawn. If you expend the last charge, roll a d20. On a 1, the staff drains your own life force, aging you by 2d8 + 4 years and gaining as many charges. If you cannot age, or if you are immune to effects that would age you, the staff does not gain any charges on a roll of 1. You can also use an action to cast one of the following spells from the staff without using any charges: detect poison and disease , gentle repose , lesser restoration , or spare the dying .
