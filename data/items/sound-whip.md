# Sound Whip

**description**: Sound Whip Weapon (Whip), Rare (requires attunement) Crafted by a legendary storm sorceress who channeled some of her overflowing magic into arcane relics, this whip harbors the shattering power of thunder. When inactive, this item appears to be a whip handle. While holding it, you can use a bonus action to cause a whip of pure sound to spring into existence, or make the whip disappear. Made of thunderous sonic waves, the Sound Whip has the light property. When you attack with this weapon, a loud crack of thunder, audible from as far away as 1200 feet, emanates from it. Sonic Lash. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon, which deals thunder damage instead of slashing damage. When you hit a creature made of inorganic material such as stone, crystal, or metal with it, as well as a nonmagical object that isn’t being worn or carried, that target takes an extra 2d8 thunder damage. Deafening Strike. When you hit a creature with an attack with this whip, that creature must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be deafened until the start of your next turn. Creatures made of inorganic material such as stone, crystal, or metal have disadvantage on this saving throw. Item Variant: Greater Sound Whip At the gamemaster’s discretion, a legendary version of this item can be found. The Greater Sound Whip grants a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls (instead of +2) and deals an extra 4d8 thunder damage to creatures made of inorganic material such as stone, crystal, or metal with it, as well as nonmagical objects that are not being worn or carried (instead of 2d8). Additionally, the Constitution saving throw DC for Deafening Strike increases to 20, and creatures that fail it are deafened for 1 minute. Player characters may undertake a quest to upgrade a Sound Whip into a Greater Sound Whip, possibly by enlisting the help of a storm sorcerer or a powerful air elemental.
