# Scythe of the Headless Seahorseman Halberd

**description**: Weapon (glaive or halberd), rare (requires attunement) Component: beast (giant sea horse) fin This rusted scythe, with its driftwood handle that supports a plethora of sea life, is surprisingly light and hydrodynamic. Fearflame. As a bonus action, you can speak the weapon’s first command word, causing an eerie green flame to shimmer along the blade’s length for 1 minute. For the duration, whenever you hit with this magic weapon, the target takes an extra 2d6 psychic damage and must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you until the start of your next turn. This property can be used twice, regaining all expended uses daily at dawn. Find Steed. As an action, you can speak the scythe’s second command word and cast the find steed spell. When the spell is cast in this way, it always summons an amphibian seamare, which has the statistics of a giant sea horse with the following changes: It has a walking and swimming speed of 60 feet. It can breathe both air and water. It can use an action to roar, forcing each creature of its choice within 60 feet of it that can hear it to succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of the seamare for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature’s saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to this action for 24 hours. Once this property of the weapon has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Hydrodynamic. Attacking while underwater doesn’t impose disadvantage on attack rolls you make with this weapon.
