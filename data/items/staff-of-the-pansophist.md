# Staff of the Pansophist

**description**: Staff of the Pansophist Staff, Legendary (requires attunement by a sorcerer, warlock, or wizard) Few Fateweavers ever reach the rank of the Pansophist and any who do find themselves among an elite of wizards with vast magical resources and expansive political influence. A wizard reaching the rank of Pansophist within the Fateweavers is often entrusted with a Staff of the Pansophist or another staff of legendary power fit for the wizard’s exalted status. This staff can be wielded as a magic quarterstaff that grants a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it. While holding it, you gain a +2 bonus to Armor Class, saving throws, and spell attack rolls. The staff has 50 charges for the following properties. It regains 4d6 + 2 expended charges daily at dawn. If you expend the last charge, roll a d20. On a 1, the staff loses power, becoming a Staff of the Observer with 10 charges. On a 20, the staff regains 1d12 + 1 charges. Greater Fateweaving. Whenever a creature you can see makes an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can expend 1 or more charges, up to double your proficiency bonus, and add or subtract that number from the roll (no action required). Spells. While holding this staff, you can use an action to expend 1 or more of its charges to cast one of the following spells from it, using your spell save DC and spell attack bonus: arcane eye (4 charges), augury (2 charges), contact other plane (5 charges), clairvoyance (3 charges), detect magic (1 charge), detect thoughts (2 charges), divination (4 charges), foresight (9 charges), identify (1 charge), legend lore (5 charges), see invisibility (2 charges), scrying (5 charges), or true seeing (6 charges). Optional Rule: Upgrading Staff of the Observer into Staff of the Pansophist At the gamemaster’s discretion, player characters may find a way to upgrade a Staff of the Observer into a Staff of the Pansophist. This could be an arcane ceremony through the Fateweavers or a forgotten ritual that imbues the staff with even more divination magic. Adventure Hook: Fateweaver Trouble The Fateweavers protect their secrets and their magic treasures fiercely. If any of their items fall into the wrong hands, the Fateweavers will use powerful divination and conjuration spells to find the item and summon it back to the order, and failing that, they will seek to strike a deal with whoever obtained it to allow them to keep it.
