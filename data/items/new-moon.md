# New Moon

**description**: Weapon (Sickle), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) The handle of this sickle is made of pale bone, and the blade is a spectral silver. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. This weapon has 3 charges, and regains all expended charges when you finish a long rest. While holding this weapon, you can use an action to expend 1 charge, causing the weapon’s blade to shine, filling a 10-foot sphere with dim moonlight for 1 minute or until your concentration ends (as if concentrating on a spell). This sphere moves with the weapon, but disappears if you drop the weapon, are disarmed, or become unconscious. Any creature (excluding you) that enters the sphere for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw, taking 2d10 radiant damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Shapechangers make this save with disadvantage, and if they fail, they are also forced to revert to their original shape and can’t assume a different form until they leave the sphere. Encounter Ideas Druid. The adventurers encounter an injured archdruid who lost a battle with lycanthropes. He agrees to trade his sickle in exchange for the party’s oath to track down the remaining lycanthropes. Lycanthropes. In the midst of a wild wood, the adventurers discover a cluster of crude huts around a large cave mouth. The family of wereboars who live here are not happy with the intrusion. A new moon hangs on one wall of the cave, displayed above a grisly feasting hall. Altar. While traveling through a dense forest, the adventurers find a moss-covered altar in the middle of a glade. The sickle lies on the altar, bait for a trap laid by an adult green dragon.
