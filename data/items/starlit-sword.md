# Starlit Sword

**description**: In the last days before the old world ended, faithful of the god Vidris received dream visions. The light of the stars will vanish forever, they heard. His followers knew they had to capture it, preserve it, and one of the ways they did so was by creating Starlit Swords. Even though the stars are not his domain, Vidris is strongly associated with them. He is depicted with stars in his eyes, and his holy symbol is an orb filled with stars. Some believe that his very essence is captured in starlight. As this light was brought over to the new world through items like the Starlit Swords, Vidris himself was reborn in Ashandri. This could be nothing but interesting tales, but considering that Vidris is also the god of trickery, such a stunt would very well befit this deity. The Starlit Swords show no signs of any kind of divine power, but faithful of Vidris still hold them in high regard. Magic schools: Evocation Cost: 1.500 gp Damage: 1d6 slashing + 1 radiant Magic properties: The Starlit Sword glows with a dim light comparable to a torch. Glowing stars can be seen in the blade itself, appearing and vanishing again over the course of hours. You have a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with the Starlit Sword. The bonus damage counts as radiant damage. Ethenniel stood in awe as Vamaelle, the temple’s high priestess of Vidris, gave him the sword. “Cherish it”, she said. “This contains one of the last remnants of starlight from our old world. There are still stars in this world, but they are not the same. This sword is far more valuable than its magic powers may show.” The acolyte took the Starlit Sword cautiously. “I will guard it with my life.” Vamaelle smiled. “Don’t just guard it. It was meant to be used, too. Which brings me to our next topic: There’s a community of werewolves a few miles north. A group of adventurers has decided to kill them, simply because they are werewolves. But they aren’t bad. They are in control of their powers. Travel to their lair, and protect them with your life.” Ethenniel nodded. His first assignment. He would not fail.
