# Owlsight Goggles (rare variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon Component: beast (giant owl) eye “Don’t go out at night or the giant owls will get ya,” has been said by more than one exhausted rakin parent in a mostly-unsuccessful attempt to curb their offspring’s exploratory and mischievous tendencies. Unfortunately for rakin, this isn’t an exaggeration: giant owls are the number one predators of forest-dwelling tanukin. The naming of this acuity-enhancing eyewear is undoubtedly a nod at this cruel twist of nature by the infamous and ungracious artificer, L’Arsène Upin. Owlsight. While wearing these goggles, you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet and attacking at long range doesn’t impose disadvantage on your ranged weapon attack rolls. In addition, you have advantage on Perception checks made to spot Small or smaller creatures. Rare variant: If you already have darkvision, wearing the goggles increases its range by 60 feet. The goggles have the Thermal Lock property. Thermal Lock. As a bonus action, you can choose a creature within 30 feet of you and activate the goggles’ thermal vision. For 1 hour, you can see that creature based on the amount of heat it emits, with low amounts of heat represented in blue and purple, and high amounts represented in yellow, orange, and red. If the creature emits heat (GM’s discretion), you have advantage on Perception checks that rely on sight made to detect it, and you can always see it regardless of light conditions, obscurement, invisibility, or cover, but this vision is blocked by 1 foot of stone, 1 inch of common metal, a thin sheet of lead, or 3 feet of wood or dirt. In addition, you can see objects and surfaces that it touched within the last 10 minutes, giving you advantage on checks made to track its movements. Most constructs, elementals, plants, and undead don’t emit heat. Once this property of the goggles has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn.
