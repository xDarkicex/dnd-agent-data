# Hand of the Spellbinder

**description**: Wondrous Item, Very Rare (Requires Attunement) This magic glove holds three tiny, tightly-wound scrolls on its back. Scrollbound. A creature can cast a spell into one of the glove's scrolls by touching the scroll as the spell is cast. The spell must be of 3rd level or lower, have a casting time of an action or a bonus action, and affect only one target. The spell has no effect, other than to be stored in the glove. The glove can hold up to three spells at a time. While at least one spell is stored in the glove's scrolls, you gain the following benefits: Unarmed strikes you make using the hand wearing the glove and attacks you make with a weapon held in that hand, deal an extra 1d6 Force damage on a hit. Once per turn, when you hit with an unarmed strike using the hand wearing the glove or a weapon held in that hand, you can cast one of the spells stored in the glove's scroll on the target as part of the attack. The spell uses the slot level, spell save DC, and spellcasting ability of the original caster, but is otherwise treated as if you cast the spell. If the spell requires a spell attack, it automatically hits the target. The spell is no longer stored in the scroll.
