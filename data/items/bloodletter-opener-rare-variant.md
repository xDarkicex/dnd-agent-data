# Bloodletter Opener (rare variant)

**description**: Weapon (dagger), uncommon Component: phial of humanoid blood Dripping with sanguine liquid, bloodletters were used to help flush out debilitating veinworms, a plague throughout the Remembered Realms, in a practice called bloodflushing. This operation could be extremely traumatic. Thus magical bloodletters—ones that retained a part of the host’s blood in a reservoir to be fortified and augmented—were developed to help restore life should the operation be unsuccessful. This weapon has 4 charges and regains 1d4 expended charges daily at dawn. Draw Blood. As an action while holding this weapon, you can use it to draw blood from a willing creature within your reach. Alternatively, when you hit a creature that has blood with a weapon attack using the bloodletter opener, you can draw some of its blood into the weapon’s reservoir. While the weapon contains blood, you can use a bonus action to flick a switch on its handle and expend 1 charge to either augment or corrupt the blood. Unused blood in the dagger vanishes at dawn. Augment. The blood becomes enhanced with healing properties. As an action, you can inject the blood into the creature from whom it was extracted. The creature regains 2d4 hit points and its speed increases by 15 feet for 1 minute. Corrupt. The blood becomes corrupted into a poison bespoke to the creature from whom it was extracted. When you hit that creature with an attack using the weapon, you can inject the bloodletter opener’s contents into the creature. The creature must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, the creature takes 2d8 poison damage and is poisoned until the end of its next turn. On a success, the creature takes half as much damage and isn’t poisoned. Rare variant: Increase the hit points regained to 4d4, the DC to 15, and the damage to 4d8.
