# Ring of Thunderstorms, Minor

**description**: This swirling ring of clouds is like a miniature thunderstorm, just big enough to fit around a finger. If someone tries to put it on, it changes size to fit perfectly for any character of small, medium or huge size. Tiny lightning bolts constantly appear within it. The secrets of creating these rings were given to the mortals by the deity Gadrishval, the Lord of the Wind. Depending on the skill of its creator, different versions of the item have been made. It is said that some of the most powerful ones were crafted by Gadrishval himself. Magic schools: Evocation Cost: 6.000 gp Magic properties: The Minor Ring of Thunderstorms grants Resistance to Thunder damage. A number of times equal to the wearer’s Proficiency bonus, the wearer can add 3d4 lightning damage to a melee attack. The effect can be activated after a successful attack roll, but before the damage is rolled. Once all charges have been used, the ring needs 24 hours to recharge before it can be used again. “Finally”, Arthi whispered, opening the chest. She expected gold, gemstones, flashy jewelry. Instead, there was a single object inside. “Well... still kind of flashy”, she mumbled to herself, as she picked up the strange ring. Clouds moved in a constant twirling motion. Lights flared up within, tiny lighting bolts hidden in the ring of clouds. Arthi half expected to hear thunder, but everything stayed silent. She hesitated for a moment, then shrugged and put on the ring. It shrank a little, adapting perfectly to her finger. Then she noticed the rune carved into the bottom of the chest. It wasn’t magical, it was just a single word written in an old language of the humans. “Ghadhri”, she read it out loud. It meant “The Lord’s Blessing”. The next moment, she was knocked off her feet as a magical storm erupted from the ring. A sphere of wind formed, just a few steps away from her, swirling and raging. But that wasn’t all: Crackling energy engulfed her hand, lightning that was only waiting to be unleashed on her enemies. Arthi smiled. This was so, so much better than gold...
