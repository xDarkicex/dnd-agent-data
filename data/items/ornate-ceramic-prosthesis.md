# Ornate Ceramic Prosthesis

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement by a creature missing a limb) Component: construct (clay golem) flesh Developed by a mysterious puppeteer, the painted patterns that decorate the ceramic surface of this appendage seem to shift from day to day, and vary in texture and hue from one individual to another. You must have a missing limb to attune to this item. Once attuned to it, it reshapes itself to take the place of your missing limb, functions as a normal limb, and can’t be removed against your will. When you make an unarmed strike using this prosthesis, its plating morphs into a sharp, ceramic blade. Unarmed strikes made with the prosthesis count as magical for the purposes of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage and deal slashing damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier. Full Plating. As an action, you can speak the prosthesis’ command word to activate the ceramic plating. It grows to cover your entire body, giving you the appearance of an articulated porcelain doll for 1 minute. For the duration, you gain a +2 bonus to AC. Additionally, you have vulnerability to bludgeoning damage and resistance to fire and slashing damage. This property can be used twice, regaining all expended uses daily at dawn.
