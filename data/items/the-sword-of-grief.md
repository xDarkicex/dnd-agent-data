# The Sword of Grief

**description**: The Sword of Grief Melee weapon (martial, longsword), uncommon This suit of chain mail and sword were the property of a very old elf traveling to the faerie realm in the hope of retiring there for eternity. He was too frail to continue carrying his armor, so he decided to leave it behind. The items carry some of the sadness that plagued the elf for leaving his children behind. The two items are fashioned of a beautiful silvery metal decorated with small leaves. They gleam brightly and are both very light (half the normal weight). Both are +1 magic items, with one small quirk: Whenever they are used in combat, the bearer must make a Dismay check. If the roll fails, a deep feeling of sadness will overcome the adventurer. This will not prevent him or her from fighting, but might cause them to sit down and weep after the fight is over. The feeling will pass after a few minutes. Dismay check: The new owner should roll against a difficulty of 8 the first time he or she picks up the items to see if they acquire a Dismay point. Further checks as described above will not result in additional Dismay gains. You have a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon. Versatile: This weapon can be used with one or two hands. A damage value in parentheses appears with the property–the damage when the weapon is used with two hands to make a melee attack. Damage: 1d8+1 (1d10+1) Damage Type: Slashing Properties: Versatile Weight: 1.5
