# Withercloud Pitchfork

**description**: Weapon (Trident), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) This farming implement consists of three rusted points twisted together before spreading out like iron vines. The prongs sit atop a gnarled wooden polearm. Stale and heavy air hangs about the weapon. This magic weapon deals an extra 1d8 necrotic damage on a hit. When you kill a living creature with this weapon, the creature dries out into a husk and dissolves. All living creatures within 30 feet of the killed creature must make a DC 17 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, the creature takes 4d8 necrotic damage. A creature who is brought to 0 hit points as a result of this damage dies, triggering the effect again. On a success, the creature takes half damage and doesn’t suffer the withering effects if they drop to 0 hit points. Hit points lost to the necrotic damage dealt by this weapon cannot be regained except through healing magic. In addition, you can cast blight (spell save DC 17) from this weapon. Once used, this property can’t be used again until you finish a long rest. Encounter Ideas Village Quarantine. A rapid plague forces the villagers to isolate themselves, unable to confront the mysterious stranger that now stalks their roads. Blighted Crops. A farmer struggles with diseased produce. She suspects a jealous rival is responsible after seeing the rival enter the forest with a bag of coins and emerge with a gnarled pitchfork. No Rest for the Withered. A forgotten temple has revealed itself in the woods after the vines covering the entrance withered away. Inside resides the weapon, along with servants of the ancient deity of decay and disease that corrupted it.
