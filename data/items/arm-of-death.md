# Arm of Death

**description**: Weapon (Club), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) This club is a disembodied arm of an ancient mummy lord. The limb is wrapped tight with cloth ribbons, and the hand is balled up in a grisly fist. It is numbingly cold to the touch, and the stench of decay and perfume permeates your nostrils. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon, which deals an extra 2d6 necrotic damage. If you roll a 20 on the attack roll, the target must succeed on a DC 16 Constitution saving throw or be cursed. While cursed this way, the target can’t regain hit points for 24 hours, or until they are targeted by the remove curse spell or similar magic. The weapon has 5 charges for the following properties, which you can use while holding it. It regains 1d4 + 1 expended charges when you finish a long rest. Undead Smite. When you hit a creature with an attack using this weapon, you can expend 1 charge to regain hit points equal to the necrotic damage dealt. Sandstorm Blast. As a bonus action, you can expend 2 charges to open the shriveled hand of the mummy lord, sending the fury of a desert storm erupting forth and creating a 20-foot cone originating from it. Each creature within this area must succeed on a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw or be blinded until the end of their next turn. Encounter Ideas Grave Robbers. Dirty Nails, a human grave robber (thug) who possesses the club, is the only survivor after a recent tomb raid. After watching her friends perish while combating the mummy, she wishes to sell the weapon so she can get out of the tomb raiding business for good. She can also tell you where the mummy’s tomb is—for a fee, of course. You Complete Me. A mummy lord just wants his arm back and asks the party to find the evil villain (a paladin, naturally) who took it. It Belongs in a Museum. A museum, specifically a mummy exhibit, has been ransacked by a local thieves’ guild. The curator asks the group to return the stolen property for a reward.
