# Ka-Ma-Ra

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon Component: fiend eye “Please… paint the picture!” Sergeant Flotsam pleaded. “Can’t,” replied the red-scale imp. “On strike.” “Listen, I point you at the dead body, you pick up your paintbrush, and you paint. I can see you’ve got a full stock of paint in there,” said Flotsam, peering in through a small window in the Ka-Ma-Ra’s side. “What’s that in the corn- Is that what I think it is?!” “S’private,” said the imp. “Listen. Union rates are three ounces of brimstone and a half ounce of sulphur every ten pictures. It’s not my fault you kept pressing the trigger while I was in your pack. I’m almost out of black paint now!” All modern technology is accompanied by growing pains, and the Ka-Ma-Ra is no different. Summoned from the hells and bound into a half-foot-cubed wooden box, the imps that power these pictograph-producing devices tend not to have ‘helpful’ on their list of traits. By pulling a trigger, a small hammer within the box kicks the imp out of bed, whereupon it paints a picture of whatever the Ka-Ma-Ra is pointing at. Make sure to turn the flash on or end up with a picture in greyscale! This item has 10 charges. As an action, you can supply the imp within it with up to 10 gp worth of paint (in a variety of colours), brimstone, and sulphur, causing the item to regain 1 expended charge for each 1 gp worth of materials provided. Shoot. As an action, you can point the Ka-Ma-Ra in a direction of your choice and expend 1 charge to pull the trigger, flashing bright light in a 20-foot cone. At the start of your next turn, the imp hands you a small, 3” by 2” picture of whatever the Ka-Ma-Ra was pointing at. If you move or use a reaction between taking the action and the start of your next turn, the picture is blurry; you must remain perfectly still while the imp is at work. If the Ka-Ma-Ra is left unused for too long, the imp within may expend charges of its own accord to produce risqué images of other imps in an attempt to ward off boredom.
