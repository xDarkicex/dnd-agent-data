# Wand of Vital Death

**description**: The Wand of Vital Death is a curious item that combines life and death into a powerful magic effect. It’s unknown who made the first of these wands. What is known is that the original Wands of Vital Death have a few special properties: The command word to activate them is Muerza, a word that even most sages don’t know anything about. When they break, they disappear at dawn - allegedly, to appear elsewhere in the world, fully intact. Mortals later tried to copy these items, mostly successful. As a tradition, they usually have the same command word, but they don’t disappear after they’re broken - they just stay broken. Magic schools: Transmutation Cost: 10.000 gp Magic properties: This wand has five charges, which regenerate each dawn. If all five charges are used, roll a d20. On a 1, the wand breaks, losing its magical powers forever. As an action, you can cast a special version of the spell Animate Dead. The prerequisite is that there is plant life no more than five feet away from the corpse or skeleton you want to animate. Vines, roots and leaves grow and entangle the dead body. These plants are what is actually animated. The created skeleton or zombie has all the stats of the regular undead version, but counts as a living plant under control of the wielder of the wand. To cast the spell at a higher level, you have to use one charge of the wand for each additional level. Tulacc stared at the small creature in front of him. Was that a little bear with wings? Whatever it was, the little one wouldn’t stop him from taking his deserved loot. “Better leave as long as you can”, he grunted, drawing his sword. “I’m sorry, I can’t do that.” The creature shook its head. “That dead man has stolen from my people, and his belongings are ours.” The half-elf laughed. “If he took from you, it became his. And now it’ll become mine. It’s as simple as that.” Again, the little winged bear shook its head. “I disagree.” He pulled a wand from his belt, and pointed it at the corpse... and suddenly, the vines on the ground began to move, growing and entangling the dead body. Only seconds later, the corpse stood up, drawing a sword. “I’m sorry”, the bear-person said. “I didn’t want this to get ugly.”
