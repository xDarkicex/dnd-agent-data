# Blood Armor

**description**: Armor (Any), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) This magic armor is fashioned from a bone-like material and red, vein-like strands flow from it. When you attune to this armor, its veins connect with yours. Dormant. In its dormant form, the armor has the following properties: While you have fewer than half your hit points, you gain a +2 bonus to AC. Whenever you take non-Psychic damage, the armor gains 1 charge. The armor can have up to 3 charges and loses unspent charges 1 hour after gaining them. As a reaction to a creature missing you with a melee attack, you can deal 1d10 Necrotic damage to the creature per charge you expend. Infused. In its infused form, the armor has the following additional properties: While you have fewer than half your hit points, you have advantage on ability checks and saving throws using Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution. At the start of your turn, you can expend charges to vitalize yourself (no action required). For each charge you expend, you gain 1d10 temporary hit points and suppress one of the following conditions on yourself until the start of your next turn: Blinded, Deafened, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, or Stunned. Blood Infusion It is up to the GM when the Blood Armor and Blood Helmet become infused. It might just require that a character attunes to both at the same time, completing the set, but it may also only happen when the character dies while wearing the armor or the helmet. If they're brought back to life, the armor and/or helmet is now infused for that character.
