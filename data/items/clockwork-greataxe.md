# Clockwork Greataxe

**description**: Weapon (greataxe), rare (requires attunement) This formidable greataxe is crafted entirely of gleaming, polished brass and steel, with intricate clockwork mechanisms visible under its blade and gold inlays on its haft. When activated, the blade vibrates rapidly, emitting a high-pitched whirring sound as it slices through the air. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. The clockwork greataxe has the unique ability to manipulate time. This magic weapon has 3 charges and it regains 1d3 expended charges at dawn. Upon striking a foe, you can use a bonus action and expend 1 charge to slow the target creature for three rounds. An affected target's speed is halved, it takes a −2 penalty to AC and Dexterity saving throws, and it can't use reactions. On its turn, it can use either an action or a bonus action, but not both. Regardless of the creature's abilities or magic items, it can't make more than one melee or ranged attack during its turn. If the creature attempts to cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action, roll a d20. On an 11 or higher, the spell doesn't take effect until the creature's next turn, and the creature must use its action on that turn to finish casting the spell. If it can't, the spell is wasted. A creature affected this way can make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw at the end of each of its turns. On a successful save, the effect ends for it.
