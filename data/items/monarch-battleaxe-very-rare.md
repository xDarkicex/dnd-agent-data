# Monarch Battleaxe (Very Rare)

**description**: Monarch Battleaxe Weapon (Battleaxe), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. The battleaxe deals an additional 1d8 radiant damage on a hit. Druids are proficient with the Monarch Battleaxe. Summon Butterflies. While holding this weapon, you can speak a command word as a bonus action to summon 24 Tiny purple butterflies which flutter around the axe head for 1 hour. Each butterfly has AC 10, 1 hit point, and automatically fails saving throws. While at least one butterfly remains, the weapon emits a purple glow, creating bright light in a 10-foot radius, and dim light for an additional 10 feet. Once you have summoned butterflies, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest. Butterfly Burst. When you hit with an attack using this weapon, you can expend up to 6 of your summoned butterflies to deal additional radiant damage to the target equal to the number of butterflies expended. Expended butterflies are destroyed. Monarch Wings. You can use a bonus action to expend up to 6 butterflies to cause spectral, purple butterfly wings to sprout from your back, giving you a fly speed equal to 5 times the number of butterflies expended. The wings remain for 1 hour, or until you use a bonus action to end the effect. Wrapped in Wings. As a bonus action, you can command 12 butterflies to swarm a creature within 60 feet of you that you can see. While swarmed this way, attacks against the creature have advantage. Any remaining butterflies return to the axe head if the creature is reduced to 0 hit points. Progression The Monarch Battleaxe progresses as the creature who is attuned to it progresses. Whenever the creature’s proficiency bonus increases, the weapon’s rarity also increases.
