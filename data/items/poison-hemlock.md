# Poison Hemlock

**description**: This herb may grow 7 feet high. The smooth, hollow stem is usually purple or red striped. Its white flowers are small and grow in small groups that tend to form flat umbels. Its long, turnip like taproot is solid. This plant is very poisonous and even a very small amount may cause death. This plant is easy to confuse with wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, especially in its first stage of growth. Any creature eating any part of the plant must make a DC11 Constitution saving throw or take 5 (2d4) points of poison damage and gain the poisoned condition for 2d4 hours. The poison damage scales up based on how much is eaten. When not accidentally eaten a poisoner usually uses the entire root which will cause 24 (8d4) poison damage.
