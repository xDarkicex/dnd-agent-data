# Ovinrbaane, Enemy of all Enemies

**description**: Ovinrbaane, Enemy of all Enemies Melee weapon (martial, greatsword), artifact (requires attunement) Ovinrbaane (literally translated as “enemy of all enemies”) is a powerful but cursed greatsword. Magic Weapon. Ovinrbaane is a magic weapon that grants a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it. The greatsword also functions as a ring of free action and a sword of wounding . Random Properties. Ovinrbaane has the following properties (you can swap these out for similar random properties if you so desire): While you are attuned to the sword and wielding it, you can use an action to cast bless. After you cast the spell, roll a d6. On a roll of 1–5, you can’t cast it again until the next dawn. (1 minor beneficial property) While you are attuned to the sword and wielding it, the sword can cast dispel magic to attempt to remove an undesirable spell effect as soon as it affects you. Whether or not this is successful, after it occurs, roll a d6. On a roll of 1–5, the sword can’t do this again until the next dawn. (1 major beneficial property) Over the course of time, the sword modifies its wielder’s memories to mislead its wielder into believing they’re the real Armag and that anyone around them who isn’t obviously a member of their clan is a mortal enemy. The cursed greatsword also slowly changes the features of the wielder over the course of several days or even weeks to appear as those of the original Armag. If left unchecked, these powers seemingly bring the warlord back to life—it’s the wait for this subtle transformation to be complete that’s kept Armag in “meditation” in this chamber for so long. (1 major detrimental property) Curse. The sword bears a curse that affects any creature that becomes attuned to it. Even if the attunement ends, the curse remains. The sword’s curse functions as that caused by a berserker axe , but also transforms the user’s body and mind. Destroying Ovinrbaane. Ovinrbaane is dedicated to the slaying of all life, and can’t be destroyed unless it is brought to the Boneyard and used to strike three blows against Armag’s own gravestone, causing the sword to shatter. Heavy. Small creatures have disadvantage on attack rolls with heavy weapons. A heavy weapon’s size and bulk make it too large for a Small creature to use effectively. Two-­Handed. This weapon requires two hands when you attack with it. Damage: 2d6 Damage Type: slashing Modifiers: Melee Attacks +2, Melee Damage +2 Properties: Heavy, Two-Handed Range: 5 ft. Weight: 6
