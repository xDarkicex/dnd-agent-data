# Rictus (Legendary)

**description**: Weapon (Mace), Legendary (Requires Attunement by a Good Aligned Creature) Common When Undead are within 120 feet of this magic weapon, its silver skull splits, revealing golden spikes. Uncommon Rictus deals an extra weapon die of damage to Undead creatures on a hit. Decaying Countenance. As an action, you can assume an illusory shroud of undeath for 1 hour or until you dismiss it (no action required). A successful DC 13 Wisdom (Insight) check allows a creature to see through your illusion. Undead with an Intelligence of 6 or less automatically fail this check. A creature who fails this check perceives you as Undead. Spells and magical effects that detect creature types, such as a paladin’s Divine Sense or the trigger of a symbol spell treat you as Undead unless you fail a DC 19 Charisma saving throw, in which case they discern your true nature. Rare The DC of Decaying Countenance’s Insight check increases to 15 and the DC of its Charisma saving throw decreases to 17. Castigation. Rictus deals an extra 1d6 radiant damage on a hit. When you score a critical hit against a creature with this weapon, the target must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or it is wracked with pain and makes its next attack roll with disadvantage. Very Rare The DC of Decaying Countenance’s Insight check increases to 17 and the DC of its Charisma saving throw decreases to 15. In addition, the save DC of Castigation increases to 17 and the additional radiant damage increases to 2d6. When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with this weapon, you regain hit points equal to the radiant damage rolled. Legendary The DC of Decaying Countenance’s Insight check increases to 19 and the DC of its Charisma saving throw decreases to 13. In addition, the save DC of Castigation increases to 19. Finally, Rictus now deals two extra weapon die of damage to Undead creatures. Address the Departed. While carrying this weapon, you can cast commune (contacting the spirits of the dead), death ward, and speak with dead. Once a spell has been cast from the weapon, it can’t be cast this way again until you finish a long rest.
