# Flesh Symbiont, Heart (very rare variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare Component: humanoid heart This symbiont is the abominable side product of a crazed necromancer’s arcane experimentation. Brimming with eyes and teeth, this beating lump of flesh vaguely resembles a humanoid heart. Revive. As an action, you can press this heart to the chest of a humanoid corpse within reach. The heart burrows into the corpse’s chest, consuming any existing heart. Over the course of 1 hour, it grows to resemble the original heart, casting the raise dead spell on the creature. Curse. Pressing the heart into a creature forcibly attunes the creature to the heart and prevents it from being removed by any means. If the creature is already attuned to the maximum number of items, it loses its attunement to one at random. While attuned to the heart, the creature is cursed and has disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks when it, the target of its attack, or whatever it’s trying to perceive is in direct sunlight. In addition, it gains resistance to necrotic damage and can use the Symbiotic Vigor property. If a remove curse spell or similar magic is cast on the heart, or a creature ends its attunement to it, it rots and is destroyed, killing its host. Symbiotic Vigor (Requires Attunement). The heart has 6 charges and regains 1d6 charges daily at dawn. These charges manifest as lidless eyes appearing on random locations across your body. While the heart is inside you, you can use your reaction when you take damage to expend 1 charge to cast the false life spell at 2nd level, gaining the temporary hit points immediately before you take the triggering damage. You can’t use this property of the heart while in direct sunlight. Whenever you expend a charge, one of the eyes closes. Very rare variant: The heart casts resurrection instead of raise dead, but does not restore the missing heart that it replaces. Increase the level at which false life is cast to 3rd.
