# Amethyst Deceiver (Very Rare)

**description**: Weapon (Dagger), Very Rare (Requires Attunement) Common This magic weapon shimmers with a subtle purple hue while it is within 60 feet of a poisonous substance. While within 30 feet of a poisonous substance, the purple shimmer becomes bolder. In addition, you have advantage on Medicine and Nature checks to identify poisons. Uncommon Pinpoint Poison. While holding this weapon, you can cast detect poison and disease (no concentration required) but only to detect poisons. Once used, this property can’t be used again until you finish a short rest. Antidotal Absorption. By touching the tip of the weapon to a poisonous substance no larger than 3 feet in any dimension, you can absorb it into the blade as an action. You can also absorb the poison from a poisoned creature. To do so you must use an action to deal 1 piercing damage to the target with the weapon (this damage can’t be reduced in any way). This removes the poisoned condition from the target. In either case, the weapon becomes imbued with poison. The dagger can only be imbued with one poison at a time. You can use this property three times, and regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. Venomous Vengeance. While imbued with poison, the weapon deals an extra 2d6 poison damage on its next hit. A creature who takes this poison damage must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned until the start of your next turn. Once this poison damage has been dealt, the weapon is no longer imbued with poison. Rare You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon. The weapon now remains imbued with poison for two successive hits, the poison damage dealt by Venomous Vengeance increases to 3d6, the DC for its Constitution saving throw increases to 15, and the duration of the poisoned condition increases to 1 minute. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. Very Rare The bonus to attack and damage rolls increases to +2, the weapon now remains imbued with poison for three successive attacks, the poison damage dealt by Venomous Vengeance increases to 4d6, and the DC for its Constitution saving throw increases to 17. In addition, a creature that fails three Constitution saving throws against its poison becomes paralyzed while poisoned.
