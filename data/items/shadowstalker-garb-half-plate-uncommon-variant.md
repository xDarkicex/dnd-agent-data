# Shadowstalker Garb Half Plate (uncommon variant)

**description**: Armour (light or medium), rare (requires attunement) Component: construct instructions This mechanical garb was tailor-made to allow assassins to glide over their target without being heard, before eliminating them with a swift, swooping attack. Anton Spark, the famous tinkerer, modelled this design after a snowy owl silently snatched a doughnut from his hand. Shadowglide. Whenever you fall from a height of 10 feet or more while wearing this armour, you can use a reaction to glide. While gliding, you move 1 foot horizontally for each 1 foot you fall, take no falling damage when you land, and have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks to move silently. Take-off. As a bonus action, you can activate your wings and gain a flying speed of 30 feet for 10 minutes. Once this property of the armour has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Death From Above. While wearing this armour, you can use a bonus action while in the air to fold yourself into a diving position and move straight down up to 120 feet. If you make a melee weapon attack against a creature immediately after moving in this way and hit, the attack deals an extra 1d6 damage for every 10 feet you moved, and you can immediately move another 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks. If the attack misses, you crash, taking 1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you moved and landing prone. Uncommon variant: Once the Shadowglide property has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Reduce the distance you can move straight down with the Death From Above property to 30 feet.
