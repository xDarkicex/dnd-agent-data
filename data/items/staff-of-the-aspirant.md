# Staff of the Aspirant

**description**: Staff of the Aspirant Staff, Uncommon (requires attunement by a sorcerer, warlock, or wizard) Given to apprentice spellcasters by their masters as a sign of trust and a test of arcane aptitude, a Staff of the Aspirant is often the first magic item a mage-in-training will obtain. This staff can be wielded as a magic quarterstaff that grants a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it. While holding it, you gain a +1 bonus to spell attack rolls. The staff has 5 charges and regains 1d4 + 1 expended charges daily at dawn. If you expend the last charge, roll a d20. On a 1, the staff retains its +1 bonus to attack and damage roll but loses all other properties. On a 20, the staff regains one charge. Spells. While holding this staff, you can use an action to expend 1 or more of its charges to cast one of the following spells from it, using your spell save DC and spell attack bonus: levitate (2 charges), magic missile (1 charge), or ray of enfeeblement (1 charge). Optional Rule: Different Staves for Different Schools At the gamemaster’s discretion, a Staff of the Aspirant may match the school of the wizard teacher who gives it to a student. To create such a staff variant, the gamemaster may replace one of the staff’s spells with a wizard spell of the same level from a specific school. For example, to create a Staff of the Aspirant Abjurer the gamemaster may replace magic missile with mage armor . To create a Staff of the Aspirant Necromancer, the gamemaster may replace levitate with blindness/deafness . When doing such replacements, keep in mind that some spells have material costs that could be bypassed if casted through an item, which can potentially make this item unintentionally powerful. Moreover, note that ray of enfeeblement is a second-level spell that uses only one charge when cast through this staff, and replacing it with other second-level spells may again make a variant of the item considerably more powerful than the standard Staff of the Aspirant. Optional Rule: Arcane Upgrades At the gamemaster’s discretion, player characters may find a way to upgrade a Staff of the Aspirant into a Staff of Promised Power, and to further upgrade a Staff of Promised Power into a Staff of Power. That way, a spellcaster who obtains their Staff of the Aspirant early can upgrade it twice through different quests from uncommon to rare to very rare as their adventure progresses.
