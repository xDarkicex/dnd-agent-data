# Veil of Illusory Armor

**description**: Veil of Illusory Armor Wondrous Item, Rare (requires attunement) This sheer and almost lightless veil has been imbued with strong illusion magic by crafty caravan halflings. While wearing this veil, you can use an action to cast the disguise self spell from it at will. The spell ends if the veil is removed. Illusory Armor. If you are wearing no armor and use the Veil of Illusory Armor to appear as if you are wearing nonmagical armor, the veil’s magic subtly alters your appearance so that you are harder to hit by those affected by its illusion. Any creature who targets you with an attack while you wear the illusory armor must first make an Intelligence saving throw against your spell save DC (or 8 + your proficiency bonus if you are not a spellcaster). On a failed save, the creature treats you as if you wore the armor projected by the illusion and you have the effective AC of the particular armor you are projecting for that creature. The creature makes the saving throw with disadvantage if you project the illusion of light armor and with advantage if you project the illusion of heavy armor. Enemies can repeat the saving throw each time they attack you. To discern that you are not really wearing armor, a creature can also use its action to inspect your appearance and must succeed on an Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC (or 8 + your proficiency bonus if you are not a spellcaster). A creature that discerns the illusory armor for what it is sees it as a vague image superimposed on you and becomes immune to this effect for the next 24 hours.
