# Ma'Aru Scale Mail

**description**: As the Phaenec travel the vast deserts of the Ember Wastelands, they gather a mineral called Stargold. It can be found anywhere in their native lands, but only a few grains at every single spot. The material needed for a single armor has been gathered over centuries, and only a few hundred of these items exist today. Stargold has properties similar to iron, but looks like shimmering, highly reflective gold. Receiving a Ma’Aru armor is considered a great honor, and the Phaenec almost never gift such an item to anyone but their own Threndavi warriors. When becoming such a warrior, the tribe member usually receives an armor that belonged to a retired or dead Threndavi. They learn from their mentor to repair and adapt a Ma’Aru in order to “make it their own”. A Threndavi Warrior wearing a Ma'Aru armor The Phaenec believe that the spirits of the ancestors who wore the Ma’Aru before help and protect the young warrior. By wearing a Ma’Aru armor, the tribe member not only becomes part of the Threndavi, but also part of the community of warriors that wore this specific armor. The word Ma’Aru literally translates to “spirit armor” or “spirit shell”. To the Phaenec, the word encompasses all the knowledge and spiritual belief that is associated with the items. A Ma’Aru armor can be of any armor type. They are usually not magical, although a few exceptions are known. The armor reflects bright light in a way that it can temporarily blind opponents. Phaenec Threndavi have mastered the skills to use this to their advantage. If anyone other than a Threndavi wears such armor while in bright light, every creature relying on eye sight and attacking the wearer has to make a Constitution Saving Throw against DC 10 on their first attack. On a failure, the attacker has disadvantage on attacks until the end of their next turn. After that, their eyes have adjusted to the reflective light. This effect can only be avoided by completely covering the armor Khazimi wiped the tears from her eyes. She had always wanted to become a protector of the tribe. To wear the golden armor. But she was too young. Now the world had ended, and her tribe had been destroyed. Khazimi wasn’t there to protect them. Her fingers caressed the shining metal of the Ma’Aru armor. It was the only thing still intact. Khazimi made a decision. She would wear the golden armor, and she would protect every single Phaenec that was still alive. From this moment on, she would be a Threndavi, and her entire kind would be her tribe.
