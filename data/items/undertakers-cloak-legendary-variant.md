# Undertaker's Cloak (legendary variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon Component: undead (mummy) rancid fat Mort I. Fiéd had learned one thing whilst under his father’s tutelage; in this world, corpses don’t always stay corpses. When the dead start rising, you want someone to have your back. Someone… or something. Grave’s Embrace. Trailing from your back, ragged scraps of cloth and bandage float in an ethereal wind, even on the calmest of days. As an action, you can speak the cloak’s first command word, causing shreds of it to pull away and begin entangling a Large or smaller creature you can see within 10 feet of you. The target must succeed on a DC 13 Strength saving throw or become restrained for 10 minutes. A creature restrained in this way takes 1d6 necrotic damage at the start of each of its turns as it desiccates. A creature can use its action to make a DC 13 Strength check, breaking the restraints and freeing itself or another creature within its reach on a success. As a bonus action, you can speak a second command word, releasing the target and ending the effects as the scraps of cloth fall lifelessly to the ground. Once this property has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn, at which point the cloak regrows. Rare variant: Increase the DC to 15 and the damage to 2d6. A restrained creature has vulnerability to fire damage. Very rare variant: Increase the DC to 16 and the damage to 3d6. A restrained creature has vulnerability to fire damage. Legendary variant: Increase the DC to 18 and the damage to 4d6. A restrained creature has vulnerability to fire damage. The Grave’s Embrace property can be used twice, regaining all expended uses daily at dawn.
