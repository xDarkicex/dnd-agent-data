# Armor of the Midnight Sun

**description**: Armor of the Midnight Sun Armor (medium or heavy, but not hide), Legendary (requires attunement) First worn by a legendary hero who destroyed a menacing shadow dragon in service of Nelchior, the warring god of night and dark secrets, this armor has been infused with the power of the Eternal Light, allowing its wearer to see the unseen and pierce the heart of the night with pure sunlight. While you wear this armor, you can use an action to cause it to shed bright light in a 45-foot radius and dim light for an additional 45 feet. Invisible creatures and objects are visible as long as they are in the armor’s bright light. You can use a bonus action to end this effect. This light is sunlight. Dawn’s Fury. When you are hit with an attack while wearing this armor, you can use your reaction to cause the armor to flash with blinding sunlight in a 30-foot radius. Any creature in this light excluding you must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 8d8 radiant damage and is blinded until the end of your next turn. On a successful save, it takes half as much damage and isn’t blinded by this effect. Undead creatures have disadvantage on this saving throw. Optional Rule: Celestial Blinders At the gamemaster’s discretion, player characters who obtain an Armor of the Defiant Dawn or an Armor of the Midnight Sun may also be able to obtain matching Celestial Blinders for their mounts. Celestial Blinders is an uncommon magic item magically paired to a specific Armor of the Defiant Dawn or Armor of the Midnight Sun and makes a mount that wears it immune to the Luminous Retribution and Dawn’s Fury effects of the armor they are paired with while the wearer of the armor rides the mount. Optional Rule: Upgrading Armor of the Defiant Dawn into Armor of the Midnight Sun At the gamemaster’s discretion, player characters may find a way to upgrade an Armor of the Defiant Dawn into an Armor of the Defiant Dawn. The process might require the aid of a legendary armorsmith or the acquisition of a rare meteoric metal such as asterite.
