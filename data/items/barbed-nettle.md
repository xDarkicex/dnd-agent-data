# Barbed Nettle

**description**: Weapon (simple, crossbow), rare This specialized wrist-mounted weapon uses a spring-loaded mechanism to launch barbed needles, and can be easily rigged to fire on impact from the wielder’s fist. These needles can be used to inject or apply poisons, and the barbs and the depth to which they sink into their victim’s flesh make them excruciatingly painful and impossible to safely remove. The barbed nettle is a simple ranged weapon with a range of 30/120 and the loading property. Its darts deal 1d4 piercing damage on a hit in addition to applying any poison applied to the dart. However, the darts remain stuck in the target’s flesh, and the creature takes a -1 penalty to attack rolls and saving throws as long as it has a dart in its flesh. As a bonus action, any creature may remove the dart. When removed, the target must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or take 2d4 piercing damage, or half as much on a successful save. This weapon is considered a simple weapon. Additionally, once per turn when the wielder hits with an unarmed strike, they can immediately choose to make a ranged attack with this weapon (no action required) against the target of the unarmed strike. This attack roll has advantage.
