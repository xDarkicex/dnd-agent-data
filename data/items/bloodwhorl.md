# Bloodwhorl

**description**: Weapon (Sickle), Rare (Requires Attunement) A wickedly curved blade of scarlet steel fixed to a handle carved from black bone. The blade feels wet to the touch. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. As an action, you can toss the weapon into the air and speak its command word, causing three identical phantom weapons to hover in a circle around you, spinning horizontally. As a bonus action, you can launch one phantom weapon at a target within 30 feet. Make a ranged weapon attack, using Bloodwhorl’s attack bonus. On a hit, the phantom weapon deals 2d4 necrotic damage, then vanishes. Once all three phantom weapons have been expended, the true weapon reappears in your hand. Once used, you can’t use this property again until you finish a short or long rest. Encounter Ideas Bloodletting. A vampire countess learned that bathing in blood was as effective as drinking it. She uses Bloodwhorl to open her prey. Harvest Moon. During the first full moon of autumn, Ragged Jack (revenant) emerges from the corn, wielding Bloodwhorl as he hunts the descendants of the villagers who murdered him. Tomb Guardian. The grave monument of a miserly wizard is secretly a stone golem in the shape of a cloaked skeleton. The monument holds Bloodwhorl in one hand and a longsword in the other.
