# Jade Skull of the Sun King

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement by a spellcaster) Component: pouch of celestial (couatl) feathers This jade skull wears the crown of a fallen Sun King. Legend says that each king had to prove themselves worthy of the blessing of a couatl so that they may craft their own crown from its gifted feathers. The stories also tell of the power stored in these precious artefacts, and of the hubris of thieves. Those for whom the crowns were not intended are subject to bestial curses should they try to don these royal raiments. This item has 3 charges and regains 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn. Vestigial Radiance. While holding the skull, you can use an action to cast the sacred flame and dancing lights cantrips, using your spell save DC. Blazing Eyes. As an action, you can brandish the skull in front of you, speak its command word, and expend 1 charge to cast the scorching ray spell (3rd-level), which uses your spell attack modifier and deals radiant damage instead of fire damage. Curse. Removing the crown from the jade skull curses the last creature that touched the crown until it’s once again placed upon the jade skull. While cursed, the skull’s magical properties are suppressed, and the crown appears on the head of the cursed creature and transforms it into an accursed feathered ape , multicoloured like a couatl, using the ape’s game statistics instead of its own. The ape is unwilling to part with the crown and is hostile towards any creature that it can see within 60 feet. When the curse ends, the creature reverts back to its original form and gains 2 levels of exhaustion. This curse can be removed from this item by the wish spell or a deal with a couatl.
