# Neb

**description**: Wondrous item, unique Neb is a chaotic neutral sentient magic item, a tiny silver parrot figurine of wondrous power that gained sentience when Master Arlen the Constructor bound a beholder’s soul inside it. It became a sentient magic item that refused to deliver messages or turn back into a figurine. Neb escaped Master Arlen and fled to the east until it came across the ship of Nimora and Ralin Olbareth, who captured Neb in a portable hole. The Olbareths’ ship, Starfire, was sunk by Captain Zevasha Indroza of the Dread Stingers, who had the ship looted and found Neb. Neb immediately gained control over the pirate leader and has since then been bossing the Dread Pirates around through the captain, keeping them in line with its eye beams. Neb has an Intelligence and Wisdom score of 16, and a Charisma score of 20. It communicates to anyone in possession of it telepathically, but can speak Common and Draconic. It has hearing and darkvision in a 120 ft. radius. It has a fly speed of 40 ft.. It is an object with an AC of 21 and 27 hit points, a damage threshold of 10, and immunity to poison and psychic damage. It can make a single attack with its silver beak. Silver Beak. Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 2 (1d4) piercing damage. Innate Spellcasting. Neb’s innate spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 13 ). It can innately cast the following spells as eye beams with a range of 120 ft. and a single target, requiring no components. 3/day each: blight , charm monster , disintegrate , fear , finger of death , flesh to stone , hold monster , sleep , slow , telekinesis . If anyone is in possession of Neb, it demands complete obedience. If its owner refuses to comply with its wishes, Neb can attempt to take control. Neb’s owner must make a DC 17 Charisma saving throw . On a failed save, the creature is charmed by Neb for 1d12 hours. While charmed, the creature must try to follow Neb’s command. If the creature takes damage, it can repeat the saving throw, ending the effect on a success. Whether the attempt to control someone succeeds or fails, Neb can only use this power three times per day
