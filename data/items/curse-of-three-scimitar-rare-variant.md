# Curse of Three Scimitar (rare variant)

**description**: Weapon (any sword), uncommon (requires attunement) Component: fey bone It’s often said that bad luck comes in threes. Legend states that, to create such a jagged, wicked blade, one must walk under three sets of stairs, cross paths with three black cats, and shatter three mirrors during the crafting process. And, of course, there’s the customary death of the craftsperson three days later. Forge at your own risk! This weapon has 3 charges, each represented by a red and black spectral skull that floats around the blade, and regains 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn. Three Strikes. When you hit a creature with an attack roll using this sword, you can expend 1 charge to attempt to curse the target with misfortune. It must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on the next three attack rolls it makes within 1 minute, after which the curse ends. Rare variant: Increase the DC to 15. While a creature is cursed by the Three Strikes property, it also takes 3d4 necrotic damage the next three times it makes an attack roll.
