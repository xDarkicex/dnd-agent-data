# Urchin Flail

**description**: Weapon (flail), uncommon (requires attunement) Component: beast stinger A sphere of dark purple bristling with needle-sharp spines is connected to a driftwood shaft by a rusted chain. Urchin Nature. This magic weapon deals piercing damage instead of bludgeoning damage. You can use it to make a ranged weapon attack (range 20/60) called a spikeshot, which uses your Strength modifier for attack and damage rolls and deals 1d6 piercing damage. Spikey Death Balls. As an action while holding this weapon, you can fire all the flail’s spikes at a point you can see within 60 feet of you, where they blossom into a field of squirming, darting, baby urchins. On land, you cover a square up to 15 feet on a side with these spike balls. Underwater, you fill a cube up to 15 feet on a side. When a creature enters this area for the first time on its turn or starts its turn there, it must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw. A creature moving through the area at half speed makes this saving throw with advantage. On a failed save, a creature takes 4d4 piercing damage and its speed is reduced by 10 feet until it takes an action to remove the urchins. On a successful save, it takes half as much damage and its speed isn’t reduced. Once this property of the weapon has been used, the weapon loses its Urchin Nature and Spikey Death Balls properties until the next dawn. Hydrodynamic. Attacking while underwater doesn’t impose disadvantage on attack rolls you make with this weapon.
