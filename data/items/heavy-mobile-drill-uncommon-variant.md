# Heavy Mobile Drill (uncommon variant)

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement) Component: elemental core of earth A wonder of mining technology, this drill can turn even the most meagre gnome into a walking excavator. While wearing this item, you can’t jump. However, you can use the drill arm to raise you up to 10 feet off of the ground (no action required). You can’t use the drill’s properties while raised. Weapon. While wearing this item, you can use it as a simple melee weapon that doesn’t require the use of your hands. You are proficient with this weapon, which deals 1d10 piercing damage on a hit. You have advantage on melee attack rolls made with the drill against creatures made of inorganic material such as stone, crystal, or metal, and it deals double damage to structures. Excavate. As an action, you can choose a portion of non magical soil, sand, or rock within 5 feet of you that fits within a 10-foot cube and excavate it, depositing it up to 10 feet away. Drill! As an action, you can make a melee attack with the drill against a creature within 10 feet of you. On a hit, you deal piercing damage equal to 3d10 + your Strength modifier. If the creature is wearing armour made of an inorganic substance such as stone, crystal, or metal, its armour takes a permanent and cumulative -1 penalty to its AC. The armour is destroyed if the penalty reduces its AC to 10. Power. The crystals that power this device are temperamental, often discharging when too much power is drawn. Whenever you use the Drill! property, roll a d4. On a 1, the crystals go dark and the drill becomes a dead weight; while wearing it, you can’t use it as a weapon, use any of its properties, and your walking speed is halved. The crystals must take 10 lightning or radiant damage in order to recharge and resume normal function. Uncommon variant: Remove the Drill! and Power properties.
