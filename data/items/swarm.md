# Swarm

**description**: Weapon, Rare (Requires Attunement) This ancient boomerang is fashioned from the bone of an unrecognizable creature. Indecipherable carvings and magical runes cover its surface, emitting a faint red glow. This boomerang is a simple ranged weapon. When thrown, it has a range of 60/120 feet, and any creature that is proficient with the javelin is also proficient with this magic weapon. In addition, when thrown, the weapon transforms into a swarm of buzzing, infuriated insects, and reverts to its normal form when caught. On a hit, the boomerang deals 1d4 bludgeoning damage and 1d4 poison damage. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. In addition, while holding it, you have resistance to poison damage. This weapon has 3 charges for the following properties, and regains all expended charges when you finish a long rest. Exotic Honey. You can use an action to expend 1 charge to create enough golden honey to feed one person. When consumed as an action, the honey satisfies any need for food and water for one day and heals a number of hit points equal to twice your proficiency bonus. The honey loses its magical properties if it is not consumed within 24 hours. Summon Swarm. While holding this weapon, you can use your action to expend 2 charges to summon a swarm of insects (wasps) in a space within 5 feet of you. The swarm disappears after 1 minute or when it drops to 0 hit points. The swarm is an ally to you and your companions. In combat, the swarm shares your initiative count, but it takes its turn immediately after yours. It obeys your verbal commands (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any, it takes the dodge action and uses its move to avoid danger. Encounter Ideas This Boomerang Is Juuust Right. An awakened brown bear holds Swarm as its most prized possession. If the weapon is stolen out of its den, the bear and its friends hunt for the culprits until they recover the boomerang. Exterminators. Jimmy, a gnome antique store owner, asks the characters to rid his attic of an insect infestation. He hears buzzing but refuses to investigate, for fear of getting stung. Swarm can be found amid the clutter. Please Clean Before Use. A marauding hill giant named Thick Tongue uses Swarm to pick detritus out from between her teeth after meals.
