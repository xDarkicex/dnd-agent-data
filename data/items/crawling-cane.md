# Crawling Cane

**description**: Weapon (Blowgun), Uncommon (Requires Attunement) This eccentric, iron walking cane is fashioned in the disturbing likeness of a centipede. However, the inside of the cane is hollowed out to be used as a blowgun. Out of the corner of your eye, the legs and mandibles of the centipede appear to move, only to be completely still when focused upon. Those who hold the cane can feel it gently squirm in their hand. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon, which deals an extra 1d6 poison damage on a hit. Crawl. As an action, you can throw the weapon down at your feet to transform it into a giant centipede as per the find familiar spell (however, the creature’s type is Aberration). This effect lasts for 1 minute, or until the creature is reduced to 0 hit points, or you use your action to end the effect, at which point it reverts to its cane form. Once used, you can’t use this property again until you finish a short or long rest. Encounter Ideas Squeamish Storekeeper. While perusing a merchant’s wares, the characters notice the grotesque cane. The storekeeper is quite motivated to sell it—almost too willing. While the party may be suspicious of her motives, she is simply afraid of insects. Granny’s Walking Stick. After the party dispatches a hag (or similar fey enemy), they find the cane in the creature’s treasure hoard. Disappointing Dinner. The cane is found in a large bird’s nest. Mama bird brought it back to feed the baby birds, but all they got was this lousy cane.
