# Clockwork Wyvern

**description**: Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement) Component: construct lifespark This limited edition clockwork wyvern comes with a fully operational, miniature coal engine. Thanks to an intricate mix of steam-powered gears and pistons, the construct has lifelike movements and mannerisms. Unlike its nonmagical cousins, this particular specimen gains a degree of self-awareness when a creature attunes to it. Combat. In combat, the clockwork wyvern shares your initiative count, but takes its turn immediately after yours. It can move and use its reaction on its own, but the only action it takes on its turn is the Dodge action, unless you take a bonus action on your turn to command it to take an action in its stat block or the Dash, Disengage, Help, Hide, or Search action. The wyvern has 6 charges; see the wyvern’s stat block for details on how it can expend and recharge this resource. Death and Damage. If the clockwork wyvern is reduced to 0 hit points, it loses all charges and becomes inert. A wyvern that has 0 hit points regains 1 hit point when it takes lightning damage from a spell of 1st level or higher, or if 2 gp of charcoal is placed in its chest-furnace. The clockwork wyvern regains 2d6 hit points each time the mending cantrip is cast on it. Except for these two means or its Reconstitute action, a wyvern can’t regain hit points.
