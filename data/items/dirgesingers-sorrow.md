# Dirgesinger's Sorrow

**description**: Weapon (rapier), rare (requires attunement) Once the weapon of a skilled dirgesinger who traveled the land sending spirits on to the afterlife, this ornate, purple-hilted rapier has a small reed flute built into the hilt that plays a haunting tune when blown. The weapon treats the Injury thresholds of Undead as 5 lower (to a minimum of 5), can be used to attack creatures on the Ethereal Plane, and can be used as a bardic spell focus. Additionally, you can use each of the following abilities once per day: Have the rapier cast gentle repose Have the rapier cast speak with dead Lay to Rest. As an action, you can blow the flute, creating a burst of energy that sends untethered souls on to the afterlife and lays animated corpses to rest. Each undead that can see or hear you within 30 feet of you must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. If the creature fails its saving throw, it is turned for 1 minute or until it takes any damage. A turned creature must spend its turns trying to move as far away from you as it can, and it can’t willingly move to a space within 30 feet of you. It also can’t take reactions. For its action, it can only use the Dash action or try to escape from an effect that prevents it from moving. If there’s nowhere to move, the creature can use the Dodge action. Additionally, when an undead fails its saving throw against this ability, it is destroyed if its CR is 1 or lower.
