# Binary Staff

**description**: Weapon (quarterstaff), legendary (requires attunement) An orb hovers at either end of this staff, one a brilliant white, the other a lightless black, time and space distorting around each. The staff can be wielded as a magic quarterstaff and grants a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with it. The staff has 8 charges, which are used and recovered when attacking with it. When making an attack with the staff, you may choose to strike with either the white sphere or the black sphere. Reaping Void. If attacking with the black sphere, the staff recovers 1 charge for every 10 damage dealt by the attack, siphoning energy and matter from the target to fuel the staff. Scattering Radiance. If attacking with the white sphere, you may expend charges from the staff, dealing an additional 1d8 force damage per charge expended. Master of Space. Once per day, you may cast the teleport or plane shift spell. When you do so, you ignore any limitations on the creatures that the spell can transport, instead transporting all creatures within 30 feet of you. I feel no small amount of concern about this order having a potentially unverse-ending relic in their possession. Is Arch-Magus Animus aware of this? Master of Time. Time bends to the will of the staff. Once per day, you may cast the time stop spell. Event Horizon. You may use an action to shatter the staff over your knee or a solid surface, causing the two spheres to combine with one another. The resulting release of energy destroys the staff, you, and the multiverse itself as the entirety of existence condenses down to a single point then explodes outwards, sparking the creation of a new reality. At one extreme, the heaviest weight in the multiverse. At the other, the lightest. Together, the binary spheres create a weapon where both weight and destructive power are in perfect balance. Upset that balance in the slightest, and the very fabric of the world would tremble.
