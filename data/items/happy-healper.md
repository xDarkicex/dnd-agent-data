# Happy Healper

**description**: Wondrous item, rare Component: celestial liver When the wards are packed, healpers are deployed. Each of these temporarily-sentient constructs act as an extra pair of hands for an overwhelmed sister of the Night Hospitaller, administering aid and stabilising those on the brink of death. Healping Hand. As an action while holding the healper, you can speak the command phrase “Healp! Healp!” followed by one of the commands below. The bundle of alchemical paraphernalia springs into life for 1 minute. It is a Tiny construct with AC 13, 20 hit points, a walking speed of 30 feet, a Strength score of 3, immunity to poison and psychic damage, immunity to all conditions except grappled and restrained, and it can’t attack. If it drops to 0 hit points, it loses its magic and is destroyed. It has 3 charges and regains 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn. The healper takes its turn immediately after yours. As part of the action to activate it, and as a bonus action on subsequent turns, you can give it one of the following instructions to use as its action on its next turn, which expends the listed number of charges: Stabilise (0 charges). The healper casts the spare the dying cantrip. Heal (1 charge). The healper pours a healing draught into the mouth of a willing creature within its reach, restoring 2d4 + 2 hit points to the creature. Restore (2 charges). The healper performs a minor surgery on a willing creature within its reach, ending one of the following conditions on that creature: blinded, deafened, paralysed, or poisoned.
