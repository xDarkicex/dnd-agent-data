# The Brothers (Legendary)

**description**: Weapon (Greataxe), Legendary (Requires Attunement) Common While attuned to this magic weapon, you can read, speak, and write Dwarvish. Uncommon The damage this weapon deals on a hit changes based on your alignment. Lawful. 1d6 acid damage and 1d6 slashing damage. Neutral. 1d4 acid damage, 1d4 lightning damage, and 1d4 slashing damage. Chaotic. 1d6 lightning damage and 1d6 slashing damage. Rare You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon. This bonus increases against certain creatures, based on your alignment. Lawful. +3 against chaotic creatures. Neutral. +3 against lawful and chaotic creatures. Chaotic. +3 against lawful creatures. Very Rare Once per turn, when you hit a creature with an attack using this weapon, you can choose to deal additional damage based on your alignment. Lawful. +1d6 acid damage. Neutral. +1d4 acid damage and 1d4 lightning damage. Chaotic. +1d6 lightning damage. Legendary As an action, you can temporarily release one or both brothers’s spirits from the axe, based on your alignment. Lawful. Gvorik. Neutral. Both. Chaotic. Gvern. Each brother uses the gladiator stat block with these changes: Their type is Undead (Dwarf). They have resistance to acid, cold, fire, lightning, thunder; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical attacks. They are immune to necrotic and poison damage. They are immune to the following conditions: charmed, exhaustion, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained, and unconscious. Gvorik’s attacks deal acid damage and Gvern’s attacks deal lightning damage instead of their usual types. Each spirit appears in an unoccupied space you can see within 10 feet of you, obeys your commands, and acts after you on your turn. A spirit disappears after 1 minute or when it is reduced to 0 hit points. Once used, this property can’t be used again until you defeat a creature of the alignment opposing yours (or of either extreme, if neutral) with a CR equal to or greater than your level. Final Progression After summoning one or both brothers’ spirits at least once, whichever you summoned teaches you a ritual. If your alignment is lawful or chaotic, the ritual destroys one brother and frees the other. If you are neutral aligned, the ritual frees both brothers. You can perform the ritual as part of a long rest. If you do, you can no longer summon the spirits, and instead the bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon increases to +3 and the bonus against creatures of certain alignments increases to +5.
