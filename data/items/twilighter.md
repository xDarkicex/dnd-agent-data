# Twilighter

**description**: Survival always comes at a cost; with this halberd, ensure that cost is taken from someone else. Legend The Twilighter is so named for that transitional period between night and day, or as others see it, between life and death. It was forged by a powerful lich as a gift for one of his warlocks. No one quite remembers the warlock’s name, let alone the lich’s. Though the particulars of its origin are lost to time, the Twilighter is well-known for tempting its wielders to toy with the forces of life and death for their own gain, often at the expense of others. Location Many seek the Twilighter for its ability to siphon energy from its victims for its wielder. The adventurers won’t find this item in a long-abandoned tomb or adorned on a wall. The Twilighter demands use. Encounter Ideas Champion’s Weapon. In the city there is a fighting tournament, and its champion owes his hundred-round winning streak to his trusty halberd that glows a malevolent purple. Hour of Need. The princess of a kingdom under attack believes this halberd of legend can somehow save her people. Lich-Sake. A lich keeps this halberd close, hoping to lure the perfect protégé to his cause by offering it as a gift. Progression The Twilighter progresses as the creature attuned to it casts more necromancy spells. Once the weapon progresses, it can’t do so again until the attuned creature’s proficiency bonus increases. Life for Death. When you cast a necromancy spell of 1st-level or higher, you gain a number of temporary hit points equal to the spell’s level. Uncommon . You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. Rare . The bonus to attack and damage rolls increases to +2, and the number of temporary hit points gained from Life for Death is now twice the spell’s level. Very Rare . The weapon deals an additional 1d6 necrotic damage on a hit. Legendary . The bonus to attack and damage rolls increases to +3, and the additional necrotic damage increases to 2d6.
