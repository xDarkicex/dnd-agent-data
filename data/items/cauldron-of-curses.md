# Cauldron of Curses

**description**: Wondrous Item, Rare Made of cast iron with an etched metal band around it, this cauldron is 1 foot in diameter, weighs 15 pounds when empty, and can hold up to 5 gallons of liquid. Cauldron's Magic. When the cauldron is filled with water and stirred for 10 minutes, you can cause one of the following effects: Scry . You cast the Scrying spell (save DC 15) with it. Stew. The water within the cauldron transforms into a hot stew that provides five rations worth of food. After 1 hour, the stew dissipates and any creature that ate a portion from it must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a success, the creature gains the effects of the Heroism spell for 8 hours. On a failure, the creature suffers 1d2 levels of Exhaustion. Warp. The water shimmers with energy. If you dip a branch into it, the branch transforms into a Wand of Enemy Detection , Magic Detection , Magic Missiles , Polymorph , or Wonder (chosen randomly by rolling a d6), and the liquid turns back into water. The wand has 1d3 charges, can't regain charges, and loses its magic if you use the cauldron to create a new wand. Whenever you use the cauldron to create an effect, roll a d4. If the number rolled is equal to or lower than the number of times you have used the cauldron since the last dawn, you become cursed until the next dawn and the cauldron's magic can't be used again until the next dawn. While cursed in this way, you have disadvantage on ability checks and saving throws using Wisdom, Intelligence, or Charisma.
