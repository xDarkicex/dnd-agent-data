# Juvenile Spawn Elixir

**description**: Potion, rare Component: aberration egg The pale, fleshy worm in this bottle moves sinuously through its sickly-green contents, turning to look at the nearest living creature with its unnerving, alien eyes. When you drink this potion, you become the host of a parasitic scleraworm. The parasite burrows through your flesh, dealing 3d8 necrotic damage to you. This damage can’t be reduced or prevented in any way. Upon reaching your brain, it erodes its way through your skull, appearing in the centre of your forehead. Mutually Beneficial? For as long as you host the parasite, you have resistance to psychic damage and can use a bonus action to speak telepathically to creatures you can see within 30 feet of you. You can cast the detect thoughts spell at will (save DC 15), taking 4d8 psychic damage when you do so. This damage can’t be reduced or prevented in any way. Proliferation. Whenever you finish a long rest, you must succeed on a DC 10 Intelligence saving throw or gain an additional eye on your head. You automatically fail if you roll a 1 on the d20. Once you have ten additional eyes, you become completely overwhelmed by the parasite and are no longer in control of your actions. Removal. Over the course of an hour, a creature can perform an operation to remove the parasite from a willing creature. The operating creature must make two DC 15 Wisdom (Medicine or surgeon’s tools) checks. If the creature succeeds on both checks, the scleraworm is removed and can be bottled. If only one is successful, there is no change. If the creature fails both checks, both patient and the scleraworm is killed.
