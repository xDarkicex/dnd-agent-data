# Maracas of Mauling

**description**: Maracas of Mauling Weapons (Two Maces), Very Rare (requires attunement) Valued by brawling bards and musically inclined warriors alike, these two magic maracas are also powerful maces. The Maracas of Mauling can be used as musical instruments and as maces. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with each of them. Maracas of Mauling come in pairs. If one of the maracas in a pair is destroyed, the other one becomes nonmagical. A creature can attune to both maracas or two creatures can attune to one maraca each. A creature attuning to both maracas uses a single attunement slot to do so. Dance of Doom. While wielding both Maracas of Mauling, you can use a bonus action to burst into the Dance of Doom, which is audible from as far away as 300 feet and lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are incapacitated or if you drop both maracas. You can also finish the Dance of Doom at any time (no action required). While you’re doing the Dance of Doom, you gain the following benefits: You have advantage on Charisma (Performance) checks to dance, sing, and play the maracas. Your walking speed increases by 10 feet. Melee attacks with the Maracas of Mauling deal an extra 1d8 thunder damage. When a creature hits you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction to perform a series of defensive dance moves. Until the start of your next turn, you have a +3 bonus to AC, including against the triggering attack. Once finished, the Dance of Doom can’t be danced again until the next dawn. Optional Rule: Extended Dance of Doom At the gamemaster’s discretion, a creature may continue doing the Dance of Doom until they become exhausted. After doing the Dance of Doom for 1 minute, a creature wishing to continue to dance must make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. On a success, the creature can continue to dance for 1 additional minute. On a failure, the creature stops dancing, gains 1 level of exhaustion, and falls prone. A creature that succeeds on the saving throw may choose to continue extending the dance. The DC of the Constitution saving throw increases by 5 each time the creature attempts to extend the dance by 1 more minute. Orelyn on Maracas of Mauling “Obviously, the creator of these curiously combined musical instruments cannot have been a serious person, but the damage these… ‘maracas’ can deal is serious indeed. I suppose they could be deadly in the hands of a warrior who is not afraid to combine their martial prowess with some dancing. How unusual!”
