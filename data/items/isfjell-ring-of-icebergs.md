# Isfjell, Ring of Icebergs

**description**: Ring, very rare (optional attunement) Component: elemental (ice mephit) core of water Crafted in the Frozen Forge, this ring is capable of entombing its wearer in a protective iceberg, or isfjell as it is known in the Giant tongue. Such rings are found in all sizes; a ring crafted for a giant might be worn as a torc by Loic Giantslayer, or as a belt by Mitji the Tiny. This malleability arises thanks to the wisps of water vapour that run between the ring’s jagged ends and its geodesic jewel. Isfjell. As a reaction to taking damage, or as a bonus action, you can spin the ring’s jewel, causing it to expand into a hollow block of clear ice that completely surrounds you. This breaks any grapples in which you are involved and pushes any other creatures in your space out of it. The iceberg occupies your space and creatures can’t pass through it, regardless of size. There is enough space within the iceberg to turn around, but you can’t move more than 6 inches in any direction. Spells and other magical effects can’t extend through the iceberg or be cast through it. While encased in ice, you can’t take any reactions, and the only action you can take is a bonus action to cease the jewel’s spinning, causing the effect to end. The iceberg has 100 hit points, which takes as much of any triggering damage as possible. The iceberg can’t regain hit points, has immunity to cold, poison, and psychic damage, and has vulnerability to fire damage. Creatures that hit the iceberg with a melee attack take 2d8 cold damage. The iceberg lasts up to 1 minute, until it is reduced to 0 hit points, or until you use a bonus action to cease the jewel’s spinning. When it ends, you can choose to have it explode, launching sharp shards of ice at all creatures within 15 feet of you. Each creature must make a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw, taking 2d8 cold damage and 2d8 piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. Once this property has been used, it can’t be used again until the ring spends 8 continuous hours in snow, ice, or cold water. Optional Attunement. If you attune to the ring, you gain resistance to cold damage while you wear it.
