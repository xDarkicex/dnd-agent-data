# Branch of the Worldtree (very rare variant)

**description**: Wand, rare (requires attunement by a druid or ranger) Component: plant bark Whether or not this branch truly came from the mythical Worldtree is unknown. However, it exudes potent, magical energy capable of manipulating plants and of great feats of healing. As it is used to channel a spell, the tips of its branches glow green-gold and the air is perfumed with the scent of mountain pines. This wand can be used as a spellcasting focus for your druid or ranger spells and has 7 charges, represented by its seven green-gold leaves. When you expend a charge, a leaf withers and drifts to the ground. Each day at dawn, new leaf buds unfurl and the wand regains 1d6 + 1 expended charges. If you expend the wand’s last charge, roll a d20. On a 1, the wand withers and petrifies, losing all magical properties. Regrowth. The wand can enhance your healing spells. When you cast a spell that restores hit points and deals no damage while holding the wand, you can expend a number of charges equal to the spell’s level to increase that level by 1. You can use this property only once per casting. King of Plants. The wand has power over other plants. While holding the wand you can cast the druidcraft cantrip at will. In addition, you can expend charges to cast the plant growth (2 charges) and tree stride (3 charges) spells. Very rare variant: Increase the charges to 9 and the recharge to 1d6 + 3. Add transport via plants (4 charges) to the list of spells that can be cast using the wand.
