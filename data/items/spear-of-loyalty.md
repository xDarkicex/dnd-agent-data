# Spear of Loyalty

**description**: Spear of Loyalty Melee weapon Price: 20,000 gp This one-handed spear appears to be made of a strange kind of wood that feels harder than normal. The tip of the spear looks like glass, though it doesn’t seem to shatter when it comes in contact with hard surfaces. The Spear of Loyalty acts as +1 spear (it gains an additional +1 bonus to Attack Rolls and Damage Rolls) and the owner of the spear can recall it to their hand at any time as a Free Action. When they do so, the spear instantly teleports from its previous location into the holder’s hand. There is no range for this effect, and it can even teleport from great distances or across other planes of existence to reach its owner’s hand. Only an anti-magic field can stop the spear from returning to an owner’s hand. A character who is capable of making multiple attacks each round may use this effect to throw the Spear of Loyalty multiple times in one turn. The Spear of Loyalty is nearly indestructible, and can only be damaged or destroyed by an equally powerful magical object. Melee. Melee Weapons are used to attack enemies up close in hand-to-hand combat. Light. Light weapons can be wielded with finesse in one hand, leaving the other hand free to carry other essentials, like a torch or a shield. Small and light melee weapons can use either Strength (Str) or Dexterity (Dex) for the Attack Bonus and Damage Bonus, whichever is higher. Ranged. Ranged weapons are used to attack enemies at a distance. These weapons can be either shot like a bow and arrow or crossbow, or thrown like a spear or javelin. All ranged weapons use Dexterity for both the Attack Bonus and Damage Bonus. Damage: 1d6
