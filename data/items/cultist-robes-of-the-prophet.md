# Cultist Robes of the Prophet

**description**: Wondrous item, uncommon (requires attunement by a spellcaster) Component: aberration hide Thick, purple cloth; occult, gold embroidery; and deep pockets for hiding sacrificial daggers; the cultist’s robes are everything a disenfranchised, young acolyte could hope for. As one rises through the cult’s ranks, one’s embroidery gets more intricate and the enchantments more powerful. Ethereal Haunt. As an action, choose a creature within 60 feet of you that can hear you. It must make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw as occult whispers wrack its psyche. The DC for this saving throw increases by 1 for each creature friendly to you also wearing cultist robes within 15 feet of you (maximum 5). On a failure, a creature takes 3d6 psychic damage, becomes frightened of you for 1 minute, and must immediately use its reaction to move a distance equal to its speed away from you, avoiding obviously dangerous ground. On a success, a creature takes half as much damage, isn’t frightened, and doesn’t move away. A creature frightened of you in this way can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. Once this property of the item has been used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn. Dark Devotion. If you spend 1 hour in prayer to the being in whose name these robes were made (which can be done as part of a short or long rest), you gain advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened until you finish a short or long rest. Rare variant, Cultist Robes of the Chosen: Increase the DC to 15 and the damage of Ethereal Haunt to 4d8. While under the effect of Dark Devotion, you can cast command (save DC 15). Once the robes have been used to cast a spell, they can’t be used to cast that spell again until the next dawn. Very rare variant, Cultist Robes of the Prophet: Increase the DC to 16 and the damage of Ethereal Haunt to 5d10. While under the effect of Dark Devotion, you can cast the following spells (save DC 16): command and dominate person . Once the robes have been used to cast a spell, they can’t be used to cast that spell again until the next dawn.
