# Coral Wand (rare variant)

**description**: Wand, uncommon (requires attunement by a spellcaster) Component: beast poison gland Such wands often take months to fashion, requiring the formation of an entire coral ecosystem. From molluscs to cnidarians and seaweeds, the inhabitants of such an item share one common theme—they all carry a deadly toxicity. While holding this wand, you can cast the poison spray cantrip. Toxic Discharge. When you cast poison spray using the wand, you can incite one of the organisms inside it to give the spell an extra boost. Roll a d4 and consult the table below to determine what additional effect the target suffers on a failed save: d4 Effect 1-2 Lingering Toxicity. The creature is poisoned until the end of its next turn. 3 Ocular Irritant. The creature is blinded until the end of its next turn. 4 Total Discharge. The cantrip deals an extra 2d12 poison damage. The wand wilts and the Toxic Discharge property can’t be used again until the next dawn. Rare variant: Increase the die size of the Toxic Discharge property to a d6. The poison spray cantrip is cast as a 5th-level caster. When you use the Toxic Discharge property, roll on the following table instead: d6 Effect 1-3 Lingering Toxicity. The creature is poisoned until the end of its next turn. 4 Ocular Irritant. The creature is blinded until the end of its next turn. 5 Neurotoxin. The creature is incapacitated until the end of its next turn. 6 Total Discharge. The cantrip deals an extra 3d12 poison damage. The wand wilts and the Toxic Discharge property can’t be used again until the next dawn.
