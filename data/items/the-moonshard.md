# The Moonshard

**description**: Wondrous item, artifact (requires attunement) The Moonshard is a hand-sized piece of gray, pumice-like rock that could easily be ignored by those searching for valuables. It draws attention to itself, however, by producing a moonbeam-like glow that makes it an obvious thing of power. This cylinder of energy creates an interplay of light and shadow, always bisected by the artifact. The Moonshard concerns itself with cosmic balance. War, death, slavery, decay, these things are but catalysts for prosperity, birth, harmony and burgeoning life. It is the balance of these concepts and the cycle of the cosmos that must be preserved, and thus the Moonshard acts in subtle—often undetected—ways to maintain this balance. Only those devoted to balance in one form or another are ever granted the ability to handle—and draw power from— the Moonshard . Those Rybalkans who embrace an amalgam life—that is, part Vikmordere and part Klavekian—have come to revere the “word of balance” that the Moonshard is said to bestow on the region. Protected. The aura that protects the Moonshard harms any who attempt to handle it without its consent. The Moonshard will suppress the aura and indeed casts a benign suggestion (save DC 20) on anyone it wishes to pick it up. A creature touching the Moonshard without its consent must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw, taking 49 (9d10) necrotic damage and 49 (9d10) radiant damage on a failed roll, or half as much on a successful one. Only those the Moonshard favors are allowed to attune to the artifact. Spells. The bearer gains access to the following spells: private sanctum (cast with all six possible protections), guardian of faith (summoning two guardians per casting, one angelic, one demonic), enhance ability (as a bonus action), and legend lore (always providing a balanced perspective). Skills. The bearer makes all Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma skill checks with advantage while holding the Moonshard . Abandonment. Should the attuned bearer of the Moonshard act in such a way as to disrupt balance on a grand enough scale for the artifact to notice, it will abandon its bearer, ending the attunement. The Moonshard has a knack for ending attunement at just the right time for another worthy user to come into its possession. Destruction. The Moonshard appears fragile but is impervious to most damage. It can only be destroyed by leaving it in the exact center of the Material, Astral, or Ethereal Plane. When deposited at one of these locations, the artifact ceases moving as it senses it is at the point of planar balance and its work is done.
