# Bag of Spiderlings

**description**: Bag of Spiderlings Wondrous Item, Very Rare This luxurious bag is actually an extradimensional spiderling paradise. Its interior is a divinely designed spider palace that can comfortably house a thousand baby spiders. The spiderlings live in luxury. The bag provides them with as much air, food, and drink as they need while keeping them young for as long as they stay in. When found, a Bag of Spiderlings will typically hold about a thousand baby spiders. Unlike lesser bags, the Bag of Spiderlings is indestructible. Pick a Spiderling. As an action, you can reach inside the bag and pick a spiderling. Alternatively, you can use your action to place a spiderling in the bag. Arachnid Flood. As an action, you can turn the bag inside out, releasing all thousand spiderlings in a 30-foot-radius sphere of baby spiders. This arachnid flood persists for one round as spiderlings tumble and fall, heavily obscuring the area. The ground in a 30-foot-radius circle becomes difficult terrain as spiderlings scurry about. The spiderlings also cling on every surface within the area, creating thick, sticky webs after 3d6 rounds. If there are at least two solid masses (such as walls or trees) within the original 30-foot-radius sphere, the webs cover the entire sphere. Otherwise, the webs cover a 30-foot-radius circle on the ground with a depth of 5 feet. The webs are difficult terrain and lightly obscure their area. Each creature that starts its turn in the webs or that enters them during its turn must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, the creature is restrained as long as it remains in the webs or until it breaks free. A creature restrained by the webs can use its action to make a DC 12 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check. If it succeeds, it is no longer restrained. The webs have AC 10, 15 hit points, and immunity to bludgeoning, piercing, and psychic damage. Optional Rule Adoptable Spider Eggs and the Bag of Spiderlings At the gamemaster’s discretion, Adoptable Spider Eggs can be placed inside the Bag of Spiderlings for protection. Regardless of size, each egg counts as a single baby spider when calculating the bag’s capacity. After an adoptable spider egg hatches inside the bag, the baby spider becomes one of the bag’s residents as normal.
