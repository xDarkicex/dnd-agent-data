# The Emperor's Bracelet

**description**: The Emperor's Bracelet This golden bracelet once belonged to the Carolingian emperor, Charles III. His nickname “Charles the Fat” was not actually appropriate because his magical bracelet prevented him from becoming too big. But it did not cure him from the addiction. The bracelet is approximately 6 centimeters (3 inches) wide and inscribed with the Latin words “Deus nobis concedit manducare” (God allows to eat) on the inside. It was created by one of Charles’ bishops using white magic. Anyone wearing this artifact will be able to eat as much as she or he wants, without ever gaining any weight. The unwanted side effect, however, is that the wearer will eventually starve – unless he or she is smart and only puts it on every other day or so.
