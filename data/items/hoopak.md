# Hoopak

**description**: Hoopak Melee weapon (martial, hoopak) A hoopak is a sturdy stick with a sling at one end and a pointed tip at the other. Ammunition. You can use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from the weapon. Each time you attack with the weapon, you expend one piece of ammunition. Drawing the ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack (you need a free hand to load a one-­‐‑handed weapon). At the end of the battle, you can recover half your expended ammunition by taking a minute to search the battlefield. If you use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a melee attack, you treat the weapon as an improvised weapon. A sling must be loaded to deal any damage when used in this way. Finesse. When making an attack with a finesse weapon, you use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls. Special. When you make a melee attack with this weapon, you ignore its ammunition property. You can use the hoopak as a martial ranged weapon. If you do, it uses the ammunition property, uses sling bullets, and deals 1d4 bludgeoning damage on a hit. Two-­Handed. This weapon requires two hands when you attack with it. Cost: 1 gp Damage: 1d6 piercing (melee) or 1d4 bludgeoning (ranged) Damage Type: Piercing Properties: Ammunition, Finesse, Special, Two-Handed Range: 40/160 Weight: 2
