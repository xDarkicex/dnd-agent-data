# Phantom Blade Scimitar (rare variant)

**description**: Weapon (any sword), uncommon (requires attunement) Component: undead (ghost) ethereal ichor Branton bodged this project up so badly he actually managed to kill the sword. “Swords aren’t even alive,” I hear you say. You’re right—they’re not. Branton Quark’s control of magic is so porous that he not only managed to grant an inanimate object life but, while it was revelling in its new-found consciousness, kill it almost immediately. The sword is now livid and haunts any who take the life of a sentient being. This blade is an almost permanent fixture in the FBI evidence lockers; no one is quite sure if sword murder is actually an offence. This magic weapon deals an extra 1d4 psychic damage to any target it hits. This damage is doubled against undead with the Incorporeal Movement trait. Sentience. A phantom blade is a sentient, lawful neutral item with an Intelligence of 14, a Wisdom of 12, and a Charisma of 12. It has hearing and darkvision out to a range of 120 feet. The weapon can speak, read, and understand Common. Personality. Phantom blades see themselves as righteous defenders of inanimate objects and take umbrage at being used to break down doors or crack open chests. In addition, their own experience with death makes them particularly sensitive to inflicting it upon others. Whenever you use the phantom blade to kill a creature that isn’t undead, the sword attempts to haunt you. You must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw or develop a short-term madness effect. Once the sword has attempted to haunt a creature, it can’t do so again until the next dawn. Rare variant: Increase the damage to 1d6 and the DC to 15. This damage is tripled against incorporeal undead instead of doubled.
