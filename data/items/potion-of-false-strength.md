# Potion of False Strength

**description**: Potion, rare This potion has a deep purple hue, which seems to darken eternally into an inky blackness without ever actually changing color. Drinking this potion grants one great strength at increasing cost. After drinking this potion, and at the start of each of your turns for 1 minute, your Strength score increases by 1 and you have advantage on Strength ability checks and saving throws until the end of your next turn. Whenever your strength score increases in this way, roll 1d6. D6 EFFECT 1-2 You have disadvantage on Intelligence saving throws until the end of your next turn, and your Intelligence score is reduced by 2 (to a minimum of 1) until the potion’s effects end. If this reduces your Intelligence score to below 6, you lose the ability to communicate, though you still mostly understand languages you know. 3-4 You have disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws until the end of your next turn, and your Wisdom score is reduced by 2 (to a minimum of 1) until the potion’s effects end. If this reduces your Wisdom score to below 6, you lose the ability to easily distinguish friend from foe, and must make a DC 10 Wisdom saving throw at the start of each of your turns (including when your Wisdom score is first reduced below 6). On a failed saving throw, you must include the nearest creature to you as the target of any attack you make or spell you cast until the start of your next turn. 5-6 You have disadvantage on Charisma saving throws until the end of your next turn, and your Charisma score is reduced by 2 (to a minimum of 1) until the potion’s effects end. If this reduces your Charisma score to below 6, you begin to lose your sense of self. You must make a DC 10 Charisma saving throw at the start of each of your turns (including when your Charisma score is reduced to below 6) or be unable to take actions until the end of your turn. Your ability scores return to normal when the minute is up.
