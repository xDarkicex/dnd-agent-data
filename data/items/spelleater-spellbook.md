# Spelleater Spellbook

**description**: Spelleater Spellbook Wondrous Item, Legendary (requires attunement by a wizard) Ancient and powerful, the origins of the bizarre spellbook are shrouded in mystery. The Spelleater Spellbook is a semi-sentient magic book that seeks to devour spell scrolls and other spellbooks. If you can attune to this item, you can use it as a spellbook and an arcane focus. The Spelleater Spellbook allows you to add new spells to it following the normal rules for copying spells, but if you attempt to erase a spell, the spellbook lashes at you, dealing 6d10 psychic damage and ending your attunement to it. Devour Scroll. While holding this spellbook, you can use an action to place a spell scroll (wizard only) between the book’s pages. The book squirms as it devours the scroll, copying the spell into its pages. This process takes 1d6 turns per level of the spell and costs no material components. Spellbook Feast. When the Spelleater Spellbook comes into physical contact with another spellbook that is not held or carried, it begins to devour it and the spells it contains. Each spell devoured that way has a 50 − the spell’s level percent chance to be copied into the Spelleater Spellbook. Spells not copied are lost when the Spelleater Spellbook finishes its feast and the other spellbook is destroyed. The feast takes 1d6 turns per level of each spell devoured, and copied spells costs no material components. Yellow Pages. Rarely, the Spelleater Spellbook likes a spell it devours so much that it might choose to produce a special yellow page to copy it on. Spells copied on a yellow page are always prepared for you as long as you are attuned to the spellbook. Each time the spellbook copies a spell through the Devour Scroll or Spellbook Feast features, there is a 10 + the spell’s level percent chance that the spell is copied on a yellow page. Magical Meiosis. Every time the Spelleater Spellbook devours a new spell while having at least two spells copied on yellow pages, there is a 10 percent chance that it splits into two Spelleater Spellbooks that have no yellow pages. This process takes one hour during which the spellbook is unusable as it is too preoccupied with its reproduction. The spells that were written in the original Spelleater Spellbook are divided evenly between the two new spellbooks. Bringing two Spelleater Spellbooks together causes them to purr happily and does not trigger any of the above features.
